WYCOMBE v. ORIEL.
A team from which Fred Langley, Bird, Baines, Mumford, Raffety, and the R.-v. E. D. Shaw were absentees did not look on paper strong enough to do battle successfully for Wycombe when they had to meet the Oriel club on Tuesday. However, with the assistance of C. Langley and G. Langley—the latter playing for his brother—and W. Stone, a Wycombe man, who, since his residence in the Midlands, has been tried in the Warwickshire Colts' team, they managed to win on the first innings by 47 runs, to which Keen contributed 33 and Stone 22, and four other batsmen also got into double figures, so that when the last wicket fell the total had reached 131. Unfortunately Page was unable to bowl, having twisted his knee in turning to make his last run; but Keen and G. Langley bowled with such effect that Reid and W. R. Percival were the only two among the visitors who made anything approaching a stand against their deliveries, and the whole side were disposed of for 54. The bowling of the Oriel men in Wycombe's second innings was somewhat loose, and got severely punished, as shown by the subjoined score and analysis:—

WYCOMBE Y.M.C.A. v. MR. A. GRAY'S XI.

WYCOMBE MARSH P.L.—A general meeting of the above was held on Tuesday, July 30, to consider if they should enter the Junior League, when after some discussion it was proposed and carried. It was decided to adjourn the meeting until August 13th, at the Red Lion, at 8 o'clock. All members are requested to attend, as the business will be election of officers, &c., for the next season.

CHALFONT PARK v. ST. SILAS (LONDON).—The annual match was played between the above, at Chalfont Park, on the 6th, and resulted in a win for the home team, who are to be congratulated, considering the number they had to contend with (21). Sidney Golding played excellent cricket, 21 not out in the first and 24 in the second innings. Orr bowled capitally, taking 16 wickets for 12 runs. The visitors' scoring was very low; Austin bowled fairly well for them. Chalfont made 58 in the first innings and 111 for six wickets in the second. The visitors' twenty-one were all got rid of for 32.

BURNHAM v. POSTAL TELEGRAPHS (LONDON).—This match was played at Burnham on Monday last, when the visitors won a game which, except for the weather, was a very pleasant one. Burnham batted first, but were all out for 47—Tilbery 11, Baxter 16 (not out), and Painter 10. James bowled splendidly, taking 6 for 20 runs, Mackie 3 for 14, and Milner 1 for 10. The Postal team fared better than their opponents, knocking up 65, W. Milner (33) and Bordeaux (25) doing best. Baldwin took 6 for 34 and Baxter 4 for 16. Going in again, Burnham scored 91 for the loss of 4 wickets and then declared; Baker (36 not out), Woodley (16), Baldwin (14), and Hall (12 not out) batted in fine form. Rain, however, stopped further play, and the Telegraph Staff thus won by 36 runs on the first innings. Mackie captured 5 wickets for 36 runs.

TAPLOW STATION v. BAYLSTON-SQUARE.—Played on the Station Ground on Saturday, the homesters winning easily. After disposing of the visitors for 33 (F. Smith 13), Taplow scored 59 for 6 wickets when the game was stopped by rain. H. Painter made 15, G. Jones 10 not out, and C.Frost 10. For the winners Fisher obtained 7 wickets for 20 runs, Frost 3 for 1, and J. Wright 1 for 10. For Bryanston Rowland (5 for 18 and Garrett (1 for 19) were the successful trundlers.

COLHAM GREEN v. GERRARDS CROSS.—This popular match was played on the charming ground at Erylyn. Showery weather somewhat interfered with the game and only one innings each was played. The visitors scored 69; Gerrards Cross put together 117 and won by 48 runs. Captain Fendon made 34 and G. Lewis 36 for the home team. Mrs. Worssley's hospitality to the players was much appreciated.

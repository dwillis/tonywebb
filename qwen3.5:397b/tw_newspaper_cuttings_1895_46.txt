STOCKPORT v. WERNETH.
Stockport met Werneth, on the ground of the latter, for the first time this season before a grand gate of over two thousand spectators, and in charming weather. Both teams were well represented. Werneth were minus the services of Wharmby, one of their pros, but had Brookes, of the Manchester staff and late of Stockport, assisting them, and who did very well too, taking five wickets for just over nine runs a piece. The Werneth captain being fortunate in winning the toss sent in Redfern and Haggas to face the bowling of Brown and Fred Torkington. Both men scored a boundary each off Torkington, but the score had only reached 22 when Haggas was grandly caught by Harry Rovis at square leg. 22—1—10. Hall, the popular Werneth pro, came next, but after scoring a single he was magnificently caught at slips by F. Tyler, 25—2—1. Heyworth followed in and here some good cricket was seen. Both men played well, though Brown troubled them with his trickiness with the ball. At 46 a change of bowling was tried, F. Tyler going on for F. Torkington. The separation, however, came from the other end, Sam Brown bowling Heyworth with a grand ball, after that player had reached 14 in nice style. 53—3—4. Wickets after this fell fast, Sam Brown being almost unplayable, the whole side being dismissed for the not over grand score of 98. This was due to the splendid bowling of Sam, whose analysis read—21 overs for 8 maidens 25 runs and 7 wickets. The fielding of the Stockport men was also very good, all the catches that were took being very fine. Stockport opened their venture by sending in W. Hartley and J. Bates to the bowling of Hall and Brookes. The start was at once disastrous, losing Bates and Arthur Torkington with only 15 runs on the board. Brown and W. Hartley soon after fell victims, and four good wickets were down for 27 runs, but when Harold Hartley and Disney got together a different complexion was put on the game, and they treated the bowling with the utmost confidence, especially the former, the fifth wicket falling at 55, Disney being bowled. Fred Long next joined Hartley, who was playing a splendid game, and he also found a good partner in Long, who also played very well. Hartley, after playing a splendid game, was most unfortunate in playing on. After making 27 he was loudly cheered on returning, the sixth wicket thus falling at 89. Little need for further comment except the batting of Fred Long and Frank Tyler, the former scoring 14 and the latter 12, not out; the innings closing for 110—Stockport winning by just a dozen. It was a grand match, and was fought out right to the end very determinedly. Great praise is due to Harold Hartley, Fred Long, and Frank Tyler for the splendid way they kept their wickets up against the fine bowling of the tricky Hall and Brookes; and Stockport have every reason to be proud of their handsome victory, every man being a thorough trier. STOCKPORT v. WERNETH (2nd ELEVEN).—These two old rivals met for the first time this season at Cale Green Park, in glorious weather, and before a fair attendance. The visitors, winning the toss, decided to bat a little after three o'clock, to the bowling of Jones and McLachlan. Jackson opened splendidly with a four, but on the whole runs came slowly, eight wickets going down for 93, when Marshall and Watson became partners and a splendid stand was made, the score being taken to 170 before either were separated, the score being still the same when the innings closed. After the usual interval Stockport commenced to bat a quarter past five, with a rather formidable task before them. May soon set to work in earnest. The first wicket compiled 47 towards the number. May, the retiring batsman, accounted for 43 of these. F. Selby, the new man in, seemed rather uncomfortable at first, but he was getting more at home when he lost the service of Royle, the score reading 73—2—19. Marshall joined Selby with almost a hundred runs wanting, and the hand of the clock pointing to six. They had much to do, but the batsmen got down to work, Marshall hitting freely in one over, excitement ran high as time crept on. The spectators became quite riveted to the game, especially the junior portion, who were enthusiastic with delight as each batsman sent the leather to the boundary. It was a treat to watch Marshall play each fresh bowler. Nothing seemed to come amiss, but another wicket fell before the required runs were reached, Selby being cleanly bowled by the captain for a very useful 46. With ten minutes left to get the remaining nine runs, Jones joined Marshall, the latter soon making the winning hit with a boundary. He was shortly afterwards bowled for a grand 67, comprising 13 fours and 4 twos. Time arrived when the fifth wicket fell, Stockport being victorious by 11 runs and five wickets. Werneth was very strongly represented in this, their first defeat of the season, but the homesters have to some yet. Next week Stockport 2nd journey to Cheetam Hill, and it is to be hoped the team will be as successful as they were on Saturday. Score:—

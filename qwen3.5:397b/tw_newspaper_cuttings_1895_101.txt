LUDLOW AND SOUTH SHROPSHIRE v ROYAL MONMOUTHSHIRE MILITIA ENGINEERS.
This two days' match was commenced at Ludlow on Monday, in fine weather. The visitors went to the wicket first, and Captain E. W. Smythe (72), Captain G. G. Morgan (27), and Viscount Southwell (27) played good cricket. The score reached 167, all out. On the home team going in F. L. Evelyn (24) and H. G. B. Phillips (47 not out) played well, and at the drawing of stumps for the day the score was 107 for three wickets. Play was resumed on Tuesday, when Ludlow were all out for 156-11 behind the Engineers. The latter, on going in again, scored 118. Ludlow thus required 130 to win. F. H. Sitwell, M. E. Stanton, and O. J. Clerke played well, and at the call of time the home team had lost five wickets for 122 runs, and so were thus within 8 runs of winning. Score:-

ROSS 2nd XI. v WESTON.-This return match was played at Weston on Saturday. The home captain won the toss, but elected to put the visitors in. This proved successful, for Wintle and Jeffreys both bowled in fine form, and dismissed the Ross team for a small total of 30 runs, the former taking 5 wickets for 9 runs, and the latter 2 wickets for 15 runs. When the home team went in they did very little better against Harrison's bowling, and when the ninth wicket fell there were still five runs wanted to win. Here the turning point came, for before Wintle was caught for a well played 21, the two batsmen raised the score from 26 to 67. Score:-

EASTERN DAILY PRESS: WEDNESDAY 5 JUNE 1895 - 2.

DISS v. KING'S DRAGOON GUARDS.
Played at Diss on Monday, and resulted in a win for Diss by 80 runs. Charming cricket weather prevailed, and numerous spectators watched the game with evident enthusiasm. Diss won the toss, and electing to bat first, ran up a total of 182, to which Tipple, Candler, Ireland, Nicholls and Dowson were the chief contributors. The Dragoons then batted but made rather a poor show against the bowling of Ireland and Notidge, the former taking six wickets. Harper scored freely towards the finish. Lieutenant Searight bowled well for the losers. Scores :—

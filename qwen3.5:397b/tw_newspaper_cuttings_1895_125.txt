ATHLETIC NOTES.
Matches for to-day are as follows:—West Herts v. Bushey, at Bushey; Watford Brewery v. The Roamers, on the Brewery ground; Cannon Brewery v. Harefield, at Harefield; Rickmansworth v. Pinner, at Pinner; Melrose v. Camden, on the Recreation Ground.

On Monday and Tuesday, the 12th and 13th, Gentlemen of Hertfordshire v. St. Albans, at St. Albans.

On Wednesday and Thursday, 14th and 15th, Hertfordshire v. Bedfordshire, at St. Albans.

On Friday and Saturday, 16th and 17th, Hertfordshire v. Norfolk, at Norwich.

To-day the Watford Cycling Club run to Missendon at 3 o'clock.

To-day the Watford Wheelers run to Hertford, and on Wednesday evening they go to Radlett.

The success of the great amateur athletic meeting on the West Herts Ground on Bank Holiday was marred by the weather. A strong wind and driving rain set in about 4 o'clock and damped the ardour of spectators and competitors alike.

With a more suitable day the sports would doubtless have proved an immense success. The arrangements were admirably carried out, thanks to the energetic Secretary, Mr. C. R. Humbert, and a zealous committee. Six thousand spectators is a respectable number, but one which deserved to be doubled, considering the quality of the sport that was provided. The only complaint heard was against the weather, the course being in splendid trim previous to the heavy rainfall.

Under ordinary circumstances the utmost enthusiasm would have been aroused to see Bacon, Watkins, Wills, Blair, and Thomas competing in a three miles invitation race. Hopes had been entertained of the grass record being beaten, but wind and weather was against such feats, and one by one the champions fell out of the running.

Blair, the Yorkshire champion, ran pluckily and won the race in 14min. 47sec., and Watkins came in second having covered the distance in 14min. 49 3-5sec. The time for the first mile was 4min. 47 3-5sec., the second mile was finished in 9min. 44sec., the third in 14min. 47sec. Blair was loudly cheered on reaching the winning post.

The County Week finished very disappointingly on Saturday, for the rain robbed West Herts of what appeared to be an almost certain victory. Berkshire having made 93, the home batsmen ran up a score of 200, and in Berkshire's second innings six wickets fell for 83. Herts were thus in a majority of 22 and six wickets. Playing for the visiting county was W. Howard Dixon, who has been seen in both the West Herts cricket and football teams.

Pank had the wonderful analysis of eight wickets for nine runs, and his splendid bowling was largely responsible for the favourable position of the home county. White throughout captured four wickets for 88.

Throughout the County Week the same generosity was extended to players, members, and lady visitors to the West Herts Ground as had been shown by the Ladies Tea Committee in the Club Week, and we noticed that Mrs. Martin and Mrs. A. Sedgwick were included amongst the subscribers.

The matches on Tuesday and Saturday finishing so early owing to the rain, the supply of cakes, &c., was not wanted, but the maxim "waste not, want not" was acted up to, and the inmates of the Workhouse received the treat.

The match at Bedford, on Monday and Tuesday, between their home county and our home county ended in a very even draw. L. C. W. Thring and J. Manton compiled 49 and 41 respectively, but on the Herts side the highest score was H. Figg's 31.

Some good cricket has been witnessed at Bushey during the week. Unfortunately a deluge of rain cut short the match with the Vampires on Bank Holiday, but it will be seen by the scores that the draw looked very much in favour of Bushey.

On Tuesday the team fell before an eleven from the Emerald Isle, who scored 147 against the locals 61. Had it not been for the good stand made by Green and Harford the contest would have been disastrous to Bushey.

An interesting and well contested match was played on Wednesday, when the team met H. J. H. Bladon's XI. Both sides passed the century, and the visitors passed their opponents' score by 16 runs. Keeling, Green, Harford, and Sargent batted well for Bushey.

The weather, which opened inauspiciously at the beginning of the week, improved considerably later on, and the matches were witnessed by a fashionable company.
The promenade concert at the Manor House on Wednesday was largely attended.

The Abbots Langley C.C. have lost no time since the first heavy shower last month to improve the condition of their pitch, and we are glad to hear that rain and roll have removed any grounds of complaint the dry weather created earlier in the season.
The team is meeting with fair success too, and they have some promising young bowlers.

On Monday Croxley entertained the Copthall C.C., a team from Shoreditch way. The match was not brought to a definite conclusion, however, as the rain towards the finish made play almost impossible, and the game was accordingly abandoned in an interesting condition, as Croxley with two wickets to fall required 14 runs to win. The scores were as follows:—Copthall 1st innings, 49; 2nd innings 67; total, 106. Croxley, 1st innings, 67; 2nd innings, for 8 wickets, 39; total, 96.

RAGLAN BARRACKS v. NORTH DEVON.
The match was played at Mount Wise yesterday afternoon. The Raglan Barracks team was composed of members of the Somerset Light Infantry and Berkshire Regiment. The Barracks went in first on a comparatively good wicket, and knocked up 107. In reply the visitors knocked up 113 for six wickets, thus securing a substantial win. Score:—

SIDMOUTH v. INCOGNITI.
This match commenced at Sidmouth on Wednesday and concluded yesterday, the innings winning by an innings and 3 runs. Rain interfered with the match the first day, and when play were drawn the visitors had made 155 for the loss of seven wickets. Yesterday the wicket was altogether in favour of the bowlers. The innings of the Incogniti was soon finished, and Sidmouth were disposed of the first time for 61 runs before lunch. Going in in the afternoon they only made 73. Himeon and Fox bowled splendidly, the former taking twelve wickets during the match for 73 runs, and the latter eight for 51. Score:—

TAVISTOCK v. PLYMOUTH.
Played at Tavistock on Tuesday. Plymouth began well—with Carey and Collins putting on 51 for the first wicket, but against the bowling of Wood and Milford the others only added 29, and the innings closed for 115. Tavistock started badly, losing Pomeroy without scoring. Moore (pro), although he had a little bit of luck, played a sound game, further proving him to be one of Devon's best bats at present. The innings realised 136, the home team winning a good game by 20 runs. Score:—

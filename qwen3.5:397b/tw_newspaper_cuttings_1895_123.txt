HERTS ADVERTISER : SATURDAY 10 AUGUST 1895-5.

COMET v. REDBOURN.
On Bank Holiday the Comet C.C. paid their annual visit to Redbourn, and were again successful in defeating the home team, they being the only Club that lowered the Redbourn colours last season. Unfortunately the weather was anything but desirable, and the rain greatly interfered with the game. Owing to a breakdown on the railroad the visitors were unable to reach Redbourn till one o'clock, to do which they were compelled to walk from Harpenden. It was agreed to play twelve aside, and the Comet's captain winning the toss put Redbourn in first, which proved disastrous to the home team, the whole of whom were disposed of for 48 runs. A. A. Coles and H. Pratt being the only ones to reach double figures, the former making 15, and the latter 11 not out. The bowling of Lambert for the Comet was very fine, he being credited with the capital analysis of 10-2 overs, 12 maiden overs, 14 runs, 8 wickets. Watching their opportunity between the heavy showers of rain, the visitors commenced their innings. Two wickets down for five runs did not look very promising for them, three for 21 was better, and when Greaves joined Baxter they ran out easy winners with eight wickets in hand, Baxter being answerable for 22 and Greaves 16, T. Sansom having secured the three wickets at the cost of 24 runs. The rain being so disrespectful further play was abandoned. The following were the scores :—

HEMEL HEMPSTEAD v. KILBURN.
Rain seriously interfered with this Bank Holiday match at Boxmoor. On a wicket which gave the bowlers great assistance, Kilburn were dismissed for 41, Mitchell and Kenny alone making anything like a decent stand. Wardle took five wickets for 18 runs, and Turner (who did the hat trick) five for twenty-five. Richards kept wicket remarkably well; no extras were scored, and the stroke which got rid of Pocknee was unusually smart, as the batsman only lifted his heel, and Richards whipped off his bails. Hemel Hempstead went in, and lost four wickets for 12 runs. Rain then came on and it was anything but easy to bowl and field with a wet ball. The visitors, however, stuck to their work in spite of the rain, and at twenty minutes to five the High Bailiff made the winning hit amidst applause. The brothers Woodman continued to add to the score sheet, and when the game was finally abandoned they were still not out with 30 and 27 respectively. Score—

LONDON CITY POLICE v. REDBOURN.
On Thursday week Redbourn met for the first time the City Police against whom they secured a creditable victory. Winning the toss the homers elected to bat, and were all dismissed by the capital bowling of the Police for the small total of 79 runs, T. Sansom having contributed the lion's share—a freely hit 46, Pratt being not out for 10. The City Police, however, on going to bat could not stand against the fine bowling of T. Sansom and Pratt, and were disposed of for 26. T. Sansom took 4 wickets for 10, and Pratt for 13 runs. On Redbourn's second venture H. Arnold and Pratt went to the wickets first, but the latter was soon bowled by Padgham for three. On T. Sansom joining Arnold, however, things soon began to get lively, and the score was carried to 110 for three wickets, when the innings was closed. The policemen's re-start was an improvement on their first innings, 30 being up for the first wicket, but the next two wickets fell without a run being added, and when time was called five wickets were down for 85 runs, Hunt and Goodenough being the most successful with 17 and 19 respectively.

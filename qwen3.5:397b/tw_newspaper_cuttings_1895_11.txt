TOWN SECOND ELEVEN v. CADDINGTON.
Played at Dunstable on Thursday, and ended in a win for the home team by 24 runs on the first innings. The batting of the visitors was exceedingly feeble; the only one who seemed able to play the bowling was Sams, who carried his bat for 11; he also took five of the home wickets. The runs for the winners were mainly from the bats of three men—Anderson 15, Love 13, and Marchant 10. In bowling Smith took seven for 16 runs, Gray two for 18. Score :--

TOWN SECOND ELEVEN v. CARTER'S C.C.
The town second eleven added one more to their long list of victories, on Saturday last, on the town ground, by defeating Carter's C.C. by 45 runs after a good game. The home batting was fairly good, the principal scores being: Love 21, Gray 21, Tompkins 14, Poulton 10, and Wright 18 (not out). On the visitors' side Hanker played well for 21, as also did Mooring and Timm for 11 and 10 respectively. In bowling Giddings for Carter's took six of the town wickets at a small cost, while the wickets of the visitors were equally divided among four of the home bowlers. Score,

DUNSTABLE VOLUNTEERS v. LUTON DETACHMENT.
This match was played at Luton on Saturday, and resulted in an easy win for Dunstable. Luton batted first, and were all disposed of for 19 runs, owing to the very successful bowling of Corporal Smith. Dunstable scored 71, thus winning by 52 runs. The best scores for the winners were made by Corporal Smith and Private Bunker (21 each). The following comprised the Dunstable team:— Colour Sergt. Elliott, Sergt. R. Hambling, Sergt. W. D. Hambling, Corporal Smith, Private Bunker, Private Sharratt, Private Bonham, Private Burch, Private Darby.

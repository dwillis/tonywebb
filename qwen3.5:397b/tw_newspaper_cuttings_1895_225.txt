G.W.R. v. BATH ASSOCIATION.
The G.W.R. team placed another victory to their credit in above match at Bath, on Saturday last, although they were minus the services of Pearman, Beachcroft and Major. The home captain won the toss and decided to bat first, but it is questionable whether this was of any advantage, as the wicket was somewhat soft, and Down and Overton again bowled with great effect, the former having the remarkably good result of eight wickets for ten runs. Bath were all dismissed for 39 runs in about forty-five minutes, only Twining reaching double figures. The first four wickets of the visitors fell for 37 runs, but H. Hartley and C. Smith increased the score to 56 before the latter was bowled. H. Hartley played another good innings and was top scorer, Beanland and A. Hartley rendering useful assistance, the score eventually reaching 102, or 63 to the good. Bath did much better in their second attempt, Twining again batting well, his 56 being the result of some fine hitting, and included four drives out of the ground. It is only fair to state, however, that the G.W.R. were without the bowling of Down in the second innings, that player having to leave early. Kirby bowled well, taking three wickets for two runs. With a few minutes more to spare, the visitors would probably have won the match outright by eight wickets, as only seven runs were required to win at the call of time. Scores :—

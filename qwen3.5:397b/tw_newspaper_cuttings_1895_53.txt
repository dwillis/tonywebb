CHESHIRE COUNTY NEWS: FRIDAY 23 AUGUST 1895 - 2.

CRICKET.
SATURDAY'S MATCHES.
Stockport v. Werneth.
STOCKPORT WIN BRILLIANTLY.
This match between these old rivals was played at Gale Green Park in magnificent weather, and before a large crowd of spectators, it being also set apart for the benefit of the professionals, Brown and Disley, who throughout the season have done splendid work, and it is hoped that they will receive a substantial reward. It will be remembered that the first time these teams met at Werneth this season Stockport won a most interesting match by eleven runs, due on the whole to the splendid bowling of the evergreen Sam Brown. On Saturday skipper Hartley won the toss, and of course chose to bat, on a splendid wicket, taking in with him J. Bates to face the bowling of Hall and Hodgkinson. The start was none too good from a Stockport point of view, as Bates in trying to get a full toss from Hodgkinson to leg was elegantly caught at slip. Arthur Turkington joined his captain who cut Hall splendidly for four, but after scoring ten he was caught at the wicket. Sam Brown came next and was warmly greeted by the crowd. He immediately got to work, scoring runs faster than Turkington. The score had reached 37 when Turkington was caught by Redfern, after that player had scored six. Disley joined his brother professional but only to see him brilliantly caught at short slip by Collinson, after playing a splendid game. H. Bromley-Davenport of Cheshire county and Cambridge University fame came next and here a splendid stand took place, both men defying all the efforts of the Werneth bowlers. Davenport delighted the spectators with his batting, while Disley, on the other hand, was playing a most finished game, his cutting being a treat. At 72 Davenport was run out in stepping forward to face a ball from Besnor, which hit the wicket keeper's pads and came back into the wicket before Davenport could recover his ground, he had hit up 17 in a very few minutes and he was loudly cheered on retiring. F. Tyler joined Disley, but shortly after the latter was caught at mid off for a splendidly played innings of 31. The telegraph board now showed 87—51. F. Selby came next, but little needs further comment with the exception of Frank Tyler's display, which was a really meritorious performance. His innings of 44 not out included six four's two three's, and three two's. He was ably assisted by J. Besnor, who played a nice innings of eleven before being caught on the boundary. The innings closed for the respectable score of 139. After the usual interval Werneth opened their venture by sending in Wharmby and Eaton, the attack being entrusted to Sam Brown and Bromley-Davenport. Brown opened with a maiden over, but Eaton got the amateur's first ball away for three, but soon after lost Wharmby, who was nicely caught by Selby at cover point, the first wicket falling at nine. Bagster took Wharmby's place but with addition of six runs lost Eaton, that player being defeated by a grand ball from Brown. Redfern was next in and next out, Selby taking him beautifully at point off Davenport, and three wickets were down for 15 runs. Matters never altered much for with the exception of Besnor, Hall, and Besnor, no one seemed able to play the bowling, the innings closing for 70. Stockport thus being credited with a win over Werneth for the second time this season. I must just make a note of the splendid bowling of Sam Brown and Harry May, the former taking four for 18, and the latter four for 15 and this along with the splendid fielding of the Stockport men, who never missed a chance, was due in no small measure to their brilliant victory. The spectators seemed to fairly enjoy the pleasant sport, and especially so to see their own men come out on the right side.

MANCHESTER v. CHESHIRE ROLLING.—Played on the ground of the latter. The visitors took a moderate team with them, but proved strong enough to vanquish the home lot. The game at times was very exciting, Manchester having seven wickets down and wanting 30 runs to win, but Mr. Stills (who played extremely well on being joined by Bennett) knocked off the runs, the partners club thus winning by 31 runs and one wicket to fall.

EASTERN DAILY PRESS: MONDAY 2 SEPTEMBER 1895-3.

NARROW SECOND ELEVEN v. NEW CITY.—Played at Lakenham, on Saturday last, and resulted in a win for Narrow by 46 runs after a good game. Only Cooke and Dickerson could successfully cope with the bowling of Moore and Jermyn. Narrow Second Eleven—Palgrave 0, Moore 19, Parry 3, Murray 2, Jermyn 10, Keyner 21, Cowles 1, Spinks 5, Betts 3, Wales (not out) 14, Goreham 10, extras 6: total, 84. SILVER ROAD v. ST. PAUL'S.—Played at Mousehold on Saturday, and resulted in a win for Silver Road by eight wickets and 3 runs. For the winners King was the best bowler, taking twelve wickets for 21 runs, and also the best bat, scoring 11 and 11 not out. Lubbock also batted finely for 15 not out in the second innings. ST. PHILIP'S v. ST. PHILIP'S INSTITUTE.—Played on Dis's Land on August 31st, and resulted in the defeat of the Institute by 33 runs. The Institute could make no stand against the bowling of Scott (seven wickets for 2 runs), Prior (five for 6), and Clapham (three for 6), and compiled 7 and 22. St. Philip's replied with 29 and 38. For the Institute P. A. Hunt and Metcalf were the most successful bowlers. St. Philip's—Scott 6-2, Mihill 6-8, Prior 5-7, Clapham 0-10, Stringer 4-3, Allward 2-0, Saunders 4-0, Nicholson 1- not out, Bacon 0-0, Read 0-0, extras 1-7; total, 29-38.
THORPE VILLAGE v. COLTISHALL. — Played at Coltishall on Thursday, and resulted in a win for Thorpe Village by 11 runs. Coltishall batted first, and were all disposed of for 88. Thorpe, on going in to bat, fared badly at first, but eventually made 99. George Wright batted and bowled well for Thorpe Village. Mr. Extras scored heavily on both sides. CATFIELD v. OULTON AND BLICKLING PARK.—A most enjoyable game was played between these clubs on Thursday at Catfield, the Park winning by 40 runs. The visitors were entertained by the Catfield Cricket Club Committee, and, after an excellent lunch, Mr E Le Neve, on behalf of the Oulton and Blickling Club, tendered their best thanks for the capital way they had been received and entertained. Dr Gordon, on behalf of the Catfield Club, suitably responded. LADIES v. GENTLEMEN.—Played at Heydon Park on August 28th, and resulted in a victory for the Gentlemen by 54 runs. This enjoyable match was played in superb weather before a large number of spectators. The Gentlemen, who were handicapped in the usual style, declared their second innings closed with three wickets down, and were kindly allowed by their opponents' captain, Mrs. Sullivan, to play beyond time in order to finish the second innings of the Ladies.

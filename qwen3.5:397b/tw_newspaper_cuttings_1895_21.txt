PARISH CHURCH INSTITUTE v HIGHCLERE. — The return match between these teams was played, by kind permission, on the cricket ground on Saturday in splendid weather. Highclere winning the toss decided to bat first, but owing to the bowling of Gunning and Gayler, the first four wickets fell for 21, when Collins came in and made a useful stand, necessitating several changes in the bowling. Still runs came, Collins doing the lion's share, but Gunning going on at the top end fairly best him with a beauty, whilst the Thirkills made some useful stands. After the interval the Institute started batting with Cook and Pyke, the former hitting his wicket, whilst Pyke contented himself by keeping up his wicket. Mashin the next man, made things more lively putting on 18 in quick time. The others making double figures were Pyke, Young and Gunning. Both teams were entertained to tea, through the kindness of Miss L. Godding and Mr H Godding. Score :-

NEWBURY v. 43RD REGIMENTAL DISTRICT. — The return match between these two elevens was played on Wednesday, on the ground of the former, in perfect cricket weather. The soldiers, who had got together a strong team, were the first to bat, Sergt Bromley and Lieut Croome facing the deliveries of H. D. Wells and W. T. Hill. The score increased steadily, and it was not until 154 runs had been made that the first two batsmen were separated. The wicket was good, and the bowling was not too fast, and the whole side were all out before lunch for 142. The South, who showed fine form, was not out 62, his best hits being four fours, five threes, and ten twos, the only other batsman to make a stand were Bromley (23), and Croome (31). The home team opened their innings shortly after three o'clock, E. F. Hill and Cave opposing the bowling of Bedding and Croome, when runs came quickly. It was not until the telescope board showed 74 that Middleton secured the professional's wicket. The retiring batsman had made 50 by some good cricket. H. D. Wells went in and Hill was directly afterwards bowled by Bromley, Cave for 76. Ingram joined Wells and the two completely mastered the bowling, the result being that the visitors' score was passed just before five o'clock. Wells still continued to hit freely, and though several changes of bowling were tried they failed to bring about the desired effect. Stumps had to be drawn at 6.15 owing to the Reading men leaving to leave by the 5.34 train, the score standing at 103 for two wickets. The two not-outs Wells (66) and Ingram (45) gave a fine display of batting. They were at the wickets just over an hour, during which time they put on 118 runs. The match was watched with interest by a fairly large number of spectators. Score :-

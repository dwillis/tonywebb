WATFORD OBSERVER: SATURDAY AUGUST 1895-3.

WEST HERTS v. EARL OF ESSEX'S XI.
This season the Earl of Essex again raised a team to meet the West Herts Club, the match coming off on Saturday. There was a large attendance, which might have been still larger had it not been that there was a strong wind blowing, but on that score no complaint could be made as the showery weather of the morning had very fortunately passed away. Such inclemencies, however, had little or no effect upon White's pitch. During the afternoon Lady Essex with her daughter, Lady Iris Capel, drove on to the ground, and others present throughout the day were Lord Hyde and Lady Edith Villiers, the Hon. A. H. Holland-Hibbert and party, Mrs. Schreber, Mr. C. R. Humbert and party, Mr. H. Morton Turner, Dr. A. T. Brett, Captain Wymer, Mr. Green, Dr. F. H. Berry, and Mr. A. P. Blathwayt. Lord Essex having won the toss sent W.-I. Herts out to field, the Hon. F. Egerton and the Hon. R. Ward facing the bowling of J. Woods and R. R. Barker. A poor start was made and the first two wickets fell for only six runs, but before the Hon. C. Ward was dismissed for four Egerton added a few. C. Heseltine and the Hon. H. Stonor made three and five, and H. Millin was only short double figures by one when he was bowled by Woods. The other batsmen allowed Egerton to carry out his bat for 88, and the innings closed for 75 runs. Egerton had played cautiously, as may be seen from the fact that he only made one hit for four to his 15 singles. After the adjournment for luncheon H. H. Cobb and E. R. Hill went to the wickets for West Herts, the opposing bowlers being Heseltine and Milner. Hill, when he had made five, lost his wicket to Heseltine, and with the score at 14, H. R. Barker followed for one. The state of the game improved when C. R. Humbert came in, but he was caught out by Lord Essex when he had scored 12. The Hon. R. Ward went on to bowl in place of Milner, but by Butcher's stylish play the score gradually rose. Cobb was bowled after he had been in nearly an hour for 18 runs, and Woods made a duck. On Marguel's downfall C. H. Little came in, and a capital stand was made, 68 runs being added for the next wicket, for 82 of which the new comer was responsible. At 149 Butcher was caught behind the wickets by Lord Rosslyn, after having made 41 runs by most excellent cricket. In his score were included, three fours, five threes, and three doubles. The only remaining batsman to do anything was H. G. Barnett, who shaped very well for his 15, and shortly before 5 o'clock the innings terminated for 156. Lord Essex's XI. went in again and fared worse than before, the score only reaching 51. C. Grenfell and Lord H. Tempest contributing 12 and 18 respectively, the former being magnificently caught in the long field by H. H. Cobb. It should be mentioned that in the second innings C. Heseltine did not bat. The match was thus a win for West Herts by an innings and 30 runs. Scores:—

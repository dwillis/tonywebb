WATFORD OBSERVER: SATURDAY 3 AUGUST 1895-4.

ATHLETIC SPORTS.

Matches for to-day are as follows:—Gentlemen of Herts v. Mr. G. H. Wailes's XI., at Arlesdene, Cheshunt; the Roamers v. Abbots Langley, at Abbots Langley; Watford Brewery v. Watford Urban District Council, on the Brewery Ground; Melrose v. Oxoley, at Oxoley.

On Bank Holiday, Cannon Brewery v. M. B. Foster and Sons, at Little Otterpool; Kings Langley v. St. Giles-in-the-Fields, at Kings Langley; Rickmansworth v. Northwood, at Rickmansworth.

On Monday and Tuesday, Hertfordshire v. Bedfordshire, at Bedford.

On Wednesday and Thursday, Hertfordshire v. M.C.C. and Ground, at Lords.

On Friday and Saturday, Hertfordshire v. Worcestershire, at Worcester.

To-day the Watford Cycling Club run to Luton.

To-day the Watford Wheelers run to Tring, at 3.30, and on Wednesday evening they go to Harrow.

The entries for the West Herts Sports on August Bank Holiday are the best it has ever been the club's lot to obtain, and everything points to this meeting being the largest and most successful ever held in the county.

In fact it is quite possible and even probable that some of the grass records will be beaten.

Everything that can be done in the way of arrangements for the comfort of competitors and spectators is being seen to by the Committee, who also intend to prevent overcrowding at the winning post, so that the spectators from every part of the ground will have an opportunity of seeing the finishes. There will be enclosures, too, to which admission can be gained by extra payment.

Lord Essex's XI. proved hardly strong enough for the West Herts Club on Saturday, and were beaten by an innings and 30 runs. C. Heseltine, who frequently plays for the M.C.C., was included in the team, while behind the wickets Lord Rosalyn gave a good account of himself.

For the visiting team the Hon. F. Egerton, who carried out his bat for 35 runs, was the only batsman who could make headway against the bowling of J. Woods and R. R. Barker.

On the other side A. Butcher hit up 41, and O. H. Little came next with 32.

R. R. Barker was very successful with the ball, and took eleven wickets for 84 runs, while J. Woods took eight for 32. Had it not been for C. Heseltine, who captured eight wickets for 31 runs, there is no saying where the West Herts score would have ended.

White umpired for the visitors, as it was arranged that no professional should play. On that account T. Attwell and Burton were absent.

The second match of the County week against Worcestershire was lost by Herts by nine wickets, the home eleven going first to the wickets hit up 106, to which their opponents responded with 118. The result of the second innings was to leave Herts in a majority of 25, which was wiped off by Worcestershire for the loss of only one wicket.

C. H. Little kept wicket for Herts and in the first innings there was not one extra, while he brought off two catches. Shaw filled the same position on the other side and gave a most brilliant display. He stood close up against the bowling of Raynor which was almost as fast as that of Woods. The fielding of the visitors was all through particularly smart.

There were three hits for six, for two of which Bowley was responsible, twice, once in each innings driving Titchmarsh over the boundary seats. E. T. Jobson sent a ball from W. H. Nicholls the same way.

White was the most successful of the Herts bowlers, taking five wickets for 54 runs in the first innings. Raynor in the first innings of Hertfordshire came out with five wickets for 25, and in the second six wickets fell to Arnold for 28 runs. Raynor took the last four of his wickets for only five runs.

West Herts on Wednesday and Thursday gained a most meritorious victory over the Gentlemen of Herts by eight wickets. Barker, for West Herts, made the highest score of the day, and the analysis of the bowling of Woods and White read well in both innings.

As the game finished rather early on Thursday afternoon, both teams went in again. The players on both sides slogged their hardest. Strange to say the brothers Barnett headed the scores with 34 each. Also opposing one another were the brothers Hill.

The return tennis match with Barnet was played on Saturday at Watford, and again proved a very close contest and was watched with much interest by a number of spectators. The result, though all the matches were not completed, was in favour of the home team, which by-the-bye was not of full strength. Scores appear in our report.

The players and friends were most hospitably entertained at tea by the kind invitation of Mrs. C. R. Humbert.

Mr. W. F. D. Schreiber's "little jumper" Ruin by Free Trade has again been successful in some of the best jumping company of all sizes at Wellingborough, securing the first prize. This pony's sponsors will have something to answer for!

The Bushey Grove Football Club have elected Mr. F. W. Hocker, of 21, Pinner-road, Watford, to the post of hon. secretary, as Mr. S. J. Nixon has resigned.

On Tuesday Mr. I. Clifford and Thom of the West Herts Golf Club played the return match with Mr. F. Watts and Milmo of Neasden. The arrangement was for 36 holes home and home. At Neasden the West Herts representatives won the first round by two holes and the second by three holes, and this week were again successful at Bushey Hall links, winning by four holes in the first round and one in the last. A very creditable performance.

KILDARE MILITIA v. ROY OF ALLIES.—The above match was played on the Harriest ground, Curragh Camp, on Saturday, resulting in a brilliant victory for the home team. The visitors won the toss, and sent "Kewy" and Thornhill to the wicket, but the former, before he was well set, was run out by his partner. Thorn-hill was then joined by de Roche, who commenced hitting out at once, and quickly ran up a fine innings of 58. Thornhill left shortly afterwards, having contributed a very sound 28. Loch ran well in the wickets and played good cricket for his 32 (not out). Sergeant Dr. Cahill's excellent bowling accounted for the remainder of the team, and the innings closed for 145. Keogh and Dennis started the batting for the Kildare, and the former's acquaintance with the stick was clearly brought to a close, and his place was taken by Gibson. Dennis was smartly caught by Thornhill at point for a useful 29, and Sergeant-Dr. Cahill joining them, the pair batted out freely, brought the score to 100 before the latter was dismissed for 28. The next stand was made by Greman, who, by the aid of scientific cricket, contributed 35. Wickets began to fall now with great rapidity, Colman being the first to go after an excellent 67, without giving a single chance. Whessman was the only remaining member of the team who succeeded in getting double figures, being not out 26. Mr Butler-Bowden, who is better known in the hunting line in the cricket field, made 7, and shortly after the innings closed for 235. Score:—

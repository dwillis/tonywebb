MONTGOMERY v. ABERYSTWYTH.—Played at Lymore
Park, Montgomery, on Saturday, in rainy weather. The
home captain won the toss and sent the visitors in.
Williams and A. Eaton, the Montgomery bowlers, were in
fine form, taking six wickets for 12, four for 17 respec-
tively, and get rid of the Yatwiftians for 32, of which E.
Davies, a Montgomery substitute, made 12. The Mont-
gomery innings realised 111, towards which A. Eaton (11),
O. D. Williams (41), E. T. Harris (13), Shaw (12), and L.
Griffiths (11 not out), were the chief contributors. E.
Davies, the Montgomery substitute, bowled well for the
visitors, who had a weak team.

WEM v. SHAVINGTON.—Played at Shavington on
Wednesday.
Anthony and Clayton went to the wicket
first for Wem, and faced the bowling of Lonsdale and
Arkensall. Runs came freely, and the score rose to 97
before Clayton was caught and bowled by Exham. This
broke the lea, Anthony being also dismissed in the next
over for a freely hit 42. The rest of the wickets fell
steadily, and the innings closed for 112 runs. Tea was
then partaken of, and on resuming only one over had been
bowled when rain came on heavily and stopped the play
for the day. Scores :—

MONTGOMERY v. PONTESBURY.—These teams played off
their return match at the latter place on Wednesday, in
dull weather. The home team, which comprised a strong
eleven, batted first, and were all dismissed by A. Eaton
and Williams for thirty-five, Eaton taking five wickets for
twelve, and Williams four for seventeen. Amos, who
compiled a dozen, was the only man to make any stand.
For the visitors the brothers Eaton were in rare form,
Pryce Eaton carrying his bat from first wicket down for
an admirable score of sixty-nine, while Alf Eaton did
yeoman service, and, together with his senior, succeeded
in passing the Pontesbury score with only a single wicket
down. The other Montgomery men were accounted for by
Amos and Cartwright, who secured four wickets each.

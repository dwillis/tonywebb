BERROW'S WORCESTER JOURNAL: SATURDAY 31 AUGUST 1895-S.

EASTBOURNE v. OLD MALVERNIANS.
EXTRAORDINARY SCORING.
Some remarkable cricket was witnessed in this match, commenced at the Halfmoon, Eastbourne, on Friday, in brilliant weather. Going in first the visitors remained at the wickets all day and when stumps were drawn at 6.15 had compiled 502 for seven wickets. In just over five hours the second wicket took the score from 33 to 306. Burnup played excellent cricket for 150, which included nineteen 4's, eight 3's, and eleven 2's. E. H. Simpson stayed in at J. H. Copleston hit 16, and was then stumped, the score standing at 353 for four. In his brilliantly hit 130 were twelve 4's, ten 3's and eleven 2's. The fielding was rather loose towards the finish and two or three bad misses were made.
The visitors declared their innings closed on Saturday morning without going in again. The home side, in their first venture, compiled the creditable total of 263, F. W. Bush carrying out his bat for an excellent 56, which included six 4's, four 3's and eleven 2's. The Rev. H. R. Browne hit one 6, and nine 4's in his admirably batted 56. Following on, Eastbourne played out time, and the result was a draw. Score:—

HEREFORD CUP.—FINAL TIE.
ST. JOHN'S. v. PRESTWICKE.—This important match was played on the county ground, Hereford, on Saturday, before a good number of spectators. A capital wicket had been prepared, and St. John's, on winning the toss, commenced to bat at 12.15 with Messrs. Plum and Curtis, to the bowling of Messrs. Davie and K. C. Evelyn. With the total at 15, Curtis was well caught at cover point for 10, and Mr. Carmichael followed, and a capital stand was made until Carmichael was unfortunately run out at 52 for a well-played 16. Mr. Beach Hicks came soon in, but Mr. Hobson stopped with Mr. Plum until the last over before luncheon, when the latter was bowled for a very steady innings of 39, which included a splendid drive for five, and which had occupied an hour and 40 minutes, the total reaching 82 for four wickets. After luncheon Messrs. Young and Hobson were soon bowled, the latter batting well for 13, and Messrs. H. C. Isaac and Dodson then made a capital stand for the eighth wicket, the former in particular hitting hard and clean, until Mr. Dodson was run out for 12. Mr. Robinson stayed with Mr. Isaac until the total reached 161, Mr. Isaac carrying his bat out for a splendid innings of 42. Messrs. Davie, Haines, and K. C. Evelyn were the most successful bowlers for Prestwicke, and the fielding all round was first class.
Prestwicke sent in the Rev. H. C. Green-Price and Mr. F. L. Evelyn, at four o'clock, to withstand the deliveries of Messrs. Beach-Hicks and Lightwood. The total had only reached 11 when the Rev. Green-Price was exceedingly well caught by the Captain at a cover point, and Mr. F. C. Evelyn, who followed in, was out to a grand one-handed catch by Mr. Carmichael at point in the next over. Mr. F. L. Evelyn was bowled at 20, and when the score reached 31 three wickets fell at the same total, the Rev. A. Green-Price playing well for 12. Williams was smartly run out by Young and Curtis at 36, the last two wickets putting on 24, Thomas hitting very hard for the top score of the innings, 17. The total reached 62, the innings closing at 5.15, and the game resulting in an easy victory for St. John's by 99 runs. Mr. Beach-Hicks had a splendid analysis, capturing six wickets in 11 overs for 19 runs only. Mr. J. Lightwood also took three good wickets at a cost of 52 runs.
The fielding of the winners was excellent. The trophy will be held by the winning club for one year. Score:—

HANDBORNE C.C. — MARRIED v. SINGLE.—The annual match was played on Saturday, when the Married beat the Single by 23 runs. In this match there is always a prize for the highest scorer in first innings. Mr. E. Gummery secured the prize, a copper warming pan, and those that did not succeed in getting a run had a wooden spoon presented to them. Tea was provided at the Northwick Arms. Mr. Alex French was voted to the chair, and, on his presenting the prize, made a very humorous speech. Score:—

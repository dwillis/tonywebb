ABINGDON HERALD: SATURDAY 14 SEPTEMBER 1895-1.
OXFORD MATCHES.
COWLEY 2ND XI v. ST. PAUL'S.
This match, to decide which of the above clubs should hold the City Cup for the ensuing year, took place last Friday and Saturday on the City ground. There was about the usual attendance of spectators, and the weather was eminently satisfactory, except that the condition of the wicket was greatly altered on Saturday owing to a sharp fall of rain during Friday night. St. Paul's had the advantage of first innings, but started disastrously, three good wickets falling with only 8 runs scored. Goldsworthy, however, stopped the rot, and with the help of Hart and Wark put on 20 and 43 for the fifth and sixth wickets. In the end the total reached 114, Goldsworthy being credited with the fine score of 45. Cowley did not start their venture any too well, but later on, when Bancalari and Goodey were together, runs came at a rare pace, and 59 were added for the fourth wicket. After Bancalari left for a capital 44, Goodey continued to peg away till he was run out for 61, and the total ultimately reached 184. At their second time of asking, St. Paul's failed worse than in their first, the first wicket fell at 2, the second at 7, and, with the exception of Hughes, Mason, and Hart, little opposition was offered by the rest. The ultimate total was 75, which left Cowley with 6 to get to win. These were quickly obtained without loss, and Cowley had won the cup for 1895. The best bowling performance was accomplished by Green, who took 8 wickets for 31 in the second innings; Wyatt also bowled well in the first innings, securing 4 men for 29 runs. Score :—

D.A.C. v. STAR.—Played on Wednesday. The latter won the toss, but put their opponents in. T. Walters, 33; J. Penny, 32; W. Browning, 37; and H. E. Walker, 35, not out. All scored at a great rate, and in less than a hour and a half had run up the fine total of 195 for 7 wickets. A most unaccountable collapse took place when the D.A.C. went in, six of their bats being out for 13. The last three wickets, however, played up very pluckily. Wallace, 21; Roberts, 14; and Haines 18; all doing well. The latter's 18 included four 4's, all off W. King. Walters came out with the fine analysis of six for 27, King taking the other four for 34. The D.A.C. entertained their friends and opponents to tea, as is the rule during the cricket week, and a most acceptable interlude. Score :—

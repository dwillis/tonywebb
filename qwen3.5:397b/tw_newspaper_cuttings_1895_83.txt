Coldstream v Kelso.—Played in the Home Park on Saturday afternoon, and resulted in a draw. Kelso played havoc with the Coldstream bowling, which was repeatedly changed, and put on the closure at 180 for 2 wickets. Coldstream had 114 for 7 wickets, and the play of T B Scott and Macdonald against the excellent bowling
of the Kelso men was a proof of the superior batting abilities of the two men. Macdonald's 50 (not out) was made up of very fine play. Colonel Graham's 51 for Kelso, and Stevenson's 75 (not out) were meritorious performances, and were suitably recognised by the large audience in attendance. Tea was kindly provided by Dr and Mrs Henderson, and this refection was amply enjoyed by the players and many others. The following are the scores:—Kelso: Col. CONSETT AND DISTRICT v ELSWICK WORKS.
Played at the fete at Shotley Spa on Whit-Monday, the game being watched by a vast assemblage of specta-tors. Elswick won the toss, and Consett began batting with Williamson and Siddell, to the bowling of Elsey and Robinson. In the third over Williamson was dismissed without a run having been scored. Godfrey joined Siddell, when a splendid stand was made, 68 runs being recorded before Siddell was caught by Robinson for a capital 32, he having played all the bowling with the greatest ease and confidence. Godfrey was the only one who subsequently offered any defence, getting 61 before he was clean bowled by Elsey. The Consett total reached 126. Only an hour remained for batting when Elswick began operations to the bowling of Janes and Wood. After batting just over an hour Elswick completed their innings for 28 runs. Janes took four wickets for 9 runs, and Wood six wickets for 18. Score :—

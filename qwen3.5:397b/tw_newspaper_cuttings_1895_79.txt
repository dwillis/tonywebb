Poole Park gained a very easy victory over an eleven
representing the L. and S.W.R. Co. at Bournemouth
East, on Saturday. The visitors were beaten at every
department of the game, their batting display especially
being very poor. Poole, who started batting, did not
look like making a big score, for they lost several good
men for just over 20, but when Adams and Myers
became associated a change came over the game. The
pair hit out in fine style, and before a separation was
effected the Poole score had reached a respectable
number. Myers was the first to leave, his share being
33. Adams still continued to hit out, but with the fall
of another wicket, the home captain declared, the total
standing at 124 for six wickets, with Adams 64 not out.
This is the highest score Adams has ever obtained on
the Park. The visitors started badly, and did not
improve as time went on, the fine bowling of Gill, sen.,
and Myers being much too good for them. Their
innings closed for 18. Gill's bowling was remarkable, as
will be seen :—

did rather better, the venture closing for 34, leaving
Poole the winners by an innings and 72 runs. The

PYLEWELL PARK v. BOLDRE C.C.—This match was
played at Pylewell on August 24th, and resulted in an
easy win for the home team by an innings and 14 runs.
The visitors, who won the toss, elected to bat, and were
dismissed for the miserable total of 30. Lane, who alone
reached double figures, batted well for his 16. Plowman
bowled with deadly effect, taking 6 wickets for 9 runs.
The home team were not disposed of until they had
made 106, of which Shephard and Hibbert were the
chief contributors, making 21 and 30 respectively. The
visitors were more fortunate in their second venture,
and made 62 before they were all out, Lane again reach-
ing double figures, leaving Pylewell easy winners
as above stated. Scores :—

PYLEWELL PARK v. PENNINGTON.—This match was
played at Pylewell on August 31st, and resulted in a
win for the home team by the narrow margin of 10 runs.
The victory of the home'ers was mainly due to the
batting and bowling of Hibbert, who made 30 runs and
took 7 wickets for 16. Torah also batted well for the
losers, and was unfortunate in getting run out when he
had made 20. In their second innings, Pylewell had
lost 6 wickets for 84, of which Croucher, Shephard, and
Hibbert were the chief contributors. Scores :—

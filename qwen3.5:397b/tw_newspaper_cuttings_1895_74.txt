WESTERN DAILY MERCURY: THURSDAY, FRIDAY 15, 16 AUGUST 1895-2.

BEDMINSTER v. PLYMOUTH GARRISON.
Continuing their tour in Devonshire, Bedminster opened a two days' encounter with the Plymouth Garrison at Mount Wise, Devonport, yesterday. There was but a moderate attendance when the game started at noon, but during the afternoon, notwithstanding the numerous counter attractions, the crowd increased. It was decided to play twelve aside, and winning the toss the Garrison decided to take the first innings, Captain Curteis and Lieutenant Collins being the first pair to face the bowling of McCarthy and Bucknell. The former's first over proved disastrous to Captain Curteis, who had only scored a single. The vacancy was filled by Lieutenant Perry-Ayscough, who soon secured a mastery over the bowling, necessitating a change. Ayscough was the first to go, falling a victim to a good ball sent up by Hemmens. He had made 40, which included six 4's. Lieutenant Collins did not long survive his partner, as he was well caught in the same bowler's succeeding over. Lieutenant Taylor and Lieutenant Sparks were together for a very short period, and it was not until Lieutenant Hobbs partnered the latter that anything like a resistance was offered to the bowling. Bucknell had again gone on bowling, and in his first over dismissed the Garrison captain with a ball which broke in from the off. The outgoing man had made 16, which included a six and two fours. Lieutenant Harrison and Captain Robinson were the next pair to cause trouble, but the former should have been caught when he had made 20. Profiting by the let-off both batsmen signalised the event by each hitting a 3, but eventually Harrison was well caught in the long field for 24. The innings closed for 157.
At a quarter to four the visitors commenced their batting, Cook and Lawson facing Harrison (from the Pavilion end) and Finlaison. Runs came slowly, and the result of half an hour's play was 21 runs. Lawson was then caught, and with two runs added three more wickets fell in quick succession. Brown and McCarthy made a stand, and when the former was run out the score was 55. McCarthy did not long survive, as he had the misfortune to play on in the next over. T. Hemmens and J. Bucknell now became associated, and offered a stubborn resistance to the bowling, no runs than six being tried. At length Hemmens fell to a good catch by Captain Robinson, who also, a few minutes later, accounted for the downfall of G. Hemmens by running about forty yards and bringing off a magnificent catch in the deep field. The innings closed for 117. Play will be resumed at 11.30 this morning. Score:-

BEDMINSTER v. PLYMOUTH GARRISON.
The second day's play was entered into yesterday at about 11.30. The weather was again gloriously fine. The heavy dew which fell early in the morning made the turf somewhat damp, but it was not at all detrimental to the play. But a scanty number of enthusiasts lined the rails before lunch, though the number was considerably augmented as the game grew. The Garrison batted first, Lieutenants Collins and Perry-Ayscough receiving the deliveries of Bucknell and Brown. They had compiled 10 when the former's wicket was collared at 2 by Brown. Captain Curteis partnered the remaining man, but his innings was of a short duration, and only a single was added to the former score for the fall of his wicket, and that one not to his credit. Lieutenant Sparks was bowled by Brown for a duck, and the vacancy was filled by Lieutenant Cave, who, with Lieutenant-Ayscough, made a decent stand. The latter was eventually bowled by Brown for eleven, but had the pleasure of leaving the score at 33. Lieutenant Cave, who seemed to be getting settled down, was given out 'leg before wicket' off one of Brown's full pitchers. His sixteen was the reward of steady play. The Rev. F. C. Stebbing made a three and a two off Brown's trundling, and then gave his favourite high life off Bucknell, who had his revenge soon after by clean bowling him. Taylor after contributing a dozen was caught easily by Lawson, and soon afterwards Lieutenants Hobbs and Captain Robinson found themselves together. They knocked up a few runs, when the bell sounded for lunch, seven wickets having been destroyed for seventy runs, a somewhat cheap rate. After lunch Hobbs and Robinson resumed play, each of them running into double figures. With Harrison's 11 and Finlaison's 9, and twenty-four extras, the total reached 114. Bedminster made a start soon after 3.30, and had to make 155 to win. E. H. Cook and A. J. Lawson tried conclusions with the bowling of Sparks and Harrison. After only a few minutes' play, Cook was sent back through a magnificent catch by Captain Robinson of Harrison, only having made 2, the total being 8. When Lawson had taken the score up to 20 H. G. Harris was stumped by Taylor for a duck, and the same number was telegraphed for the destruction of E. G. Murdoch's wicket, who was smartly caught and bowled by Sparks. The same bowler performed equally as smart a trick with Lawson after he had run up 18, only 1 being added for the fall of the fourth wicket. Brown and Harris partnered, and batted steadily. Harris was eventually bowled for 10 by a spanking ball from Finlaison, 47 being the total. J. McCarthy went to the assistance of Brown, but only made a single off Hobbs, who had been put on bowling, when he was declared leg before wicket off the same bowler. Brown was dismissed by a marvellous catch by Taylor when his score stood at 23, which was the highest for the day. Hemmens made a good exhibition for a well-deserved 28. Maggs had lengthy innings for 8, being stumped. The innings closed within ten minutes of time, Bedminster being 51 runs behind. Messrs. E. H. Hall and J. Gardiner were the umpires. Score:-

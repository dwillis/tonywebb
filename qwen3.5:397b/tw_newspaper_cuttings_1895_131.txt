WATFORD OBSERVER: SATURDAY 17 AUGUST 1895-2.

WEST HERTS v. BUSHEY.
The return and much looked for match between West Herts and Bushey was on Saturday decided on the Bushey ground. For this, if for no other, encounter West Herts can always rely on having a hard fight, and of late victory has rested with their rivals, the one match of this season and both of last having ended in favour of the village team. H. M. Harford, the energetic secretary of the Bushey club, had again raised a strong eleven, which was strengthened by several Hurst Johnians, who, the day before, had been in opposition. Nor had West Herts shown indifference for although White and H. H. Cobb were absent, the former assisting the county against Worcestershire, the side placed in the field was quite representative, A. C. Beaton being back again, and R. R. Barker, G. H. Nelson, H. A. F. Astley, and the brothers Barnett turning out. The day was favoured with charming weather, and the still more charming presence of a large and gay company of ladies, and the attendance was ample proof of the interest taken in the play. The home captain won the toss and putting his own side in went out R. R. Cruise and H. W. Keeling. Both batsmen started well, and before Keeling was caught by Little behind the wickets 43 runs had been put together. P. A. Stuart, although the total was increased by nine during his stay, was responsible for none himself, and was caught off Astley's bowling by Anderson. Waller too had a short stay, but with Harvey's arrival the score began to rise and had reached 82 when Cruise was caught by Anderson after having compiled a most useful 52. H. C. S. Cooper also met with success and was not dismissed until he had become responsible for 36. When the century had been passed by 11, three wickets fell, W. F. H. Ward and C. D. Fastnedge being bowled by Hurrows for ducks. It is somewhat remarkable that of the whole eleven they alone were bowled, all the others being caught. Harford was becoming set when he skied a ball into long field, and Barker brought off a most brilliant catch. The Bushey innings closed for 158, E. H. Lalley being not out 11. At about half-past 11 R. Barker and R. C. Barnett opened the West Herts attack facing the bowling of Keeling and Cooper. After a maiden over by Keeling, Cooper off his own bowling caught Barnett before any runs had been made. Another sub-fortune befell the visitors when Barker was magnificently caught by Evans low down and one-handed. The tide was stemmed by a stand by J. Woods, who made 12 before being stumped by Fastnedge, H. E. Barnett joined C. R. Humbert, and was soon out, but Humbert was more fortunate, on one occasion sending the ball out of the ground. He, however, was bowled by Cooper with a ball which broke in almost a yard, seven wickets being then down for 94. The play of Astley and Nelson necessitated a change of bowling and Ward was put on in place of Keeling, and Stuart instead of Cooper, but the score had been almost doubled when Nelson was caught in the long slips by Lalley. Anderson gave no trouble being caught off his first ball by Stuart. Astley soon followed—lbw to Ward, and the innings only realised 70 runs, Hurrows being not out. Rain prevented West Herts following on at once, but on again going to the wickets they were more successful, making 40 for two wickets, Hurrows being still not out with 38 to his credit. The match was thus a win for Bushey by 83 runs on the first innings.

GENTLEMEN OF HERTS v. ST. ALBANS.
The first match in the St. Albans fortnight was commenced on the Herts County Ground at St. Albans, on Monday, between the Gentlemen of Herts and the home team. The former batted first on a splendid wicket, and were not dismissed until they had made 244, Butcher being the chief scorer with a nicely compiled 68. St. Albans had lost two wickets for 36, when stumps were drawn. There was a fashionable assemblage of onlookers, and at night there was a grand open air concert with illuminations. Rain prevented play on Tuesday, and the match had to be left drawn. Score:—

EASTERN DAILY PRESS: MONDAY 19 AUGUST 1895-3.

SOUTHDOWN WANDERERS' TOUR.
A team from Sussex has just-completed its annual tour, after having visited and tried conclusions with the following teams:—Bury United, Fakenham, Swaffham, Holkham, and King's Lynn. The programme of fixtures also contained an announcement of a match with Melton Constable on Tuesday last, but owing to the incessant downpour of rain during the greater part of the day play was impossible, and the match had to be cancelled. The team comprised several of Lord Sheffield's Eleven, as well as Butcher, the Sussex county man. Out of the five matches played they succeeded in winning four.

BURY UNITED v. SOUTHDOWN WANDERERS.
The first match was played at Bury St. Edmund's on Monday, August 12th. Trudgett, the pro. associated with Bury and West Suffolk, rendered very good service on behalf of the home team. In bowling he took six wickets for 26 runs, and when playing in the second innings of Bury United, carried out his bat for a well played 21, 47 runs having been scored for the loss of a couple of wickets. In the first innings the veteran J. G. Francis contributed a well played 26. On behalf of the home team L. A. Kent secured two wickets for 15 runs. For the visitors Butcher and Edwards were particularly prominent in trundling.

CRICKET.
UP. SHEPHERD'S XL. beat WOOLLEY PARK.
This match was played at Woolley Park on Wednesday, Woolley going in first put together the total of 67, of which Hayden and Smith did best with 17 and 14 respectively. Mr. Shepherd's team then went in the score at lunch time standing at 81 for 6 wickets, Hutchins and Weaver playing good cricket for their runs. The innings closed for Mr. Woolley going in again put on 55. Mr. Shepherd's team now requiring 32 runs to win, obtained them with the loss of 2 wickets, Hutchins again doing well. Both teams were kindly entertained to tea after the match by Mr. Wroughton. Score:-

INKPEN beat FALKLAND C.C.
The visitors to meet the Inkpen C.C. on their own ground were rather late in coming, the time fixed for the match being two o'clock, while the game did not commence till three-four. The home four won the toss and play quickly began. The chances of winning looked rather small at one time, four wickets having fell for 20 runs. D. Bance soon turned the tide by adding 63 runs more to the total, his score consisting of a splendid drive for 4, 5, two 2's, and 18 well hit singles. The Rev. H. D. Butler made a good hit for 4, and when this innings was closed carried out his bat. G. Butler and J. Hopkins did not bat. The visitors were dismissed for 22 runs, mainly owing to the first-rate bowling of T. May, who took 7 wickets, his average being 1. Score:-

LAMBOURN beat CHILTON.
The return match was played on Saturday last, on the Lambourn ground and, as the score will show, resulted in a win for Lambourn, the home-sters thus avenging their defeat in the previous match. The game did not commence till rather late, and consequently had to be decided on the first innings. The weather was delightful and the wicket really good. Chilton won the toss, but elected to send the home team in. A very poor start was made, as two Lambourn wickets went down for 0, but a wonderfully different complexion was put on the game, when, after H. Penfold had scored seven in good style, F. Harris and J. Bryant got well set. F. Harris made some splendid hits, and the spectators loudly cheered his freehand play, his score of 35 being made in a very short time. J. Bryant also went for his shots and was greatly applauded upon his retirement. V. Hobbs and C. J. Maberley rendered great assistance to the score, before the innings was declared closed at 129. The Chilton team did but little, only one player (S. Pauting) obtaining double figures. The bowling of Messrs. Hobbs and Bryant was very effective, and F. Harris made two capital catches in the long field. Chilton followed on, and at the call of time no wicket had fallen, the score being 51. H. New and Rumball having scored well during the innings. SOUTHINGTON beat MR. A. KINGSMILL'S XII.
This highly exciting and closely contested match was played in lovely weather on the charmingly situated Symondton Cricket Ground by postal enthusiasts, the age being limited to 16, several, however, had many years still before them ere they reach this degree of experience. We would draw attention to the excellent bowling on the Southington side of Kneller and Poynter, and the batting of these and also Christie and North. The star of the Symondton team was J. Chute whose bowling was so effective in his batting, and his success was rewarded by the presentation bat offered for the highest score by Mr. Wyndham Portal. Leyland and Harrison had some share in the downfall of the Southington wickets, and by the smart wicket-keeping of Andrew Kingsmill, in spite of their tremendous exertions Southington proved successful by the narrow margin of seven runs. NEWTOWN beat PARISH CHURCH INSTITUTE.
This return match was played in the beautiful grounds of Sandford Park, on Saturday, August 31st, there being a goodly number of spectators present, including Lady Bathurst and family and friends. Newtown went first to the wickets, and by good play and hard hitting on the part of Ridal (22), and Gardner (15), made a total of 48 runs, Gunning's bowling being too good for them, he being well backed up by Matthews and Fewtrell. On the Institute handling the willow, however, they were soon disposed of for 30 runs, Vickery being top scorer with 16, Gibbs, Rudd, Allsop, and Baverstock being the bowlers. Tea was then partaken of, and Newtown again went to the wickets, but the Institute not being able to go in again, the home team won a pleasant game on the first innings by eight runs.

ADSBURY HOUSE beat NEWTOWN HOUSE.
On Thursday afternoon a match was played between elevens of the Adbury House estate and Newtown House, the game being won by the Adbury House eleven, a result being mainly due to the good bowling of H. Barber, F. James, and J. Gason; C. Cooper brought off a beautiful catch at short slip, and E. Berry made a big drive for five runs. For the losers M. Stratford, White, Street, and Weight acquitted themselves very well. A capital tea had been prepared by the housekeeper on the grounds, which she played did justice to. Altogether a most enjoyable afternoon was spent, and a hearty vote of thanks was carried with acclamation to Mr. Walter Arkwright, f.r. his kindness. Score:-

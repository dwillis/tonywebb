WATFORD OBSERVER: SATURDAY 31 AUGUST 1895-2.

WEST HERTS v. UXBRIDGE.
West Herts closed their season on Saturday by taking a fairly strong eleven down to Uxbridge. Strange to say they won the toss and batted first on an excellent wicket. H. G. Barnett and White opening the bowling of R. Moss and Woods. White was quickly dismissed by the former, and after that Cobb fell to the same bowler when he had compiled 14. Barnett, who was playing steadily, was joined by R. S. Barker, and with the score at 28, was bowled by Moss. Woods on coming in soon fell to work, driving the ball on one occasion off the ground for six. He stayed long enough to see the departure of Barker at 71, while M. S. Ward and O. H. Little both failed to score. However, on R. C. Barnett's arrival matters improved and the century was passed amid some cheering, but at 123 the new comer was dismissed by a catch by Collins, and two runs later Woods was caught by Tompson. He had given a grand display of cricket and has seldom been seen in better form. His 66, the result of an hour and twenty minutes' batting, included in addition to the hit for six already mentioned, three fours, eight threes, five twos, and fourteen singles. O. B. Humbert making nine, and F. E. Burrows six, the innings closed for 148 at about a quarter to 2. After the adjournment for luncheon, W. R. Collins and O. C. Stone opened the Uxbridge innings. Twenty-five runs were made before Collins was caught by Woods of his own bowling, but the good beginning was maintained by his successor and it was not until the score had reached 78 that Burrows captured G. A. Cobb's wicket. A. C. Bird gave little trouble, but, with Stone and Woods in, Uxbridge passed the century. At 119, however, Stone was sent back by a catch by Burrows. He had been somewhat lucky, Ward missing an easy catch early in the game. More than a third of his runs was composed of singles. The other six wickets only produced 26 runs between them, but it proved sufficient to carry them past the West Herts total by four runs, although the match had been won before the fall of the ninth wicket. WEST HERTS CLUB.
Now that West Herts have played all their matches, by the courtesy of the Hon. Secretary we are enabled to publish a summary of the season's play. The Club has this year been most successful, especially when it is remembered that the teams met have been stronger than ever. In addition to the majority of matches having been won, the average number of runs per wicket is greater than that of their opponents by 6.64 runs. The following are the details:—

URBAN COUNCIL v. BENSKIN'S AND CO.
This match was played on Saturday between the Watford Urban Council and Benskin and Co.'s team, and resulted in a win for the Urban Council's men by 11 runs. Great praise is due to Smith and Saunders—Smith taking seven wickets for 12 runs, whilst Saunders was top scorer with 15 runs, he also did very well with the bowling. Score:—

NORTHAMPTON HERALD: SATURDAY 17 AUGUST 1895-2.

NORTHAMPTON TOWN LEAGUE.
SECOND SECTION.
Victoria-road v. Excelsior Albion. — Low scoring ruled in this match, which was played on the Borough Ground on Saturday. Victoria-road compiled 31, P. King's 9 being the highest contribution. Copson took four wickets for 13, and Tate four for 10. The Excelsior Albion, who only batted eight men, were dismissed for 25, W. Darby (9) being the highest scorer. Victoria-road thus won by six runs. For Victoria-road P. Roberts obtained three wickets for 10, and V. Cooke four for 10.
Princess-street v. Doddridge. — Princess-street suffered another reverse on Saturday on the Borough Ground, Doddridge replying with 103 to their opponents' 61. J. Ingram, Princess-street's "stonewaller," who scored 24, was the only one to get into double figures against the destructive bowling of J. Smith (eight wickets for 39 runs). W. Leach obtained one wicket for 23, and A. Sutton one for 9. The chief contributors to the Doddridge total were A. Jones (11), C. Sutton (14), C. Jones (11), and W. Tharby (10). For Princess-street W. Darland took three wickets for 23, F. Smith one for 12, W. Steere two for 5, W. Rogers two for 22, and P. Brooks two for 25.
Moss Rose v. Pioneer.—The Moss Rose administered a crushing defeat to the Pioneer on the Borough Ground on Saturday. Batting first, the former "declared" when the score was 106 for six wickets, W. Kent, who played an excellent not out innings of 44, and W. Langley (35) being the chief contributors. Stanford (two for 38), A. Knight (one for 18), Williamson (two for 16), and H. Wright (one for 20), trundled for the Pioneer. The Pioneer were all out at 66. J. Knight (11) and A. Knight (10) being the only batsmen to reach double figures. Bert Goodall (six wickets for 32) and Garrard (four for 21) were the bowlers for the Moss Rose.

BEDFORDSHIRE v. OXFORDSHIRE.
This return match was concluded at Bedford on Saturday, when the home county had to bow to defeat to the tune of an innings and 30 runs. It was very evident after Bedfordshire's first venture that their opponents were too strong for them, and Oxfordshire brought the day's play to a close with the respectable total of 221, thus leaving Bedfordshire the task of scoring 131 to save an innings defeat. On Saturday they took up their second stand, but the Brothers Rogers's bowling was so destructive that they were all dismissed for 101, P. Rogers taking eight wickets for 53 runs. Oxfordshire thus gained their second victory over Bedfordshire this season by an innings and 30 runs.

M.C.C. AND GROUND v. BUCKINGHAMSHIRE.
It did not take long on Saturday to bring the match at Lord's between these teams to a conclusion, Buckinghamshire beating the M.C.C. by an innings and 26 runs—some such result having been foreshadowed by the state of the game overnight. St. James' Excelsior C.C. v. St. James' Hall.—Played at St. James' End on Thursday, and won by the former by 45 runs. The Hall, batting first, could do nothing against the bowling of Dillingham (five wickets for no runs) and Wright (five for seven runs). On the Excelsior batting they made 84. G. Wright scored 17 and A. Kirton 8. Scores: Excelsior, 84; St. James' Hall, 9.
St. Paul's v. Princess-street Y.L.C.B.—Played on the Racecourse on Saturday, and won by St. Paul's by two runs. Scores: St. Paul's 45, Princess-street 43.
St. Paul's v. Wellingborough All Saints' Juniors.—Played at Wellingborough on August 7th, and won by St. Paul's by 55 runs. Scores: 91 and 90.
Wellingborough All Saints v. Kettering Working Men's Club.—Played at Wellingborough on Saturday, and won by the home team, who scored 125 for nine wickets, against 42 of the losers. G. Hooper 58, E. Vickers 12, A. Gunn 15, W. Bennett 14, not out, and J. Pettitt 12, not out, batted well for All Saints, and S. Browning, not out, 15, did best for the visitors.

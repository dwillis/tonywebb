NORTHAMPTON HERALD: SATURDAY 10 AUGUST 1895-2
NORTHAMPTON TOWN LEAGUE
FIRST SECTION.
Excelsior v. Star.—This match was played on the County Ground on Saturday, and ended in a draw, rain on this occasion spoiling what would otherwise have been a most interesting contest. Batting first, the Excelsior compiled 145 for eight wickets, when they declared their innings closed. C. M. Lucas scored 71, included in which were one 3, six 4's, and five 2's, he being, however, missed three times; A. Migsley 33, and H. Tull 12. G. Squires obtained four wickets for 37 runs, W. Kirby two for 26, H. Davis one for 10, and C. Roberts one for 16. Upon the Star batting they made a rather poor start, F. Kendall, who was in good trundling form, obtaining three wickets in the first three overs. F. Cory, who scored 22 and A. Tite, 21 not out, however, made a good stand, and when stumps were drawn the score was 55 for four wickets. Kendall had three wickets for 16 and S. Kirkham one for three.
Clarence v. Colts.—Rain also interfered in this match on the County Ground on Saturday. Batting first, the Clarence, who only played ten men, scored 152, J. A. Bedford compiling 40, P. Twist 30, and F. Halley 15. For the Colts A. Mackness took four wickets for 31 runs, A. Ingham two for 36, and J. Brown two for 51. The Colts obtained 55 for two wickets, T. Lightwood not out 38, A. Mackness 17, and A. Butler 15. Venn took both wickets at a cost of 19 runs.
SECOND SECTION.
Trains v. Regent-square.—This match was played on the Racecourse on Saturday, when the Trains won by nine wickets and nine runs. Scores: Regent-square, 101 (W. Downing 34); Trains, 110 for one wicket (Thomas not out 63, Law not out 52). On behalf of the winners J. Potter took five wickets for 36. The Trains made their runs in an hour.
Excelsior Albion v. Artizan Star.—This game on the Racecourse on Saturday ended in a draw in favour of the latter. Scores: Excelsior Albion, 57 for six wickets (Swannell 31); Artizan Star, 134 for six wickets (A. Underwood 40), innings declared closed. A. Underwood (two for seven) for the Artizan Star, and Kendall (four wickets) for their opponents, were the most effective bowlers.
Primitive v. St. Katherine's.—On the Racecourse the "Prims" won by four wickets. Scores: Primitives, 129 for six (W. Parkin not out 36); St. Katherine's, 127 (W. Taylor not out 36, Alson 31, P. Oaklie 23). W. Taylor took three wickets for the winners, and Thornby three wickets for the losers.
THIRD SECTION.
Y.M.C.A. v. St. Sepulchre's B.C.—On the Racecourse on Saturday, the Y.M.C.A. won by seven runs. Scores: Y.M.C.A., 69; St. Sepulchre's B.C., 62.
Oak v. Wesleyans v. Wesleyan Star.—The former won this match on Saturday on the Borough Ground by 18 runs, scoring 37 runs to their opponents' 19.
Catholic Alma v. St. Giles'.—Played on the Borough Ground on Saturday, and won by the former. Scores: Catholic Alma, 35; St. Giles', 33.
BEDFORDSHIRE v. HERTFORDSHIRE.
This match was concluded at Bedford on Tuesday, and ended in a draw. Overnight Bedfordshire had been dismissed for 182, and their opponents had scored 35 for one. The play on Tuesday, owing to rain, did not commence until nearly three o'clock, when C and H. Pigg, the not outs, resumed their innings, the former being bowled by Manton when he had 25 to his credit, and the latter 31. The innings of 165 did not close until six o'clock. In the remaining half-hour Bedfordshire lost four wickets for 47, and the game ended in an even draw.

Wellingborough Grammar School (Part 2nd XI. v. Present 2nd XI.).—This match was played at Wellingborough on Friday, August 2nd. Scores: Past, 118; Present, 156.
St. Paul's v. Pioneer 2nd XI.—Played on the Racecourse on Saturday, and won by St. Paul's by six wickets. Scores: St. Paul's, 52 for four wickets; Pioneer 2nd XI., 51.
Moulton v. Overstone Park.—Played on Saturday at Overstone, and resulted in a win for the former by 26 runs. Moulton batted first, scoring 55, to which Overstone replied with 29. The wicket was very dead, and favoured the bowlers. For the visitors Mabbutt, Main, and Manning batted well, while Barnes was the highest scorer for the home-team. The Overstone wickets fell evenly to Cox and Glamspool, while Barnes accounted for the majority of those of Moulton.
Pioneer v. Church and Co. C.C.—Played on the Borough Ground on Saturday, when the Pioneer won easily by six wickets and 53 runs. Scores: Church's, 44; Pioneer, 97 for four wickets.

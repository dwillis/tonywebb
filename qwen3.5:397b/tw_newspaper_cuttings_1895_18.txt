BERKSHIRE CHRONICLE: SATURDAY 10 AUGUST 1895-2.

READING CRICKET WEEK.

A new departure in connection with the Reading Cricket Club has been inaugurated this year in the direction of a cricket week. The matches have been played during the last few days, and the cricket week has been a conspicuous success. Reading have been able to rely on excellent teams for each of their matches, such powerful exponents of the summer pastime as Capt. Nepean, Mr L. G. A. Collins, Mr H. J. Collins, Mr W. O. Nares, Mr F. E. Chapman, Rev C. E. Chapman, F. W. Finch, F. Deane, rendering assistance, &c., and some interesting cricket has been witnessed.

FIRST MATCH—v. MARYLEBONE C.C.

The week opened with this annual fixture against the M.C.C. The visitors had the assistance of four professionals, Handford, Pike, Sherwin, and Rylott, and two members of the Reading Club, Mr P. T. Baker and Mr Wm. Greenfield. Mr C. T. Murdoch, M.P., captained the visiting team.

Inclement weather greatly interfered with the game, and it had to be finally abandoned. Reading went in first to bat, and thanks mainly to A. Finch (35), H. J. Collins (25), H. G. A. Collins (27), succeeded in compiling 186 runs for nine wickets, when they declared their innings closed. M.C.C. then went in, but after scoring 18 for one wicket, stumps were drawn owing to the rain. Mrs Murdoch kindly entertained the teams at lunch and tea. In addition to Mr Murdoch, M.P., Mrs Murdoch and party, there were present Mrs Arthur, and Mrs and Miss Pott, Mr Stanley Pott and party, the Misses Finch, Mrs James Simonds, Miss Maurice, Mrs W. C. Blandy, Mr Sidney Maurice, Mrs Hayward, Col. Taylor, &c. The following were the scores:—

SECOND MATCH—v. MR C. E. KEYMER'S XI.

Mr C. E. Keymer, of Aldershot, who is an ardent cricketer, brought a strong team to Reading on Tuesday to play the local eleven. Mr Keymer's XI included C. E. Stacock, of Newbury, A. Finch, S. H. Maurice, and W. M. Greenfield, of Reading. Reading were successful in winning the toss, and their innings was remarkable for the fine display of batting by Mr L. G. A. Collins and Capt. Nepean, who made 151 and 104 respectively. When the score reached 301 Reading declared their innings closed. Mr Keymer's XI. began well, but the "rot" set in amongst the tail, and the innings closed for 105, Reading winning easily by 196 runs and four wickets. F. W. Finch took three wickets for 25 runs, Pott three for twenty, and Collins two for 4. The Reading Cricket Club provided lunch and tea for the teams. Amongst those present at the match were Major Mackenzie Edwards, Rev — Gilliam (Sunning), Colonel Taylor, Mr E. Sturges, Miss Garrett, Mr Claude Garrard, Mr J. H. Millard, the Misses Finch, Mrs James Simonds, and Miss Simonds. Scores:—

THIRD MATCH—v. MR W. HOWARD PALMER'S XI.

This match was commenced on Wednesday on the Reading Biscuit Factory C.C. Ground. Reading had spared no effort in getting together a really powerful eleven, whilst Mr Palmer had whipped up an exceedingly strong team, including those well known professionals, Bacon, of Hampshire, and Handford, of Notts. Some good play was again witnessed on Thursday by a large gathering. In the end Mr Palmer's eleven won by 35 runs. Score:—

TIVERTON v. SWANSEA TOURISTS.
The touring team of cricketers for Swansea visited Tiverton yesterday, and played against a team representing the town and district, on the Athletic Ground. The tourists went to the wickets first, and chiefly with the bowling of Shaw and Cranfield, were disposed of for 43. This total was quickly exceeded by the Tivertonians, their innings closed for 57. SIDMOUTH v. M.C.C.
This match was concluded at Sidmouth yesterday, the result being a draw. The home eleven batted the first day compiling 244, to which E. A. Nepoan contributed 121, and the Rev E. B. Bruton 61, both playing admirable cricket. When stumps were drawn M.C.C. had made 2 runs for no wickets. Resuming yesterday, they closed their innings for 192. Carlin and Titchmarsh batted for the visitors first, C. Wells did the "hat trick," bowling from the east end of the pitch, a performance which gained him warm applause from a large concourse of onlookers. The weather was all that could be desired, and the wicket in perfect condition. Score :-

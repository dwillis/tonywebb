BIRMINGHAM LEAGUE.

STOURBRIDGE v. ASTON UNITY.—This match was set for decision at Amblecote, on Saturday, but owing to the frequent showers, the umpires at six o'clock decided not to proceed further with the game. Unity won the toss, and went in the wickets, being represented by J. Devey and H. Widdowson. This pair remained together while 45 runs were put on, when, after a brief retreat for rain, Widdowson was bowled by Hingley for 15. This was the harbinger of a rapid fall of wickets, for, having found a convenient spot, Hingley bowled with deadly effect, and whereas he had been hit for 17 runs without a wicket, after the last resumption he took four wickets for 11 runs, so that he had an average of 7 per wicket. Devey played a splendid innings of 37, the chief hits in which were five 4's, two 3's, and two 2's, and he made 37 out of the 58 put on while he was in. Considerable comment was caused by the action of the umpires in closing the game so soon, and some very uncomplimentary remarks were passed about it. Score:—

BIRMINGHAM LEAGUE.—DIVISION II.

STOURBRIDGE (2ND) v. ASTON UNITY (2ND).—Stourbridge journeyed to Aston with only seven men. The Rev. A. C. Madden won the toss, and decided to bat, in the hope that others would turn up. Brown and Green opened the batting for Stourbridge, but runs came very slowly. Green batted well for his score. Madden and Brooks became partners, and made a good stand, Brooks contesting himself with keeping up his end, while Madden hit out freely. Brooks batted very patiently for an hour and a half for 6 runs. Madden's free cricket was a treat to watch. Rain came on about five o'clock, and stopped play for half-an-hour. Ultimately the match ended in a draw owing to the rain. Score:—

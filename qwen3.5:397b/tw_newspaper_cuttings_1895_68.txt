ROYAL CORNWALL GAZETTE: THURSDAY 3 OCTOBER 1895.

TRURO CRICKET CLUB SUPPER.

Those responsible for the arrangements in regard to Truro Cricket Club supper on Monday evening, chiefly, we believe, Mr. J. Bray (secretary) and Mr. T. Jennings (the treasurer), are deserving the heartiest thanks and praise of all who participated in it. The tables were exquisitely arranged and the supper admirably served, thanks to Miss Carlyon's careful and judicious management, and the smoking concert, which followed the feast, was admittedly about the best in point of vocal ability, selections of songs, and general conduct ever known in the city. The Mayor of Truro, as president of the club (Mr. Trevail), was at the head of the table. The other guests included the ex-Mayor (Mr. W. Bullen), Messrs. A. Jennings, W. J. Johns, John Julian, A. Blenkinsop, N. B. Bullen, John James, G. Gow, H. P. Smith, S. Smith, Dr. H. Sharp, Messrs. E. L. Carlyon, J. A. Jennings, J. Bray (sec.), T. Jennings (treas.), J. Rogers, R. Dixon, J. Luker, W. H. Bücher, J. Drewer, A. Johns, W. F. Hamilton, J. C. R. Crewes, Jas. Julian, J. Tonkin, T. E. Williams, A. Bluett, F. J. Mansfield, F. T. Dowsing, E. F. Winch, W. H. Knight, J. Barrett, T. Olver, B. Paul, E. Bullen, J. Lean, W. M. Pethybridge, A. H. Carlyon, L. Smith, E. F. V. Stokes, A. Smith, S. Norton, C. E. Tregoning, F. A. Cozens, E. Heard, E. F. Whitley, A. Barrett, R. Whitworth, D. At the close of the supper there was no set toast list. The MAYOR briefly gave the "Health of the Queen and rest of the Royal Family," which was heartily acknowledged by the singing of the National Anthem, and then the only other toast of the evening was also submitted from the chair—"Success to Truro Cricket Club," coupled with the names of Mr. P. Colville Smith (captain) and Mr. J. Bray (secretary).

The MAYOR said it afforded him the greatest possible pleasure to propose that toast in his capacity as Mayor of Truro, and, as such, president of the club. He hoped the rule would always be adhered to whereby the Mayor of Truro for the time being would be president of the Truro Cricket Club, and that each successive mayor would take a lively interest in the transactions of the club (applause). It was a game which brought back memories of days when they, as older men, were in their full vigour of manhood and of hope for the future, when the hope of strong intellect was backed up by the engagement in exercise calculated to give physical health and force, and to promote which nothing was calculated to be more effectual than indulgence in the grand old English game of cricket (applause). He had associated himself with the club from its formation (hear, hear), and he held it to be the duty of all on such occasions as that, to prove by their presence they felt interest in the club's success. As he moved about in other districts of the county he was glad to hear not only that they won frequently, but that the Truro Cricket Club behaved as gentlemen (applause), and he hoped they would always bear a name for gentlemanly conduct. He was sure all felt deeply sorry that Mr. P. Colville Smith was unable to be present (applause). He had telegraphed to say he was detained in London. He desired to bear testimony to the strong interest which Mr. P. C. Smith took in the club and in cricket generally, and expressed the deep obligation they were under to him for what he had done in providing the ground, contributing towards the finances of the club, and the personal part he had taken in the club's matches and its general progress (applause). They showed by their applause they appreciated all his sympathy and kindness. He was glad to say that the forthcoming bazaar in aid of the club and the Truro Philharmonic Society was to be a great reality. Promises of help and assistance were coming in from all quarters. The ladies of the city were taking up the matter in good earnest. What better guarantee could they have of a good strong healthy husband than a successful man in the cricket field? (loud laughter). The more the ladies supported the bazaar the better they would be entitled to have good, strong, promising husbands (renewed laughter) at the right time. That night Mr. Bray had given him a stronger belief in the young ladies of Truro than ever he had before (laughter). There was no citizen who had a greater appreciation of the club or heartier wish for its success than their present Mayor (applause). He gave them "Success to the club" (cheers).

Musical honours having been associated with the toast, Mr. J. Bray acknowledged it. He said he was pleased to see the supper so well attended that evening. It showed the gentlemen of Truro took an interest in the national game. They were all disappointed that Mr. P. C. Smith was unable to be present (hear, hear), but as secretary to the club, he (Mr. Bray) might explain why their subscribers had disappointed at not having had more matches on Truro ground this season. They had been anxious to cater for the citizens and the public generally and arranged 33 matches. Of these 25 were for the A and 10 for the B team. Out of the 25 of the A team, 20 were played, 14 were won, 5 were lost, and 1 was drawn (applause). Some of the most important matches were expected to take place in August, but Camborne and Liskeard both disappointed them, and the match arranged with Lord Robartes' team fell through. The B team was badly managed, having changed its captain two or three times and several of its matches did not come off. This would probably explain the reason why a few matches less were played at Truro. During the season Lieut. Smith offered a bat for the best average, and this was won by Geo. Gore with an average of 31.5. (applause). In conclusion Mr. Bray said it was a pleasure to assist a club such as theirs, especially when they had such a generous captain as Mr. P. C. Smith (applause), without whom they could not get on in Truro in cricket matters (loud applause).

The rest of the evening was devoted to conviviality, the songs being given by Messrs. N. B. Bullen, F. T. Dowsing, Harold Bullen, J. Jenkin, J. Bray, E. E. V. Stokes, T. E. Williams, A. Barrett, F. Woolf, E. Bullen, and Goodfellow, Messrs. T. Jennings and Harold Bullen sharing the accompaniments. Messrs. Bray, T. Jennings, T. E. Williams and Dowsing also gave a quartette. At the close a vote of thanks was heartily accorded the Mayor and the proceedings terminated with "Auld Lang Syne," and the National Anthem.

WATERS PLACE v. POLES.
This match was played at Poles on Saturday in glorious weather. Waters Place won the toss, and batted first on a splendid wicket. The first two men were soon sent back, but on Pratt joining Perkins the bowling was mastered, and both men were well set when through a misunderstanding the latter was unfortunately run out for a useful 14. None of the other batsmen offered much resistance to the bowling, and when the last wicket fell the total was 52. Pratt played vigorously for his 20 runs. On Poles handling the willow they found run-getting rather difficult owing to the good bowling of the brothers Salmons and Perkins, and were all disposed of for 38 runs, Mr. Hanbury being the only one to reach double figures. The visitors' second innings was very similar to their first, Perkins again playing well. The total reached 71, which left the homesters 95 to win in about an hour, and although they made a great effort to win time was called with the score standing at 64 for three wickets. Richards played grandly for his 30 not out. Waters Place thus won their last match of the season by 24 runs on the first innings. A sumptuous tea was kindly provided for the teams by Mr. Hanbury. WARE TOWN CLUB v. WARE PARISH CHURCH CHOIR.
This match was played on Saturday and resulted in a win for the Town. The Choir were considerably weaker than was anticipated, owing to several members being away for their holidays. Waller played well for the Town and compiled a useful 26. The team was eventually out for 58, which looked like a promising total for the Choir to compete against, but against the bowling of Pearce and Waller the stumps fell for less than a dozen. GREAT v. LITTLE MUNDEN.
On Wednesday a match was played between Great and Little Munden at Libury by the invitation of Mr. Jerrard, where a sumptuous spread was prepared for the visitors and friends, who spent a very pleasant afternoon. After the game finished votes of thanks were recorded to Mr. and Mrs. Jerrard for their kindness. The scoring was very low, viz., Great Munden, 1st innings, 6; 2nd innings, 43 (H. Kite 15, and G. W. Cooper 13); Little Munden, 1st innings, 28; 2nd innings, 68 for 4 wickets (A. Sinclair 21, G. Washington, not out, 10).

CRICKET.
WEST HERTS v. ABBOTS LANGLEY.
In splendid weather the West Herts Club opened their cricket season on Saturday afternoon by playing Abbots Langley, at the West Herts ground. The visitors went first to the wicket, but were dismissed for the modest total of 37. West Herts then went to the wicket, and after doing pretty much what they liked with the bowling, compiled a score nearly five times as large as that of their antagonists. The winning point was scored by Lord Essex, and Mr. Humbert contributed a score exactly equal to the entire Abbot's Langley total. The highest home score was that of J. A. Murray (44) and the highest Langley score was that of Parris (11). Abbots Langley went in a second time, and did a good deal better, having lost only one wicket when the time was up, and the score stood at 33.

MELROSE v. BERKHAMSTEAD WORKING MEN.
This match was played on the Recreation Ground, on Saturday, in beautiful weather, and resulted in a win for Melrose by 22 runs. Picton won the toss, and sent the visitors to the wickets first. Mothersole and Olliffe opened the innings, and they were opposed to the bowling of Picton (town end) and Collins. The first ball of the match beat Mothersole, and North took his place. The first ball in Picton's next over upset (Iliffe's stumps, and from the first ball of Collin's second over, North was smartly stumped by Markland—3 for 10, all the runs having been made by North. After a couple of maiden overs Collins took two wickets in one over, and the next over saw two more batsmen dismissed. Collins, after a maiden, took a couple of wickets in his last over, and Picton clean bowling Home with the first ball of his seventh over, the innings was closed. With the comparatively small total of 26 runs against them, Melrose took up the batting. At one time it looked as if there would be an exciting finish, but on Haskell joining Markham, a totally different aspect was put on the game and a total of 48 was reached, so that Melrose were once more victorious. Roxeth were again victorious on Saturday, their opponents being the Canterbury C.C., whose ground is at Kilburn. The Kilburn men first took to the wicket and compiled 31 runs. The bowling of Phillips and Iradale proving very disastrous, the former taking 5 wickets for 14 runs and the later 5 for 15 runs.
On Roxeth batting, Iredale played a fine not out innings of 27, and the total compiled was 65. The Kilburn team's second innings was not quite closed when stumps were drawn. One wicket had then to fall and the total compiled was 32—a near escape from a single innings defeat.
In the second attempt Fleet was most successful with the ball, taking two wickets for 4 runs, while Cobb took 5 wickets for 15 runs, and B. Smith 1 for 8 runs.

The Roxeth United Club have an open date so far as cricket is concerned; but the quoit team will play Mr. Watkins' team on the ground at Roxeth. More members are wanted for the quoit section, and as the subscriptions is only nominal—1s.—those wishing to join should communicate at once with the secretary, Mr. Corney.

The Wealdstone Athletic Club ground is to be open to members on Monday. The field is that just above the printing factory, and the materials can be procured at Mr. Mc Adam's up to one o'clock, but not later in the day. Members are particularly requested not to go in the long grass if they can possibly avoid it.

There is already a good membership; but others are wanted, and the secretary, Mr. W. J. Clench, would be glad at any time to receive the names of any persons wishing to join.

The run of the Harrow Cycling Club on Saturday was to Amersham. On Saturday next the Club go to Winchmore, and on Whit Monday to Great Kemble.

The Harrow Athletic and Kodak Tennis Clubs will play a tennis match on the Athletic Ground on Saturday.

Harrow and Harrow Weald met on the Harrow Weald ground on Saturday, and the visitors won by 20 runs. The home eleven went in first, Body, the first man, making the highest score—21 runs; C. Hills put together 20, and Massey made 14 before being bowled by Page. Petherbridge's average was 4 wickets for 17 runs; Kavanagh took 3 for 27; and Page 2 for 5. Kavanagh nearly succeeded in doing the hat trick, taking three wickets with four consecutive balls.
Barker was the main contributor to the 96 of Harrow, his score being 33. Petherbridge and Page were the only two others who got into double figures, making 13 and 12 respectively.

STAFFORDSHIRE CHRONICLE: SATURDAY 15 JUNE 1895-2.
STAFFORD v. WOLVERHAMPTON SCHOOL.—There were some splendid performances with the ball in the match between Stafford and Wolverhampton School at Stafford on Wednesday. Taking first turn, Stafford started badly, C. Baker being bowled by the first ball, but on Williams and T. S. Fishwick coming together a prolonged stand was made. Both played fine cricket. Fishwick scored at a rapid rate, and his hitting all round the wicket was very strong. His score included two sixes for two lofty drives over the boundary rails. While not making runs at anything like the pace of his partner, Williams gave an attractive display of batting, and his engagement promises to be one of considerable advantage to the club from a run-getting point of view. Before Fishwick was run out, the score was taken to 174, of which the outgoing batsman's share was 114. When the separation was effected three wickets fell rapidly with only an increase to the score of five runs. At the fall of the fifth wicket, Stafford adopted the closure. The School eleven made a disappointing display against Turner and Westman, they all being disposed of for the small score of 32. A feature of the innings was that all the batsmen were bowled, Westman capturing six wickets for fourteen runs and Turner four for eighteen. Score:—

SHUGBOROUGH PARK v. ABBOTS BROMLEY.—The first match of this season between these old opponents was played at Abbots Bromley on Saturday. The home team batted first, but were unable to face with any success the deliveries of C. Marsden and C. Westman, and in 10 overs and 1 ball the whole eleven were dismissed for 22 runs—nine of which were extras. No fewer than 8 batsmen had the unenviable "0" attached to their names. The visitors then compiled 76 for 7 wickets, when time was called. It must be stated that Abbots Bromley were without the services of one of their regular bowlers.

NORTHAMPTON HERALD: SATURDAY 31 AUGUST 1895-3.

NORTHAMPTON CRICKET LEAGUE.
FIRST SECTION.
Enterprise v. Excelsior.—This match, brought off on the Borough Ground on Saturday, ended in favour of the Enterprise by 22 runs, the scores reading—Enterprise 64, Excelsior 42. For the Enterprise (who batted first) A. E. Cox made 22, S. Field 21, and W. J. Bath 14. Bowling in capital style, P. Cox secured five wickets for 13 runs, while S. Kirkham took one for 8, R. Major-Lucas one for 12, F. Kendall one for 13, and C. Bent one for 19. J. M. Lucas was the only double figure score on the Excelsior side, contributing 21 (which included two fours); T. Andrews made 9. A. F. Bradford captured four wickets for nine runs, and S. Field took four for 27 each in a very fine display taking respectively two wickets in two balls. W. J. Westmoreland (one for 11) also bowled.
St. Andrew's v. Star.—A match between teams representing St. Andrew's and the Star was played on Saturday on the Borough Ground, the result being a win for the Star by nine wickets, the score reading—St. Andrew's 25, and the Star 32 for one wicket. St. Andrew's played eight men, and the Star played nine. A. Wilkinson (10) was the only one to reach double figures for St. Andrew's, who batted first. P. Cory secured four wickets for 14, and O. Squires two for 11. The most successful Star batsmen were F. Warren (15 not out) and W. Moring (13). Birch captured one wicket for 24.

SECOND SECTION.
Princess-street v. Moss Rose.—Played on the Borough Ground on Saturday. The Moss Rose sustained a crushing defeat, Princess-street making 152 for 5 wickets (innings declared closed) as against 16. C. Penn compiled 37 and W. Rogers 22 for Princess-street. W. Kent took two for 16, H. Bailey two for 25, and A. Garrard one for 32. No player of the Moss Rose team was able to reach double figures, thanks to the fine bowling of Timms, who dismissed six men for eight runs, and Penn, who captured four wickets for six.
Dodridge v. Excelsior Albions.—These teams met on the Borough Ground on Saturday afternoon, and a one-sided match resulted in an easy victory for the Dodridge team. Owing to the destructive bowling of J. Smith (five wickets for 31), H. Rowe (three for six), W. Lack (one for five), and A. Sutton (one for 20), the Albions were only able to reach 77, towards which B. Kendall scored 32 and T. Barry 12. C. Sutton (44 not out) and A. Sutton (39 not out) were the two first to bat for Dodridge, and having 86 to their credit without the loss of a single wicket they closed their innings.

THIRD SECTION.
St. Giles' v. Wesleyan Star.—This match was decided on the Borough Ground on Saturday, the scores being Wesleyan Star 105, St. Giles' 35. For the winning side J. Lewis (24), Turk (16 not out), F. Osborn (16), and Barrows (16) were the principal scorers. The bowling honours were divided between A. Lloyd (three for 19), R. Betts (three for 21), P. W. Sharman (two for 11), and P. Millward (two for 23). P. W. Sharman (13) was the only double figure scorer for St. Giles'. J. Lewis and H. Martin each took five wickets for 16 runs.
Blue Shield v. Y.M.C.A.—Played on the Borough Ground on Saturday. The Blue Shield won easily, compiling 90 against 13 made by the Y.M.C.A. For the winners W. Newcome scored 30 not out, W. Shaw 20, and O. Sheehan 12. Cox secured five wickets, Harris and Tyler two each, and Laland one. E. Crater succeeded in taking five wickets for four runs and O. Sheehan five for seven for the Blue Shield.
St. Lawrence's v. Manfield's O.C.—This match was played on the Borough Ground on Saturday, when St. Lawrence's won very easily by 97 runs. St. Lawrence's, batting first, scored 135 runs for eight wickets, and then declared their innings closed. Vickers (29), Wainwright (26), Talbot and Barcock, not out with 25 and 13 respectively, were chief scorers, Warren (three wickets), Wright and Harding (two each) doing best for Manfield's in the bowling. On Manfield's going in to bat they were dismissed for 39, F. Pulley (7) and E. Wright (10) being chief scorers, W. White (four for 15) and W. Barcock (three for 21) bowling for St. Lawrence's. Manfield's were unfortunate in losing three wickets by batsmen being run out.
Wootton v. St. Mary's.—This match was played in Cow Meadow on Saturday. Scores: Wootton 39, St. Mary's 29.
Kettering Wanderers beat Kettering Wesleyans on Saturday by 15 runs. Wanderers, 129; Wesleyans, 113.
Kettering Church Institute 2nd defeated Kettering Working Men's Club 2nd on Saturday by 21 runs. K.C.I., 41; K.W.M.C., 20 and 55 for nine wickets.
Kettering Fuller beat Brigstock on Saturday by 12 runs. Fuller, 44; Brigstock, 32.
Kettering Church Institute defeated Kettering Working Men's Club on the North Park on Saturday afternoon by four wickets and 105 runs. The Institute, for six wickets, compiled 165, when they closed their innings, the highest scorers being Hoare (43), Piggott (31), Cole (not out 32), and Maycock (24). The Working Men's Club were all dismissed for 62 (Chaplin 17).
Princess-street Excelsior Reserves v. Queen's-road.—This match was played on the Racecourse on Saturday, and, after an exciting finish, ended in a victory for Princess-street by 4 runs. Scores: Princess-street Excelsior Reserves, 18; Queen's-road, 14.
Empire Star v. Lifeboat.—This match was played in the Cow Meadow on Saturday, and ended in a victory for the former by 29 runs. The chief scorers for the Empire Star were Oldham (16) and Kent (10). The bowling honours were shared by Kent (five wickets for 5 runs, including hat trick) and Miles (five for 5). Scores: Empire Star C.C., 41; Lifeboat, 14.
Victoria-road v. Cogenhoe.—Played at Cogenhoe on Saturday last, and won by the former by 7 runs. Scores: Victoria-road, 35 (T. Garlick 10, A. Scouse 7); and Cogenhoe, 28. T. Garlick took four wickets for 4 runs, P. Roberts four for 12, P. Cooke one for 9.

Henley C.C. v. Grafton Arms.—Played at Paulins-holme Meadow, the former winning by 31 runs. Scores: Henley, 61; Grafton Arms, 30.
Queen's Arms, Raglan-street, v. Lord Raglan.—The return match was played on the Racecourse on Saturday, and resulted in the victory of the former by seven runs—69 to 62. Billingham made 24, Malspas 13, and S. Braines 9 for the winners, Robinson taking four and S. Braines three wickets respectively. For the losers Boswell made 15, Ward 10, and Bunting 9, Ward taking the bowling honours with six wickets, and Purser three.
Hardingstone v. Borough Employees.—Played at Hardingstone, and won by Hardingstone by 49 runs. Scores: Hardingstone, 71; Borough Employees, 22.
St. Sepulchre's Bible Class v. Princess-street X.L.C.R.—Played on the Racecourse on Saturday, and won by St. Sepulchre's by 69 runs. St. Sepulchre's batted first, and scored 106 for the loss of seven wickets, and then declared their innings closed. Princess-street were all out for 37. For the winners R. Osborn (56 not out) and W. Pinkard (17) batted well, whilst in the bowling J. Dickenson took three wickets for 2, A. Bradford three for 8, W. Pinkard one for 4, and H. Botterill one for 7.
Alcombe Rovers v. Uppingham Rovers.—Played on the Racecourse on Saturday, and ended in a win for the former by 17 runs. Scores: 30 and 13.
St. Sepulchre's Choir C.C. v. Kingsley Park Wesleyans.—Played on the Racecourse on Saturday, and won by St. Sepulchre's by 47 runs. Scores: St. Sepulchre's, 62 (Vaughan 16 and Eales 12); Kingsley Park Wesleyans, 15.
Wellingborough Congregational v. Northampton College-street.—These teams met at Wellingborough on Saturday, a low-scoring match ending in a victory for College-street by 24 runs. The visitors batted first, but their total was only the very moderate one of 46 (W. Crack 19, Arthur Robinson 11). J. W. Dyson took four wickets for 8, and A. Birkett six for 23. Wellingborough opened with W. Kerry and G. H. Woolston; but Kerry was sent back by the first ball delivered, and Woolston was dismissed for a cypher. J. W. Dyson and A. Birkett made a short stand, taking the score to 13 before Birkett was caught. After his departure no one was able to stay against the bowling of A. J. Robinson, whose analysis was as follows:—10.1 overs, 7 maidens, 3 runs, 7 wickets. E. Williams secured the other three wickets for 15, and the total only reached 22 (J. W. Dyson 10).
St. Paul's v. Catholic Alma.—Played on the Racecourse on Saturday, and won by St. Paul's by 12 runs. R. Derby (13 not out) was the chief scorer. E. H. Chater (six wickets for 13) and W. Care (four wickets for 20) did good work with the ball. H. Forrest (7) was the highest scorer for the losers. Scores: St. Paul's, 46; Catholic Alma, 34.
Teachers v. Wollaston.—Played at Franklin's Gardens on Saturday last, and, strange to say, says a correspondent, ended in a similar way to one of the Senior League matches, of which the opinion of the M.C.C. was taken. This high authority on cricket decided that the League match was a draw, so this match must be called the same. Wollaston won the toss, and, batting first, were disposed of for 71. G. Lacy (16), H. Lucy (14), and A. Horton (10) were the chief scorers. For the Teachers Warner bowled well, taking three wickets for 6 runs; Grant obtained five wickets for 16 runs, and Sheard and Poole each captured a wicket. The Teachers then batted, and, thanks to vigorous hitting on the part of Poole (19 not out), Turner (16), and Mackaness (15), soon obtained the same number of runs as Wollaston, viz., 71, for four wickets, when time was called. Hart obtained two wickets for Wollaston and Brown one.
A number of other matches appeared in our first edition.
Post Office v. Y.M.C.A.—Played on the Borough Ground on August 22, and won by the Post Office by 56 runs. The Y.M.C.A., batting first, made 70, Parkin 32 and Turner 27. Bowling for the Post Office, H. Toyer took three wickets for one run, S. Field four for 16, and T. Pettit three for 30. The Post Office made 126. For the winners S. Field played brilliantly for 84, the other principal scorers being Carter 11 and Margetts 7.
Britannia Rovers v. Lily of the Valley.—Played on the Racecourse on Saturday, and won by the former by 99 runs. Scores: Britannia, 139 (T. Freeman 42 not out); Lily of the Valley, 40.

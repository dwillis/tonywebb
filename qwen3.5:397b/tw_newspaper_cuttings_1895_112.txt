HERTFORDSHIRE EXPRESS : SATURDAY 1 JUNE 1895-1.
PROSPECTS OF THE LOCAL CRICKET SEASON.—(Concluded.)
The second portion of our annual article under the above heading, in which it is our custom to review briefly the arrangements of so many of the local clubs as favour us with the necessary particulars, must in this instance be the last. It is possible that clubs have been later than usual in completing their fixtures for the season and so could not accede to our request for information; but, whatever the reason, information has come to hand very slowly and although the season is several weeks old we even now lack particulars of the programmes of several clubs which should have had a place in this review. Among these, as usual, is the Biggleswade C.C., whose card we never remember receiving except on one occasion—during the brief secretaryship of the Rev. C. C. Ewbank.
ROYSTON C.C.
We are glad to chronicle the revival of this club, after the lapse of one season. Not only has the club been set going again, but it starts with excellent prospects of success. Mr. Walter C. Titchmarsh has resumed the office of hon. secretary and its affairs could not be entrusted to better hands. Mr. A. J. Jacklin, who for several seasons before the dissolution of the club was of the greatest service to it both as batsman and bowler, has been elected captain, and the committee is a representative one. We learn from Mr. Titchmarsh that one or two fresh players will be available, with most of those who were in the eleven two seasons back. A new pavilion erected on the Heath will be a great convenience. The season opened in auspicious fashion with the easy defeat of the Cambridge Nihilists C.C., Royston scoring 161 for six wickets to the 93 of their opponents. On May 28 the club was to meet an eleven of the 4th Battalion Beds. Regiment, and for the remainder of the season the programme is as follows:—

BISHOP STORTFORD C.C.
This is another club which, like West Herts, gets through a lengthy list of good matches in the course of the season. The Stortford cricket week is generally productive of long scoring and well-fought games, and this year's should prove no exception to the rule, the club's opponents being of no mean calibre. As usual, the Stortford ground will be the scene of the Hertfordshire club's home encounter with Norfolk. BEDFORD TOWN C.C.
A correspondent writes:—"The talent available in the batting department is expected to be much the same as last year, and may be considered fairly strong. It is feared that the bowling will be somewhat weak but the Committee hope to rely upon the assistance of W. F. Brown in some matches, while F. W. Potter and H. W. Oates ought to prove of great service to the team, but the loss of F. H. Edwards will be keenly felt. There is no lack of wicket keepers, and K. F. Rowe will give the club his assistance. P. T. Ball and L. G. Brown will be available on occasion. Games are to be arranged every Thursday afternoon for the benefit of any who care to turn up. The 'A' team will this season be very properly chosen from members who do not play regularly for the first eleven, this giving opportunity for more members than usual to get games."

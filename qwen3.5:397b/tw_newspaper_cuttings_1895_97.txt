THE HEREFORDSHIRE CLUB AND GROUND CRICKET WEEK.
On Monday next the County Cricket Club opens, with a two days' match against the M.C.C., what should prove to be a most successful week's cricket. We never remember seeing a stronger programme provided for lovers of the game than that which Messrs. W. T. Williams and C. H. Ware have arranged. Under their able generalship, the M.C.C. match has, during the last year or so, once more become an important feature of the Hereford cricket season, and in the coming week it is backed up by two capital county matches against the cricketing strength of such shires as Monmouthshire and Breconshire. A tent will be erected on the ground for the benefit of members and their friends, and tea will be provided on the afternoon of each day. Granted fine weather, some really excellent cricket must be witnessed, as Herefordshire can put into the field her strongest eleven, which will be selected from the following:—

HEREFORDSHIRE CLUB AND GROUND v LEOMINSTER.
This match was played on Widemarsh Common, Hereford, on Saturday, and resulted in the defeat of the visiting team by 102 runs. Moore and Sunderland batted well for Leominster, the former contributing 31 and the latter 21. Bert Sharp made the highest score on the Hereford side, putting together 35.

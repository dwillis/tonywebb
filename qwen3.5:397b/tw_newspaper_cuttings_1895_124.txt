WATFORD OBSERVER: SATURDAY 10 AUGUST 1895-4.

BUSHEY CRICKET WEEK.

BUSHEY v. VAMPIRES.

The cricket week which opened at Bushey on Monday has been full of interest, and a good number of spectators have daily been attracted to the grounds at the Manor House where the matches have been played on a fine pitch. The Vampires came first to oppose the locals, and although rain prevented the completion of the visitors innings, the game looked much in favour of Bushey. The feature of the match was the splendid innings of H. H. Harford who punished the bowling severely, and sent one ball over the hedge. He compiled 51 runs. His partnership with Green produced 66, and with Ward who was run out for 26, another 49. Barker batted in fine form. The Vampires made 68 for the loss of four wickets, which were taken by Ward, when rain stopped play. F. Turner batted well for his 27. Score:—

BUSHEY v. LEINSTER.

On Tuesday the team was rather badly beaten by Leinster, a smart lot of Irishmen on tour. Going first to the wicket, the visitors did not make a promising start, Keatinge and Lambert being sent back for a single run apiece. The latter was beautifully caught by Ward the ball nearly touching the ground. Jameson and Perrott made a fine stand, putting on 75 between them, and Foxall added 18, when he was caught by Sargent. Bushey's innings opened badly, and none of the locals got into second figures with the exception of Green and Harford, who made a creditable stand. The total was 61 against 147. Score:—

BUSHEY v. H. J. H. BLADON'S XI.

On Wednesday the team made a good fight against H. J. H. Bladon's XI.; both sides passed the century. Cruise opened the innings for the visitors with Barker, but when he had knocked up a dozen runs he was struck on the head and retired for a little while. On resuming, he continued to play good cricket and carried his score to 34 when he was given not lbw. Both the Nelsons played a good game, and Barker was not out for 93. The team totalled 182. Bushey made a poor start, Fastnedge being run out for a single run. Keeling made 15, and 26 Green and Sargent got together and carried the score to 101. The team was sent back for 110, having failed to reach their rivals' total by 18. H. J. H. Illadon's XI. went in again and made 90 for the loss of six wickets. Score:—

KINGS LANGLEY v. ST. GILES-IN-THE-FIELDS.

This whole-day match was played at Kings Langley on Monday, but the weather proved very unfavourable. The visitors' team included the old Varsity player Hine-Haycock. Farr was in grand form with the ball, and Hammond and Hine-Haycock were the only batsmen to reach double figures. The latter was dismissed by a very fine catch made by Walker at third man. Farr took eight wickets for 17 runs, and Lane two for 18. On going to the wickets the home team knocked off the runs before the fall of a wicket, Farr again distinguishing himself and being well supported by Betts. Williams also played a good innings.

WORCESTER GRAMMAR SCHOOL v. LYTTELTON GRAMMAR SCHOOL.—Played at Firs Meadow on Saturday. Lyttelton batted first but could do little against the bowling of Thorp and Spencer, the whole side being dismissed for 13. Spencer took three wickets for 7 runs, bowling six maidens, while Thorp took five wickets for 6 runs. Worcester Grammar School, on going in, passed the Lyttelton score with two wickets down. Davis and Willcock hit well, the former making 15, including a 5 and a 4, and the latter 12. The next man (Thorp) made top score (24), which included some fine cuts. The innings closed for 87. Blossome, who bowled very well, secured six wickets. On going in a second time Lyttelton did slightly better, but only managed to make 18. Willcock took five wickets for 6 runs and Thorp four for 6. Worcester Grammar School thus winning by an innings and 56 runs. Score :—

WORCESTER GRAMMAR SCHOOL v. KIDDERMINSTER GRAMMAR SCHOOL.—Played at Kidderminster, on Wednesday. Although the wicket was more in favour of the batsmen, being hard with only the grass wetted, the bowlers on both sides were most prominent. Kidderminster batted first, but were all dismissed for 8. Thorp took five wickets for 2 runs, and Spencer five wickets for 4 runs. The latter also accomplished the "hat trick." Worcester Grammar School made a better display with the bat than their rivals, but the really excellent bowling of Tye and Woodward prevented a large score. Both bowled very fast and kept good length. This is the first defeat that Kidderminster have received for a long time, and the first time they have succumbed to Worcester Grammar School in any sport.

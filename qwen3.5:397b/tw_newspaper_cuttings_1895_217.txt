STAFFORDSHIRE SENTINEL: SATURDAY 24 AUGUST 1895 - 2

BASFORD v. KEELE.
Keele visited Basford this afternoon to play off the return encounter with the team that beat them decisively in July. The weather was beautifully fine, but the wicket was treacherous, and having won the toss the home captain put his opponents in. A. Walklate and W. Beeston opened the game to the bowling of J. G. Hughes and G. Shrubsole. One run was knocked by the first-named batsman, off Hughes, when he was bowled by the last ball of the over, Beeston having added 1 more run. N. A. Coghill came in and matters improved, 24 being reached before the in-coming batsman was caught at mid-on by Rev. A. Paice. Beeston was caught by Sutton at mid-off without addition, and I. Taylor, who had succeeded Coghill, fell at 26. G. Sutton and the Rev. F. C. Stainer became associated, but the latter had his middle peg shot down without mounting. F. A. Winstanley joined Sutton, who was caught by Shrubsole behind off a peculiar stroke. A Capper did not stay long, Shrubsole accounting for him when he had made a single. F. J. Bamford and J. Taylor then got together, the former being bowled by Shrubsole at 44. W. Bowers whipped in and got a couple, when he was smartly caught by A. D. Stewart, the innings realising 47 runs and having lasted an hour. Shrubsole took five wickets for 11 and Hughes five for 35.
The Basford innings was commenced at 4 35 by the brothers Stewart, Archie taking the first over from Walklate. A disastrous start was made, the batsman in cutting the bowler's third delivery being taken at point by Coghill. A. B. Walmsley came in, and he opened the scoring by smacking the Silverdale man to the boundary for a couple. Beeston bowled at the other end, and Stewart was balled without scoring, two wickets thus falling for 3 runs. A. English took his place. He and his partner put a more favourable aspect on the game. A slight shower came on but quickly cleared off, and no stoppage was effected. Only 6 was reached when English was bowled by Beeston. Before the next batsman, G. Shrubsole, could get to the wicket, the rain again descended, this time heavily, and the players had to seek shelter. After twelve minutes' rest a resumption was made, sawdust being requisitioned. Walmsley, having knocked a snaz he went in, was bowled without any addition to the total. Shrubsole and the Rev. Mr. Paice played well, the former hitting very freely before getting leg-before. The last wicket had put on 25 runs. Sutton ran himself out, and Paice was bowled soon after. An interesting encounter was won by the home-sters on the eighth wicket.

NANTWICH v. BIGNALL END.
(LEAGUE.—2ND DIVISION.)
These teams contested the championship of the Second League at Nantwich in beautiful weather on a splendid wicket. Bignall End have 24 points, and this was their final match. Nantwich, with 21 points, have yet to meet Leek Church and Tunstall. The home team won the toss, and sent in Shenton and Hinde. A leg bye resulted off Burgess's opening delivery, with another bye from the next ball in Dale's over. Each batsman scored a single. Hinde drove nicely to the boundary for 4. Runs came freely, Hinde cutting for a boundary, followed by a smart stroke for 3. Thirty runs were put on in as many minutes. Statham relieved Burgess, and in the first over disposed of Hinde for a well earned 22, which included three boundaries. (One for 55.) Edwards joined Shenton, but the latter made all the runs, despite frequent changes of bowling. At 59 for one wicket Nantwich entertained strong hopes of wiping out the heavy defeat received at Audley. The visitors fielded well. Edwards ultimately settled down to some steady batting, but gave a high ball, which was caught. (Two for 70.) Shenton, with a boundary, recorded his 50 and sent up the century for his side at the same time, for which he was loudly applauded. Hereabouts the game was interrupted by rain.

TRENTHAM v. BARLASTON.
This match was played at Trentham this afternoon before a large number of spectators. The refreshing rain of yesterday had rendered the ground in splendid condition, and as usual the home team had to take the field. Harleston opened their innings with A. Meare and C. Menkin, the bowling being shared by Moore and Blair. This partnership was but short, as Moore was bowling a very good ball, and in his second over he took Meare for 1. Menkin was bine joined by Stevenson; he likewise met the same fate off Blair for a single also. Again Menkin had Sutton as a partner, but runs came in very slowly, the home fielding well, and gave very few chances. About twenty minutes play saw the third wickets fall for 15, Menkin being taken by Moore. This vacancy was filled by H. Waltho. Both batsmen played a very careful game, but missed no chances, and during the partnership they succeeded in taking several boundaries, which helped up the score nicely. Waltho, however, stayed but for a short time, he being caught and bowled by Moore at this point. The home-sters seemed to be revenging themselves for their defeat at last Saturday, Greentree being caught by Collins off Moore from his first ball, this being 5 for 23.

NEWCASTLE v. WHITMORE.
Played on the ground of the former this afternoon. Newcastle won the toss, and sent in Dean and J. Booth; the Whitmore attack being opened by Smith from the factory end. The scoring was opened in the third over, when Booth snicked Smith for a single, and matters continued slow until Dean made a capital hit for 4. In the next quarter of an hour 7 only were knocked up, and when the board showed 18, Saunders went on vice Tidderwell in the visitors bowling department. The Newcastle wickets fell rather quickly, the only player to make a stand up to the time 40 was telegraphed being J. Booth, who played a steady game, and bid fair to make a good score. Saunders's slow bowling had been doing all the execution, but Calow hit him for 3 in his first stroke. Rain interfered with the play, and shortly after the resumption J. Booth was bowled for 28, after seeing the fall of seven of his companions' wickets. Newcastle innings closed for 89 exactly at 5 30 p.m.

CLOUGH HALL v. BURSLEM.
(LEAGUE—2ND DIVISION.)
This League fixture was down to be played this afternoon. However, at four o'clock no appearance was made by Burslem, yet after a stormy morning the weather was fine and the ground was in good condition for players.

AUDLEY v. LEEK CHURCH.
(LEAGUE.—2ND DIVISION.)
This match came off at Audley this afternoon in fine weather and on a splendid wicket at the Kent Hills ground. The visitors won the toss and sent Chapman and Wood to the wickets, who had to face the bowling of Hemshall and George Timmis. Audley were minus four of their first men, and had to fill up with second eleven men. The innings opened out very nicely, and runs were scored rather freely. Chapman contributed 9 and Wood 6 before they returned to the pavilion. Byrne on going to the wickets played in dashing style, and by hits of three singles, four 2's, two 3's, two 4's, and one 6 he made 31 runs, after which he was caught by Leek off Hemshall. Haines, Milwood, and Ball were not so fortunate in playing the slow bowling.

BUCKNALL CHURCH v. TUNSTALL II.
(LEAGUE—2ND DIVISION.)
These teams met at Bucknall this afternoon. Bucknall won the toss, and sent in T. Edge and R. Deaville, to the bowling of the brothers Lockett. In attempting a short run from the first ball, Edge was splendidly run out by Rhodes. This let in Herriford, but two overs later Deaville was bowled by a good ball, the total being 3. A. Burdisford followed, but A. Lockett, who was bowling a good length, soon shattered his wickets. Disasters continued, and Herriford, Williams, and Egerton were out with the score standing at 10. None of the batsmen so far had been able to play the bowling. Lowe relieved the monotony by hitting A. Lockett to leg for 2, but the bowler had his revenge in the next over. A little later Toothkinson dismissed Capewell by a splendid catch. (Seven for 12.) The three last wickets only added 3 runs. The bowling analysis was very good, A. Lockett securing five wickets for 2, and S. Lockett four wickets for 11.

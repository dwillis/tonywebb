CHESHIRE COUNTY NEWS: FRIDAY 2 AUGUST 1895-2

SATURDAY'S MATCHES.
Castleton v. Stockport.
This match should have been played at Castleton, but owing to the ground being unfit caused through the heavy rains it was cancelled. Next week the Stockport team journey to Bolton, to try conclusions with the town club.

STOCKPORT AND DISTRICT SHIELD COMPETITION.
FINAL TIE.
STOCKPORT CONGREGATIONAL v. REDDISH ST. ELIZABETH'S.-This interesting event, for a beautiful shield presented by Mr. Sydney Hollins, was commenced on the football ground adjoining the Stockport Cricket Ground on Saturday afternoon. The pitch, which had been prepared for the occasion, was saturated by the rain, and all in favour of the bowlers. The Congregational have vanquished the Lads' Club and St. Peter's, whilst for St. Elizabeth's position they have had to meet and defeat the Lads' Club 2nd and last year's winners, Hanover. The Congregational batted first, sending in Dowbiggin and S. Jebson, they having to face the bowling of Hardman and Hunt. Both batsmen hit well. The innings only realised 49. Reddish started with Hardman and Dyson, Lee and Hardy looking after the howling. Runs came pretty freely. Score:-Congregational: Dowbiggin b Hunt 6, S. Jebson b Hardman 4, Jebson run out 4, Hardy b Hunt 6, Cheetham b Hardman 1, Beaty c Saxon b Hardman 2, Lee b Hardman 1, Ridley b Hardman 0, Hague b Hardman 1, Barratt not out 6, Hardy b Hunt 6, extras 2; total 49. Reddish St. Elizabeth's: Hardman b Hardy 5, Dyson lbw b Lee 20, Grimshaw c Hague b Lee 0, Miller b Lee 5, Raynor c Barratt b Lee 0, Hunt c Jebson b Hardy 2, Mason b Dowbiggin 3, Shawcross not out 1, Burrows not out 3, Saxon and Lomax did not bat, extras 2; total for seven wickets 42.

SIDMOUTH v. EALING.
This two days' match was concluded yesterday. Overnight Sidmouth had made 367 in their first innings, and Ealing had lost two wickets for two runs. On resuming the visitors raised the total to 216. The principal feature of the innings was the 101 by Conry, who was unfortunately run out, and the fine defence of McGregor for 58. On going in a second time they did not do quite so well, and were ultimately beaten by an innings and two runs. The Ealing scores were as follows :—

Killerton v. Exe Valley II.—Played at Killerton Park, and ended in a easy win for the home team. Scores:—Exe valley, 20; Killerton, 52 for 8 wickets. Wright and Gould bowled well for Killerton, the former also batted well for 18.

Devon County Asylum v. Mr. Newbery's XI.—This match was played at Exminster on Saturday, and ended in a win for the Asylum by the narrow margin of 4 runs. Scores:—Devon County Asylum, 32 and 60; Mr. Newbery's XI., 52 and 36.

Exmouth v. Mr. W. Opleton's Team.—This two days' match was commenced at Exmouth yesterday. The visitors were disposed of for 174 runs, the highest scorer being H. Andrew (run out) 51. Deane took seven wickets. At the close of play last evening, Exmouth had made 210 for two wickets down, J. S. G. Grenfell 30, R. O. Groves 80; J. A. Gibb (not out) 78.

Wivelscombe v. Wellington (Home Team).—This match was played at Wivelscombe on Saturday, and ended in a win for Wivelscombe by 20 runs. The best scorers for the winners were H. L. Hodges 25 (not out) and A. D. Hickley 7. For the losers, T. S. Page 12, and W. H. Brown 6. Score:—Wivelscombe, 62; Wellington, 42.

Bradfield v. Ashill.—This return match was played on the ground of the former, and won easily by the home team by 10 wickets. H. Lane made 10 and 13 for Ashill, and played exceedingly good cricket, helping his side considerably, and F. Mathews and W. Miller were of great assistance to the winners. Ashill, 20 and 30; Bradfield, 66 and 1 for no wicket.

Bridge Mills (Silverton) v. Uffculme.—Played on the ground of the former on Saturday, and won by Uffculme by 14 runs. The Rev. J F Pownig bowled dexterously, taking six wickets for 17 runs, and Ponsford the remaining four for 10 runs. Frank Welland, for Uffculme, showed brilliant style for 28. Pottsford played cautious cricket. Foster was neatly stumped. Scores:—Uffculme, First innings, 64; second innings, 36 for seven wickets; Bridge Mills, 40.

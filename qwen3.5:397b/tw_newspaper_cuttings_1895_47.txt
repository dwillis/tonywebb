STOCKPORT v. WERNETH.
Stockport met Werneth, on the ground of the
latter, for the first time this season before a
grand gate of over two thousand spectators, and in
charming weather. Both teams were well re-
presented. Werneth were minus the services
of Wharmby, one of their pros, but had another,
of the Manchester staff and late of Stockport,
assisting them, and who did very well
too, taking five wickets for just over nine runs a
piece. The Werneth captain being fortunate
in winning the toss sent in Redfern and Haggas to
face the bowling of Brown and Fred Torkington.
Both men scored a boundary each off Torkington,
but the score had only reached 22 when Haggas was
grandly caught by Harry Royle at square leg,
22-1-0. Hall, the popular Werneth pro, came
next, but after scoring a single he was magnificently
caught at slip by F. Tyler, 26-2-1. Heyworth
followed in and here some good cricket was seen.
Both men played well, though Brown troubled them
with his trickiness with the ball. At 46 a change
of bowling was tried, F. Tyler going on for F.
Torkington. The separation, however, came from
the other end, Sam Brown bowling Heyworth with
a grand ball, after that player had scored 14 in
nice style. 53-3-4. Wickets after this fell fast,
Sam Brown being almost unplayable, the
whole side being dismissed for the
not over grand score of 98. This was due to the
splendid bowling of Sam, whose analysis read—21-1
overs for 8 maidens 25 runs and 7 wickets.
The fielding of the Stockport men was also very
good, all the catches that were took being very
fine. Stockport opened their venture by sending
in W. Hartley and J. Bates to the bowling of Hall
and Brookes. The start was at once disastrous,
losing Bates and Arthur Torkington with only 15
runs on the board. Brown and W. Hartley soon
after fell victims, and four good wickets were down
for 27 runs, but when Harold Hartley and Disney
got together a different complexion was put on the
game, and they treated the bowling with the
utmost confidence, especially the former,
the fifth wicket falling at 55, Disney being
bowled. Fred Long next joined Hartley, who
was playing a splendid game, and he also found a
good partner in Long, who also played very well.
Hartley, after playing a splendid game, was most
unfortunate in playing on. After making 27 he was
loudly cheered on returning, the sixth wicket thus
falling at 89. Little needs for further comment
except the batting of Fred Long and Frank Tyle,
the former scoring 14 and the latter 12, not out; the
innings closing for 110—Stockport winning by just
a dozen. It was a grand match, and was fought
out right to the end very determinedly. Great
praise is due to Harold Hartley, Fred Long, and
Frank Tyler for the splendid way they kept their
wickets up against the fine bowling of the tricky
Hall and Brookes; and Stockport have every reason
to be proud of their handsome victory, every man
being a thorough trier. Below is appended full
score and analysis.

STOCKPORT v. WERNETH (2nd ELEVEN).—These
two old rivals met for the first time this season at
Gale Green Park, in glorious weather, and before a
fair attendance. The visitors, winning the toss,
decided to bat a little after three o'clock, to the
bowling of Jones andport' euhmon. Jackson opened
splendidly with a four, but on the whole runs came
slowly, eight wickets being down for 93, when
Marsland and Watson became partners and a
splendid stand was made, the score being taken to
170 before either were separated, the score being
still the same when the innings closed. After the
usual interval Stockport commenced to bat at a
quarter past five, with a rather formidable task before
them. May soon set to work in earnest. The first
wicket compiled 47 towards the number, May, the
retiring batsman, accounting for 43 of these. F.
Selby, the new man in, seemed rather uncomfortable
at first, but he was getting more at home when he
lost the services of Dixon, leading 73-2. W. E.
Marshall joined Selby with almost a hundred runs
wanting, and the hand of the clock pointing to six.
They had much to do, but the batsmen soon set
to work, Marshall hitting four 4's in one over. Ex-
citement ran high as time crept on. The spectators
became quite riveted to the game, especially the
junior portion, who were watching with delight,
as each batsman sent the leather to the boundary.
It was a treat to watch Marshall play each fresh
bowler. Nothing seemed to come amiss, but
an ather wicket full before the required runs were
reached, Selby being cleaned bowled by the
captain for a very useful 46. With ten minutes
left to get the remaining nine runs, Jones joined
Marshall, the latter soon making the winning hit
with a boundary. He was shortly afterwards bowled
for a grand 57, comprising 15 fours and 4 twos.
Time arrived when the fifth wicket fell, Stockports
being victorious by 11 runs and five wickets.
Werneth was very strongly represented. This was
their first defeat of the season, but the homesters
have to come yet. Next week Stockport's
2nd journey to Cheetham Hill, and it
is to be hoped the team will be as suc-
cessful as they were on Saturday. Score:—

GREAT MOON v. HAZEL GROVE.—At Mile End.

26 runs. The home team, who batted first, were

for six runs, but 30 runs were made for the fourth
wicket; the eighth wicket fell for 59 runs, and the

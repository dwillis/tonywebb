NEWCASTLE DAILY JOURNAL: THURSDAY 29 AUGUST 1895-2.

CRICKET.
NOTICE TO CRICKETERS.
We shall be glad to receive the names and addresses of club secretaries throughout Northumberland as early as possible, in order that the resolution passed at the recent County Club meeting held in Newcastle may be sent to them. We shall also be glad to receive the name of any one willing to subscribe to the County Club.
MR CLAYTON'S XI. v. MR R. G. MORTIMER'S XI.
To-day Mr Clayton's eleven continue their tour by playing a team raised by Mr R G Mortimer. The match extends over to-morrow, and a start will be made at 11 o'clock this morning. The teams are:-
Mr Clayton's XI: F G H Clayton, A T B Dunn, W B Anderson, H Philipson, R N Boworth-Smith, M G Barlow, J M Quinton, C G Pope, K A Woodward, H D Corbet, and N Malcolmson.
Mr Mortimer's XI: R G Mortimer, J Hansell, C Latimer, C M Henderson, R B Hoare, G Garnett, T Gowans, H J Salmon, C F Blair, with Griffin and another.
MR A BROWNE'S XI. v. Mr H J SALMON'S XI.
Our readers will remember that a short time ago "Custos" stated that the meeting between the teams of Mr A Browne of Doxford Hall and Mr H J Salmon of Newcastle at the Constabulary Ground on Monday and Tuesday, September 2nd and 3rd, would provide two days really first-class cricket. At that time the announcement was made the brothers L C H and R C N Pauncefot, as well as C B Fry, the famous Oxford triple Blue, were expected to play in the match; but, unfortunately, none of these celebrated cricketers can come north, and, consequently, Mr Salmon has been more or less compelled to confine himself to local talent. Still, from the names given below, it will be seen that his team is a fairly strong one, in both batting and bowling.
Mr Browne, on the other hand, has spread his net over a wider area, and a better eleven than he is bringing has not been seen on Tyneside, for it includes Wainwright, the Yorkshire County bowler, and Pougher of Leicestershire, whose performances, especially against Surrey, stamp him as one of the best bowlers of the present day. Besides these, Pickett and F G Bull of Essex are included in the team, so that, if Mr Salmon wins, his men will have to surpass all their previous performances. The teams are as follows:-
Mr Browne's XI: A Browne (captain), C Perkins, S Marshall, A R Fuller, F G Bull, G Elser, Lambert, Pickett, Wainwright, Pougher, and another (probably Briggs of Lancashire).
Mr Salmon's XI: H J Salmon (captain), G A T Salmon, C O Hall, J S Brown, S C Ferguson, A T B Dunn, C F Blair, H Haslam, F G H Clayton, G Simpson, and the Rev E W R Walters.
MR A. C. MACLAREN'S AVERAGE.
Including the innings completed on Tuesday morning. A. Maclaren's batting average is a fraction over 54, and he thus runs a close race with "W. G.," who failed to score for Gloucestershire on Monday. The figures are: W. G. Grace, 54.20; A. C. Maclaren, 54.01.
MR BEWICKE'S XI. v. MR F C R CLAYTON'S XI.
(By "Custos.")
The match between these teams was resumed at the Close House ground, yesterday, but on account of the rain no definite issue was arrived at. Though the early part of the day was fine, heavy showers fell at intervals throughout the afternoon, driving the players from the field, and ultimately causing the game to be abandoned as a draw, when, in their second innings, Mr Bewicke's team had scored 35 for one wicket. Mr Clayton's eleven were the greater sufferers from the wet weather, their bowlers being thus severely handicapped; though at the finish the rain undoubtedly saved them from defeat. The change from slow to a fast wicket proved too much for most of them, who often played late at Guttridge's fast deliveries, and that bowler captured six of their wickets for 32 runs; while Simpson's four cost 33. For the home side Walters again batted well.
Prompt to time Anderson resumed bowling from the railway end, Boworth-Smith being the other bowler. After four had been scored Guttridge was nearly taken by Pope as extra-cover off Boworth-Smith. Farmer followed in but had only made three when he put one up in the slips which Woodward took. Walters gave Anderson a hot catch at mid-off, which was dropped, and then, with an off drive to the boundary, he sent up the "hundred." His partner, Salmon, opened with a lofty 2 through the slips, and, after saluting another single, was caught at long on by Quinton off the lefthander. C. Perkins filled the vacancy, but lost Walters, who was taken at short slip by Dunn; and C Bewicke, the last man, came in. The end had now arrived, however, as in attempting a short run the home captain was out, the innings realising 112, or 25 behind the score of their opponents.
Dunn and Philipson began Mr Clayton's second innings, to the bowling of Guttridge (house end), and Simpson. Philipson opened the scoring with a single, the first run he has made on the tour. When he had scored three, however, he was out to a brilliant lefthanded catch in the slips by Simpson, off Guttridge. With Barlow in, steadiness was the order, and the score had been carried to 30 before Dunn was caught at the wicket. Barlow quickly followed him out, being clean bowled in trying to pull, and hitting short, at toss from Simpson. Woodward filled the vacancy, but did not stay long, being caught at mid-off by Haslam, so that four wickets were down for 37.

With 40 up Guttridge beat Anderson, and Clayton joined Quinton. They stayed together for some time, when Clayton played outside one from Simpson, and was bowled (6 for 59); and an adjournment was made for luncheon. A shower of rain caused this interval to be prolonged for about half an hour. On resuming Boworth-Smith joined Quinton, opening his account with a cut for two; but Guttridge hit his leg stump when he had scored 7-7 for 65. Two runs later Simpson bowled Quinton. Corbet, the newcomer, was sent last man, and increased the score by a couple of 4's when Page fell to a catch by Simpson in the slips, the total reaching 84, thus leaving Mr Bewicke's team 113 runs to get to win.
A A Newburn and Butler again opened the home innings. Anderson and Boworth-Smith sharing the attack. Newburn was bowled for a single, and then rain caused a delay of half an hour. Walters was the next batsman, starting with a 2 behind point. With 28 on the board rain interrupted play once more, though only for a few minutes. On turning out Dunn relieved Anderson at the railway end. However, before they had bowled half a dozen overs rain drove them from the field, and, though the shower continued, and play had to be abandoned. Both Walters (21) and Butler (10) batted steadily for their runs. Score:-

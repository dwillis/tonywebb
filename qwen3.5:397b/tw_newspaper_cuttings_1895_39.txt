MR. W. PEARCE'S (WYCOMBE) XI. v. SOUTHALL.
On Saturday last Mr. W. Pearce, of the "Nag's Head," London-road, captained an eleven to visit Southall to try conclusions with an eleven representing the Southall Gas Works. Although the Bucks men made a fine innings, having 118 runs to their credit, Southall were far and away the best team, and for the loss of only 3 wickets they had contributed 137 runs. Dixon and Hudson were said to be classed as belonging to the Middlesex team of Colts, and the way in which Dixon contributed his 76 showed that he was a batsman of no mean order. His total included one 5, three 4's, fifteen 2's, and five 3's. William Cooper, too, knocked up eight boundaries in succession. In fact, against such formidable opponents the bowling of the Wycombe representatives was taxed to its utmost; but, nevertheless, a pleasant game was the result. Both W. Pearce and C. W. Harper showed good form for the losers. Score and analysis:-

MR. COLLINS CLASS v. BOOKER TEMPERANCE.
The return match between these clubs took place at Booker on Saturday last, and a pleasant game resulted in a win for the Class by eight runs on the first innings. The meeting of these teams has always been a close one; last year it was a win for the Class and a drawn match, and this year the result of the fixtures has been the same. Batting first, the Class made 50, J. Keen (22) and Collins (10) being chief contributors. Booker then went in, and the total had reached 42 when the last wicket fell, out of which number Burnham had made 16. In their second venture the Class could only compile 27, neither batsman being able to make a stand against the under-arm bowling of U. Pearson; Heather (15) was the only one to get into double figures. Score:-

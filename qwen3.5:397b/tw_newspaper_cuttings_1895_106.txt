"HEREFORD TIMES" CRICKET CHALLENGE TROPHY.
FINAL.
PRESTEIGN v WORCESTER ST. JOHN'S.
The preliminary rounds of this competition having been duly played off it only remains for Presteign to meet Worcester St. John's in the final at Widemarsh to-day (Saturday), to decide the destination of the Cup. Two seasons ago, it may be remembered, St. John's were returned the winners, but last year the holders were defeated by Lugwardine. However, in the first round this season St. John's reversed this decision, somewhat easily defeating the strong team the Lugwardine Cricket Club had brought against them. With both teams who play in to-day's final so strongly represented, an excellently contested game should take place. Play will begin at Widemarsh at 12 o'clock to-day (Saturday). SENSATIONAL SCORING.
A remarkable match played during the two opening days of this week on Mr W. Laverton's private ground at Leighton Westbury, resulted in some wonderful scoring, over 1,000 runs being hit up during its progress. The fixture was between Leighton and Oxford Harlequins, and several noted cricketers took part in the game. No fewer than four individual efforts of over a century were compiled. E. S. Ranjitsinhji made 139 for the home eleven, whilst for the 'Quins C. B. Fry compiled 162, V. T. Hill 115, and L. C. H. Palairet 101. The match ended in a draw.

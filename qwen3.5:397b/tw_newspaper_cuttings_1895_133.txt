HERTFORDSHIRE MERCURY: SATURDAY 24 AUGUST 1895-3.

WEST HERTS v. FROGMORE.
West Herts played their last "home" cricket match on Saturday, and those who visited the ground did not have the pleasure of witnessing a very brilliant batting display, though the bowling on each side was a feature in itself. The visiting eleven was Frogmore, who instead of turning up at eleven o'clock, as was announced on the card, did not put in an appearance till lunch-time. The visitors went to the wicket first, and it was at once evident that it was a bowler's day. Wickets fell fast, only one batsman, F. May, playing with confidence. May, who went in second wicket down, batted throughout the innings, the only two batsmen to keep him company for any length of time being W. Hobbs and A. Wright. The innings closed for 73, and of this total May was responsible for 31, W. Hobbs 12, and A. Wright 11. None of the other players succeeded in getting into double figures. C. H. Miller bowled exceptionally well for West Herts, taking four wickets in succession for the cost of 24 runs—an average of six. R. Astley had an average of a little over eight; and Burrows, who was not put on trundling till towards the end of the game, captured the last two wickets for only eight runs. Having a good batting team out the West Herts supporters looked upon the game as practically won, and this opinion was strengthened when there were only 39 runs required to tie at the fall of the second wicket. When the fourth wicket fell there were 20 runs needed. But disaster overtook the home-sters, and the next four wickets fell without any addition to the score, leaving a score of runs to be obtained by the last two batsmen. J. A. Murray was the only player who increased the runs—by four—which, with one extra, left the West Herts team 15 runs in arrear. The visitors commenced their second account, but had not time to finish, and when stumps were drawn 72 runs were recorded for six wickets. The visitors thus gained victory by 15 runs on the first innings.

ST. ALBANS v. HITCHIN.
The first week of the St. Albans Cricket Fortnight on the Herts County Ground was brought to a close on Saturday with a match against Hitchin. During the week there have been various entertainments and outdoor attractions in the evenings, and they have been splendidly patronised. On Friday evening a first-rate concert was given at the County Club, and so keen was the desire to be present that more than 200 reserved seats were booked. Sir J. Blundell and Lady Maple, Miss Maple, and party, Mr. E. M. Crosse, and party, Mr. and Mrs. J. S. Hill, and party, and numerous other county gentry were present. The programme included such well-known names as Miss Stewart Ingle, Miss Marie Hooton, Mr. Dyed Lewis, Mr. Barry Linton, and Miss Edie Reynolds. Besides other capable performers, a huge attraction was provided in the shape of living pictures, in which Miss Maple, Miss Crosse, and Mr. W. J. Hardy, F.S.A., among others, took part; while a cricket song, specially composed for the festival by Mr. Furley Lewis, was sung by "W. G." The music is bright and inspiring, and the chorus goes with a good swing, and on reference to the lines below it will be found there is a rhythm which is an excellent recommendation to a song of this description:—
You may search the world all round,
But where Englishmen are found,
From the frozen Polar Ocean to the far Antipodes;
Where there's room to pitch a wicket,
You will find us playing cricket,
For King Willow rules the meadows
As Britannia rules the seas.

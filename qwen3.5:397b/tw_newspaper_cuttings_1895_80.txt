POOLE HERALD: THURSDAY 5 SEPTEMBER 1895 - 2.

Bournemouth C.C. finished their season on Tuesday last with an overwhelming victory over the newly resuscitated Lulworth Lobsters, Capt. W. S. Wyld signalling his last appearance for the club by a capital 52. All told, the club has played 17 matches, of which 10 have been won, 5 drawn, and 2 lost. The defeats were administered by Poole and Hinton Admiral. The draws were with Hampshire Hogs, Hinton, Southampton Deanery (2), and the M.C.C., and it is interesting to note that except for an intervening win between the first and second they all came together. The clubs defeated were Poole, Downton College (2), Bournemouth U. Red, New Forest, Rev. Askew's Dorset XI., Isle of Purbeck, New Forest, Swanage Visitors, and Lulworth Lobsters. Two centuries have been made for the club, Captain Wyld scoring 114 against a strong bowling side of Hampshire Hogs, and R. W. C. Fisher contributing 100 not out against Downton College. In this latter match also, H. G. Lewin got within 5 of his century. Fisher also made good scores of 57 against Bournemouth United, and 64 against the New Forest, whilst Captain Wyld contributed 38 against the New Forest, 60 against Downton College, 67 against Rev. R. Askew's XI., and 58 against Lulworth Lobsters. Pride of place in the averages table, is taken, however, by E. C. Duchesne, who batted so consistently in the eight innings in which he was able to assist the club, that his average works out at 31. One thing that the statistics demonstrate is the number of players Captain Wyld has been able to call to his aid. Very few players have played in more than half-a-dozen games, as will be seen by the list below. In bowling the work has been almost entirely done by Head and R. W. C. Fisher, whose analyses work out so evenly that it is almost impossible to differentiate between them. Head, however, is once again to the front with the largest number of wickets at the smallest cost, and it would be difficult to estimate too highly the value of his services. His most noteworthy performance was perhaps against Swanage Visitors, when, going on late, he secured the last five wickets in six overs for no runs.

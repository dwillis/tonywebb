NEWCASTLE DAILY JOURNAL: WEDNESDAY 5 JUNE 1895-2
Eppleton 2nd v Hamsteels 1st.—Played at Eppleton,
and resulted in a victory for the home team by 63 runs,
Fife securing eight wickets for 19 runs. Score :—Epple-
ton 2nd, 95; Hamsteels 1st, 32.
Tudhoe v. Norton 2nd.—Yesterday Norton 2nd paid a
visit to Tudhoe to play their return friendly. The game
was commenced at 12 o'clock, when the home team went
to the wickets. Jamerson bowled well for the visitors,
taking five wickets for 9 runs. Score:—Tudhoe, 63;
Norton, 77.
Morpeth YMCA v Ashington Excelsior.—At New-
biggin on Whit-Monday. The YMCA batted first, and
declared their innings closed with 104 for five wickets.
Ashington on going to the wickets completely broke
down before the bowling of R J Carr and M Quinn, eight
wickets falling for 1 run, the total only reaching 10.
R J Carr doing the "hat" trick. Score : Morpeth YMCA,
104 (for five wickets); Ashington Excelsior, 10.
The Hermitage v Henderson's Scratch XI.—Played at
Chester-le-Street on Monday, and resulted in a win for
the Hermitage. Score:—

HETTON LYONS CLUB.
On Saturday afternoon the members of this club met
in front of their new pavilion (which has recently been
erected) to take part in the opening ceremony. There
was a large attendance of the members and spectators.
Mr John Burn, the hon. secretary, was called upon to
preside. He called upon Mr John Brown, one of the
oldest members of the club, to make a presentation to Mr
G W Ross, engineer to the Hetton Coal Company. The
present consisted of a gold-plated key and a solid silver
salver, in consideration of his services in furthering the
erection of the pavilion, and other kind services rendered
to the club during the enlargement of the ground.—Mr
Ross suitably replied.—The proceedings terminated with
a match between the Hetton Lyons eleven and Mr Ross'
Sixteen, and resulted in a win for Mr Ross' team by one
run. Score : Hetton Lyons, 91; M Ross' team, 92.

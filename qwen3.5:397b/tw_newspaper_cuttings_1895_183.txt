OXFORD TIMES: SATURDAY 29 JUNE 1895-3.

CHERWELL VALLEY v. HOLYWELL.—On Saturday the Holywell A.C. went over to Heyford and met the new Cherwell Valley Club. The latter went in first, and were all got rid of for 65. Holywell commenced their innings very disastrously, losing three wickets for four runs, but the partnership of Craddock and Churns turned the tables. Later on with eleven runs to win and two wickets to fall it was again doubtful whether the required number would be obtained, but Fortescue and Hewlett put on seven and six each, and the score finally reached 69. With three-quarters of an hour more to play the Valley went to the wicket for a second time and scored 36 for the loss of six wickets. Score:—

LORD WILLIAMS' GRAMMAR SCHOOL v. THAME PRINTERS C.C.—A pleasant and close game was the result of the above fixture on Saturday last, which the Printers won by 17 runs. Davies for the school came out with the fine analysis of 5 wickets for 12 runs, while Cope and Holland both bowled finely for the winners, the former taking 5 wickets for 20, and the latter 4 for 38.

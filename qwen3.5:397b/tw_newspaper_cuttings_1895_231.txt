EVEHAM v. PERSHORE.—Played at Evesham on Saturday. Going in first Evesham fared badly, six wickets being down for 11. However Goodall, Needham, Blanchard, and later on Field made useful scores, and the total realised 89. Pershore could do nothing against the bowling of Field, but scored rather freely of Heath. Their total realised 68. Evesham thus winning by 21 runs. C. Phillips batted splendidly for his runs, and Waite also did well. Field took four wickets for eight runs. Score :—

HANLEY CASTLE GRAMMAR SCHOOL v. LYTTELTON GRAMMAR SCHOOL, MALVERN.—Played at Malvern on Wednesday, and resulted in a victory for the Hanley Castle boys, who, batting first, made 97 runs, Lyttelton making 66. Chadd maintained his reputation as a batsman and bowler, making 49 runs in good style, and taking five Lyttelton wickets for 16 runs. Horne (14) and Mulliner (10) batted well for Lyttelton. Score :—

MALVERN COLLEGE v. M.C.C.
This two-days' match, which concluded at Malvern on Saturday, was remarkable for the small score of the Collegians in their second innings, viz 42 is about the lowest record associated with the Malvernians. M.C.C. had the assistance of Woodcock and White-side, from Leicestershire. The former took seven wickets in the second innings, and a grandly-played century total by the Rev. R. T. Thornton, together with some useful scores, left M.C.C. winners at the finish by an innings and 68 runs.

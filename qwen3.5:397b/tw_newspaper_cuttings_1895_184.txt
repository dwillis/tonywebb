THAME 2ND XL v. OXFORD HIGH SCHOOL.—Played at Oxford on Wednesday July 10th, ending in a victory for the School by 33 runs on the first innings. The School went in first, and scored 61 before they were got rid of. On Thame going in, they were all dismissed for the paltry total of 28, B. O. Corbett and Andrews sharing the bowling honours with five wickets each. The second venture of the School realised 46. Score :—Oxford High School, 61 and 46; Thame 2nd, 28.
ATHLETIC CLUB v. IPSDEN C.C.—The return match between these teams was played at Ipsden on Saturday last, and resulted in a victory for the visitors by 29 runs on the first innings. It was a good evenly-contested game throughout, both sides acquitting themselves in gallant style. For the W.A.C. Daymond, Peyman, and Pounsett did yeoman service, the trio compiling no less than 62 runs between them, the latter two also distinguishing themselves with the ball as well as the bat, taking three wickets for nine runs, and four for 32 respectively. Arding and Hillier proved capital mainstays of the home team, both making serviceable scores. After the match the players were hospitably entertained by Lieutenant Arding, and an enjoyable evening was spent. Score :—

Going in first the visitors scored 252 for seven wickets and then disposed of their opponents for 114. Gmelin played well for his 60 and R. T. Roberts was in good form with both bat and ball.

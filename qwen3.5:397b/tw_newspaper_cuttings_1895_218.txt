STAFFORDSHIRE CHRONICLE: SATURDAY 31 AUGUST 1895-3.

UTTOXETER v. STAFFORD.—The return match between these clubs was played at Stafford on Saturday. A very late start was made, the train by which the Uttoxeter players travelled being very much behind time. Batting first, Stafford ran up a score of 70 for seven wickets, when the one-hour rule was applied; but the time at the disposal of the players was hardly sufficient to allow the match to be won, though a few more minutes would have worked the oracle. The bowling on both sides was difficult to play, the ball occasionally getting up dangerously high from the pitch, making batting anything but a pleasure. Scores:—

STAFFORD v. MR. WILSHAW'S ELEVEN.—On Wednesday Stafford placed an eleven in the field to play a team selected by Mr. T. Wilshaw. Stafford went in first, and the first five wickets went down at the expense of a very limited number of runs. R. Joyce and Hall, however, made a stand, and the latter hit out freely with the result that the score mounted rapidly, and at the fall of the next wicket a more encouraging state of affairs existed. S. W. Morgan went to the wicket vacated by Joyce and knocked 14 runs before he was clean bowled by Wilshaw. In the same over Maylott was dismissed without scoring, and Hall soon afterwards followed suit, being caught at point from a fast ball from Taynton. F. Baker went in, but the wicket was taken when he had only scored four. Mr. Wilshaw's eleven fared even worse than their opponents. The batsmen were unable to withstand the bowling of Newbold and Joyce, and for 14 runs seven wickets had fallen. The remaining batsmen, however, played hard, and an excellent stand by Taynton and Rhodes was the preventative of a crushing defeat. At the fall of the eighth wicket the telegraph showed that seventy-four runs had been compiled. The last wicket fell with only one run being added, Taynton being not out for 48.

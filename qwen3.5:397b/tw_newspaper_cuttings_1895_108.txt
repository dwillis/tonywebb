HERTFORDSHIRE EXPRESS : SATURDAY 18 MAY 1895 - 2.

Cricket Matches.

HITCHIN v. ST. ALBANS.—Played at Hitchin on Wednesday, May 15. The visitors were not so strong as they had hoped to be, but their eleven included H. J. Hill, who did so well for the county last year, Field, and the veteran Tom Pearce. St. Albans won the toss and started batting to the bowling of Wilson and F. R. Shillitoe. Very little stand was made H. J. Hill and Field giving the best exhibition, and the whole side was dismissed in 80 minutes for 87. Wilson took five wickets for 46, the Rev. Rivett Carnac three for 11, and Shillitoe two for 32. The home team occupied the wickets for the rest of the day. Two new members of the club, Captain Rivett Carnac (103) and the Rev. G. Rivett Carnac (44) gave a fine exhibition of batting. G. Sheppard, another new member, and the Rev. A. E. Lobbs were also useful, and the innings closed for 246. Pearce was responsible for nine of the wickets at a cost of 75 runs, an excellent performance. Score :—

Hitchin O.C. began the season on Saturday with an inter-club match, Married v Single, the teams captained respectively by Mr. W. E. English and the Rev. J. H. Bannerman. The weather was all that could be desired, and a very interesting game took place. The Married won the toss and started batting with W. H. English and Wilson who were not parted until the score stood at 53, Wilson being taken after a good innings of 46. English was caught in the long field with the score at 119, his individual contribution being 59. The innings closed for 153. Shillitoe proved the most successful bowler, taking five wickets at a cost of 40. The Single men could not do much with the batting of Larman and Brown, seven wickets falling for 50 runs, but when Shillitoe partnered Bauerman a good stand was made, the latter being caught for 25 when the score had reached 91. The innings closed for 103, leaving the Married men victorious by 48 runs. Larman took five wickets for 26, and Brown four for 36. Score :—

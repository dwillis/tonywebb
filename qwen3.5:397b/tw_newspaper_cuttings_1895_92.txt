Mr MORTIMER'S XI. v. Mr CLAYTON'S XL
(By "Custos.")

Yesterday the meeting between these teams on the Constabulary Ground, Jesmond, drew together a good number of spectators. The heavy rain overnight made the wicket soft to start with, but the sun soon affected it, and it became very difficult as the day wore on. Under these circumstances winning the toss conferred a great advantage. Dame Fortune decided in favour of Mr Mortimer in this matter, and he, of course, took first innings. Taken altogether the batting was moderate, that of Griffin standing out by itself as the best of the day. This professional opened with a very fluky stroke over point's head; but quickly settling down, he scored freely of all the bowlers, and did not make another mistake until he played on to Quinton. His 63 included a 6, eight 4's, a 3, and five 2's. The finest performance of the day, however, was that of C Latimer, who obtained eight wickets for 30 runs. Helped by the ground, and making the most of his opportunity, Latimer took a great amount of watching, and managed to "stick up" almost every batsman. The fielding also was smart. Some of Mr Clayton's team shaped very poorly; and at the finish, with three of them out in their second innings, they are still 47 runs behind.

Winning the toss Mortimer decided to bat, taking Garnett with him to the wickets to open the innings. The bowling was entrusted to Pope and Anderson, while Woodward kept wicket. Off the last ball in the MCC man's first over Mortimer tried a short run. Dunn, however, threw Garnett's wicket down from extra cover, so that the experiment proved a costly one. The captain then started the scoring with a boundary through the slips off Pope. Hansell filled the vacancy and commenced with a 3 to the off; but was taken in the slips by Anderson off Pope immediately afterwards. Griffin was next man in, and getting several 4's off Pope, brought on Dunn as the cemetery end when the score stood at 34, while with 43 up Corbet relieved Anderson. This change proved effectual, as Corbet bowled Mortimer, who had played good cricket for 19, the partnership having realised 43 runs. Cumberlege followed in; and Bosworth-Smith went on in place of Dunn. The new-comer was missed from a return by Corbet. Griffin did nearly all the scoring and with 89 up Clayton relieved Corbet, Quinton going on at the other end. A 4 to the professional sent up the "century" and then Quinton bowled him. The routing batsman had played a capital innings for 63 without a chance. V Thompson succeeded, and with 110 on the board an adjournment was made for luncheon. On resuming Bosworth-Smith started the bowling. Cumberlege left his crease to attempt a leg-bye, and was promptly run out. (5 for 111.) Hoare did not stay long, being caught at mid-off by Corbet off Clayton after making 9. (6 for 127.) Henderson was the new-comer. After six runs had been added Bosworth Smith dismissed Thompson with a fine one-handed catch, low down, at extra cover. Latimer failed to score; and with 140 up Anderson was tried again, but the separation came from the other end, Bosworth-Smith bowling Henderson. (9 for 156.) Salmon was last man in, but Gowans monopolised the scoring until he was caught at extra-cover by Barlow off Bosworth-Smith, the innings closing for 161. . . . .

Clayton took Philipson to the wickets with him to open their innings, Griffin and Latimer bowling. The captain started merrily, getting the professional's first two deliveries to the off-boundary, while Philipson also scored a 4 off Latimer. When he had made 11 Clayton was taken at the wicket by Salmon off Latimer (one for 15). Bosworth-Smith's wicket was saved by a wide throw-in from Gowans, and then Philipson played on to Griffin (two for 27). Barlow followed in, but at 33 lost his partner, who was sent back by Latimer. Anderson was bowled for a single, and Dunn was caught in the slips off the first ball he received. Quinton and Barlow carried the score to 60, when Latimer clean bowled the last-named batsman, repeating the performance to Woodward before the latter had scored. Quinton fell a victim to Griffin immediately after; and Pope had only made a single when he put his leg in front. Corbet, the last man, snicked a 4 as a start, and was then busted by the Northumbrian, the total reaching only 74.

Being 87 runs behind, Mr Clayton's eleven followed on, the captain going in first with Barlow, Griffin and Latimer again bowling. Barlow returned one to Latimer when he had made 2, and Clayton left at 24; and with the score at 40 Bosworth-Smith was given out leg-before. Then, as the light had become very bad, it was decided to draw stumps for the day. Play will be resumed at eleven o'clock this morning. Score:—

ACKLINGTON AND DISTRICT v. ALNWICK.

This match was played at Alnwick on Wednesday, in cold and threatening weather, on behalf of the Alnwick Cricket Club, before a moderate attendance of spectators. The visitors won the toss and elected to bat on a soft wicket, which became more and more tricky as the game went on. The start was not disastrous, two wickets being down for two runs; but on the two Grays getting together they were not separated until 50 runs had been registered by good cricket. The score finally reached 57, of which number J Achison Gray was responsible for 33, six of the side failing to score. The home side fared even worse, as only Shoomsill offered any resistance to the bowling of C Riddell and E Rudge, the former capturing three and the latter six, and the whole side were got rid of for the small score of 34.

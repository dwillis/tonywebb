BERROW'S WORCESTER JOURNAL: SATURDAY 17 AUGUST 1895-3.

WICHENFORD v. T. G. RICHENS' XI.—This match was played at Laughern Hill on Monday, and ended in a win for Wichenford by 13 runs after a most exciting match. Mr. Richens won the toss and went in, and put together a very smart 53, Messrs. Harris and Humphreys playing well for their runs. Wichenford then went in and began disastrously, as six wickets were down for 5, but when B. Pierce joined J. Page an excellent stand was made, the former hitting out with perfect ease. G. A. Fido was his next partner but one, and when these two got together they hit the bowlers to all parts of the field and eventually won the match. B. Pierce's fine innings included two 6's, two 5's, nine 4's, and nine singles. Mr. F. L. Harkness kept wicket for the losers in first-rate style. Williams and Caswell again bowled very effectively for Wichenford. Score:—

WICHENFORD v. CATHEDRAL GUILD.—This match was played at Laughern Hill, and ended in an easy win for Wichenford by 54 runs and 5 wickets. The Guild won the toss and elected to bat on a good wicket, but were soon dismissed, thanks to the excel-lent bowling of W. Williams and H. Caswell. Both bowled perfectly and with an excellent length. Wichenford got 74 for six wickets and declared, Messrs Hastings, T. G. Williams, and G. F. Jones Williams getting most runs. The Guild then went in again and did slightly better against the same two bowlers, who were bowling capitally, and got for eight wickets. Score:—

ST. JOHN'S 2ND XI. v. COLWALL.—Played at Colwall and resulted in an easy victory for St. John's. Messrs.

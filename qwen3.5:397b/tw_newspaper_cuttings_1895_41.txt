CAMBRIDGE EXPRESS: SATURDAY 3 AUGUST 1895 - 3.

SPORT AND PLAY.

GOSSIP UPON CURRENT SPORTS AND PASTIMES.

I am vain enough to think that some of my readers have missed "Vim's" notes from the columns of the Express during the last fortnight. To them I offer an apology for my non-appearance in print. Like the majority of people, I caught the election fever, and, becoming absorbed in the progress of the fight, had no time to expend upon such mundane things as athletic notes. I am afraid I have rather got out of touch with the progress of events in the athletic world during the last fortnight, but will do my best to make up lost ground.

* * *

My first duty is to express both pleasure and regret at the doings of the County Cricket Club. The drubbing the County team received from the M.C.C. and Ground was a severe one, although the Club team was not a particularly strong one. These curious collapses of our County teams are unexplainable by me, although those who are behind the scenes may be able to give a reason. With a somewhat stronger eleven, the County gave Hunts. on Wednesday a dito to the treatment they had received from the M.C.C., viz., a single innings defeat. The record for the season thus far is one win and one loss. On Monday and Tuesday next, the County will entertain the M.C.C. and Ground at Cambridge, and it is to be hoped that they may be able to give a better account of themselves then.

* * *

The old suggestion has been revived in the select circle of enthusiasts of the game of the desirability of playing at least one County match each season upon Parker's Piece. The Hunts. match was peculiarly suited to such treatment, and it is a pity that it was not played on the public ground, and used to excite a greater interest amongst Cambridge people in the doings of the Club, the future of which looks none too rosy at present.

* * *

The final of the Cambridgeshire Cricket Cup Competition will again be fought out by the Old Higher Grade, last year's winners, and the Camden. The old boys are such good sportsmen that one would like to see them win again, but, on the other hand, the Camden have been in the competition since the very commencement, and have had a great run of ill-luck, and their win would be a popular one. The final promises once more to be an extremely interesting one.

* * *

The scratching of the Cassandra Club in this Competition has caused a good deal of public comment. I have had a conversation with the secretary, who declines to state why the Club scratched, although he affirms that they had good and sufficient reason for so doing, which I do not doubt. After all, action of this kind is the Club's private business, but it seems a pity the Cassandra should have knocked out the old Perseans, who would have gone through, and then have retired without vouchsafing any reason for so doing.

* * *

I am really sorry for Sawston, who were so crushingly defeated by the Old Higher Grade last Saturday. What could have come over the Institute team? The whole side was disposed of for 11 runs, six of these being extras, and the only man who scored off the bat was Bailey.

* * *

I am informed that the suggested dates for the final are August 22nd and 23rd, but it has not yet been decided whether the match shall be played on Parker's Piece or the University Ground.

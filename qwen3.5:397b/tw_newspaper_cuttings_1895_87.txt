HOWICK v WARKWORTH.
At Howick, in beautiful weather, these teams met for the third time this season. A good wicket had been prepared and a pleasant game ensued, the only regrettable feature of the match being an accident to W Shaw, one of the visiting eleven, who, in running to take a catch, unfortunately slipped and received the ball full on his face, the injury preventing him from taking any further part in the game. Douglas was in fine form with the willow, as were also Hope and Ainslie for the home team; while G Green showed that he had not lost any of his hitting power, and Wardle also did well for the visitors. ELSWICK WORKS v ROYAL GRAMMAR SCHOOL.
These teams met on the ground of the Works Club at Benwell on Saturday, in beautiful weather, before a good attendance of interested spectators. The visitors batted first, but, owing to the excellent bowling of Elsey and Swinburne, were unable to put together more than 35 before being all dismissed, Bartram being the only one to make anything like a stand. Elsey took seven wickets for 15 runs, and Swinburne three for 14. Cochrane and Spencer were the first pair of batsmen for the home team, but the latter was clean bowled by Spurgin when he had made 4, and the home captain shared the same fate after having scored 9. Elsey, Newitt, and George all batted well, and the innings closed for the total of 113 runs. For the visitors Spurgin bowled splendidly, taking eight wickets for 46 runs, which, when the batting strength of the opposing team is considered, is a highly creditable performance for this young bowler. ROCKLIFF v. BENWELL.
Played at Whitley, on a perfect cricket. The home team won the toss and started the innings with Murray and Farmer, to the bowling of Hodgson and French. Runs came slowly but surely until with the score standing at 41 Murray was bowled by Suttie, who had gone on in place of Hodgson. F Latimer joined Farmer and another good stand was made, Farmer eventually being out to a catch at the wicket, having played a good innings of 31. With E W Taylor in the score mounted rapidly, the Rockliff captain batting freely from the start; but he fell to a fine one-handed catch by Miller at third man, having contributed 21. Shaw did not stay long, his wicket falling with the score at 123; but with Dale and Latimer together the score mounted steadily in spite of several changes of bowling, and when it had reached 191 Taylor declared the innings closed, Latimer carrying out his bat for a fine innings of 74 made in his best style. With an hour and a half to play Benwell started their innings to the bowling of E W Taylor and Dale, but made a bad beginning, Miller being bowled by the first ball he received from Taylor, and May being shortly after well caught in the long field off the same bowler, the score standing at 8. At this point Milne and Ashmead got together, and starting steadily, carried the score on until it looked as though they would play out time; but at 88 Ashmead played a ball into his wicket. Milne continued to play fine cricket on being joined by Suttie, and about 30 more runs had been added when "last over" was called. Suttie received his last over, the third ball of which he lifted clean out of the field into the sea, and at this point the match terminated in a draw, Milne carrying out his bat for a faultless 75.

ROYAL MUNSTER FUSILIERS v ARMY SERVICE CORPS.
BATTING EXTRAORDINARY.—THE RECORD BEATEN.
Captain W. Cospe Oakes, Royal Munster Fusiliers, the popular Captain of the Clontarf District C.C., and Private Fitzwilliam, of the same battalion, yesterday had the distinction of topping the batting record by compiling the immense score of 653 runs during their partnership, and as both batsmen were not out at the call of "time," when they stopped goet for another 100, it is impossible to estimate what might have happened had the game been prolonged. The batting up of this huge score occupied exactly 4½ hours, yielding an average of 151 runs per hour. 653 is supposed to have been the previous record, that being the score credited to Messrs A. H. Treven and G. F. Vernon in a match, thirham Club v. Mickley Green, some years ago, but it is understood both batsmen were "out." Captain Oakes, who has done so much for cricket, especially for the ground on which the immense score has been compiled, richly merits the distinction of having contributed the lion's share towards this remarkable performance, and it bears eloquent testimony to the value of the improvements to the ground inaugurated by him eight months ago, that this new record score should have followed so closely the over of 400 runs, recently made by the Clontarf District against Dublin University. IRISH CRICKET UNION.
A meeting of the committee of the Leinster branch of the Irish Union was held last evening in the Wicklow Hotel, when there were present—Mr. C. W. Frith (in the chair), Mr. F. Stokes (Dundrom), Mr. H. Thornton (Pembroke), Mr. C. Mitchell (Dundrom), Mr. J. Carter (Dundrom), Mr. F. H. Browning (Dublin University), hon. sec.; Mr. J. Fosall (Leinster), hon. treasurer. Mr. Fosall announced that the receipts at the recent Gentlemen of Ireland v. N.U.C. match amounted to £52, and after the discharge of all liabilities a balance of close on £30 remained to the credit of the union—a very satisfactory state of affairs. The Junior Cup competition was discussed, and it was decided that clubs, in order to be eligible, should be affiliated with the Leinster branch by July 1st. Entries for the cup close on July 16th.

ABERTILLERY v. PENARTH.
The first visit of Penarth to Abertillery drew together a big crowd to the Abertillery cricket field on Saturday. The weather was all that could be desired, and the wicket was in good condition. A stiff fight was anticipated, and the result of the match proved such to be the case. Abertillery opened their wings, Withers and Wimmill taking up the defence, to the bowling of Birch (pro.) and F. W. Morgan. Wimmill's stay was short, being smartly caught behind the wicket off Birch without a run being scored. M. Robbins filled the vacancy, and Withers notched a couple off Morgan's first ball. A separation was again brought about, Robbins being beaten by a good ball from Birch, two wickets falling for two runs. S. Robbins followed, and opened his account by hitting Birch for 2 and a single, and also notching a boundary off Morgan. Withers was dismissed by a catch by Morgan, and Browne took his place. "Si" made a good bit for 4 off Morgan, and Browne started with a 2 and 3 off the same bowler, and in the succeeding over got Birch away for a similar number of runs. With the score at 22, Gough relieved Morgan, and after both batsmen had got him away for 5 runs, Robbins was splendidly caught by Morgan, for a quickly compiled 10. Jeffreys partnered Browne, and runs again came quickly. With the score at 31, Browne lifted one of Gough's deliveries into the hands of Abraham, after contributing 17 runs. Jeffreys, who was dismissed by a remarkably clever one-handed catch by F. W. Morgan, and Steel made 13 between them, and Martindale ran himself out for 10. The remaining batsmen were soon got rid of, Leigh (8) being the only one to make anything like a stand, and the innings closed for 64 runs. Birch and Gough handled the ball very effectively for Penarth, the former taking four wickets for 24 runs, and the latter three wickets for 15.
J. G. Llewellyn and D. C. Davies opened the innings for Penarth, Withers and S. Robbins sharing the bowling. Runs came freely at the start, and 10 was telegraphed after a few minutes' play. With the score at 18, Davies was beaten by a good ball. Withers and Gough filled the vacancy, but with only the addition of a single run, the latter placed one into the hands of Jeffreys off Robbins. Birch followed, and Llewellyn shortly afterwards ran himself out. Love partnered Birch, and both bowlers put down a maiden each. Love got Robbins away for a couple, but with the next ball he was dismissed, four wickets being down for 29 runs. Gibbs followed, and at once began to hit freely, but soon lost his partner, Birch being dismissed by Withers. F. W. Morgan was next man in, and Steel relieved Robbins with the ball. Runs were got freely from the new bowler, and matters began to look brighter for the visitors. Morgan, in stepping out to drive Withers, was clean bowled, and Gibbs was also dismissed by Steel in attempting a similar performance, for a quickly compiled 19. Abrahams and Lee were now together, and the game became exciting. Strenuous efforts were made by the remaining batsmen to pull off the match, but failed to do so by a couple of runs, the innings closing amid much excitement for 62 runs. Robbins and Withers were very successful with the ball, the former taking four wickets for 13 runs, and four wickets for 26 runs being to the credit of the latter. Scores:

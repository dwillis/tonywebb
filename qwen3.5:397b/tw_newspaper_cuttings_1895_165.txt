CALEY'S SECOND v. CARROW SECOND.—Played on Dix's Land on Saturday, and after a pleasant game resulted in a win for Caley's. Carrow bringing a strong team went first to the wickets, and owing to the good bowling of Townshend and Wilson were soon dismissed for the small score of 26. Caley's fared very little better, and just managed to hold out till they beat their opponents by 8 runs, Guymar and Jermyn sharing equal honours with the bowling. Caley's—Brown 1, Smith 0, Townshend 4, Wilson 2, Folk 0, Morter 10, Churchyard 4, Colman 1, Smith 6, Mobbs 0, Moore not out 3, extras 4; total 35. Carrow—Guymar 6, Wilson 0, Murray 6, Jermyn 0, Rayner 6, Nichols 2, Cowles 2, Wales 0, Drake 0, Amis 0, Beales 1, extras 3; total 27.

UNITED v. KNOWLETT.—Played at Newmarket Road on August 10th, and resulted in a win for United by 30 runs and four wickets. United were assisted for the first time this season by Burleigh, who displayed his usual ability with both bat and ball. Thanks to the bowling of Hogg, who took four wickets for 10 runs, and the batting of Hooper, the United were able to win easily. For the losers, Dunham was in first class form, both in bowling and batting. United—Waites 6, Hooper 31, Ellis 15, Howlett 3, Burleigh 9, Hogg 12, White not out 6, Nooth not out 0, extras 7; total 89. Kingstey—Shaw 3, Smith 7, Gillingwater 6, Dunham 30, Skipper 3, Thurlow 0, Johnson 18, Jarvis 2, Holmes 2, Nichols not out 1, Scott 2, extras 1; total 68.

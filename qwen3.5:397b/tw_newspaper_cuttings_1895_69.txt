WESTERN DAILY MERCURY: THURSDAY, FRIDAY 4, 5 JULY 1895 - 2.

R.M.L.I. v. PLYMOUTH COLLEGE.
The return match between these teams took place at Mount Wise yesterday in splendid weather, the Marines winning by 111 runs. Batting first, the visitors were all disposed of for 51, R. A. Lake contributing 15. The Marines, in reply, scored 111, Captain Curteis, Lieutenant Moore, and Corporal McKean being the principal scorers. Following on, the visitors scored 159, Shacklock 52, Lake 52, Mcre 21, and Shanks 20, batting well.

PLYMOUTH v. STONEHOUSE.
This match was played at South Devon-place yesterday, and resulted in an easy win for the home team. Batting first, the home team put together 121, P. P. Tysack, H. B. Spencer, and H. B. R. Harrison scoring most of the runs, and played very fine cricket against the good bowling, coupled with accurate fielding, of the visitors. J. G. Davey bowled unchanged, and sent down 23 overs 14 maidens four wickets for 24 runs. The innings of the visitors was a poor display, owing to the brilliant trundling of H. B. R. Harrison. The Plymouth men bowled at a tremendous rate, and took eight wickets for but 2 runs. Following on the visitors fared a trifle better, and scored 22 for eight wickets. The wicket-keeping of S. Sydney was very fine, only 2 leg byes being registered. Scores :-

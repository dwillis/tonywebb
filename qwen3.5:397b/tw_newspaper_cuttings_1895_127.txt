Cricket Notes.
The County XI played three matches last week. On Monday and Tuesday they opposed Bedfordshire at Bedford, the result being a draw. On Wednesday and Thursday they played M.C.C at Lords, Herts being defeated by 6 wickets; and on Friday and Saturday they met Worcestershire, the game ending in a victory for Hertfordshire by 7 wickets. In the latter game White did well, as he captured five wickets, and made 64 not out.
The Bushey Cricket Week—which was favoured with all sorts and conditions of weather—was brought to a conclusion on Saturday, with a match against West Herts, there being a large number of spectators present. The game ended in a victory for Bushey, who made 153, West Herts being all out for 70 in their first innings.
Cannon Brewery played their return match with Harefield on Saturday, on the ground of the latter, an interesting game ending in favour of the home team by 7 runs. The brewers, who were without the services of T Willson, made 71, Atherton playing well for 19. The two "smiters" of the team—J Stevens and R Welsh—both made runs by some hard hitting.
Harefield, who were well represented, replied with 78. C Branch with 22 was top scorer, being closely followed by C King with 18, Dr Little making 14. East for the losers took four wickets for 13 runs.
Watford Urban District Council made a tie with Bushey Police, each side scoring 48 runs. The Police batted first, Brockwell making just half the runs scored off the bat, being not out 22. On the Urban Council going in wickets fell fast, R Hatton being the only one to make a stand, and when the last man went in 18 runs were required to save defeat. R Hatton and W Batting proved equal to the occasion, and made the requisite number of runs to make a tie.
The Melrose still keep adding to their list of victories, and on Saturday easily disposed of Camden United. Melrose having made 81 for 5 wickets applied the closure, and got rid of their opponents for 26. Wingfield—who means to have that prize bat —was the most successful batsman.
Sedgwick's Brewery having broken their run of ill-luck, had another victory on Saturday, when they defeated the Roamers by 9 runs on the first innings. The scoring was low on both sides, the losers being all out for 27, the Brewery making 36. The Roamers made 46 for eight wickets in their second innings.

BUSHEY V. ERRATICS.
Not the least important feature of the Bushey cricket week was the game between the Erratics and the Bushey C.C., played on the Manor ground on Friday. The home team went in to bat first, and in their three hours and three-quarters of play they knocked up 204 runs, and then declared their innings closed so as to give their rivals a show. The Erratics finished their first innings just before six o'clock, when they had made only 87 runs. The visitors elected to play out the full time, but only three men handled the bat. This enabled them to add sixteen runs to their score. The scores were:

WORCESTER CATHEDRAL SCHOOL v. MR. T. DAVIES' XI.—This match was played on Saturday on the School ground, and resulted in a win for the School by 24 runs. The visitors went in first but save for Messrs. P. P. Williams and K. W. Holland none made any stand, and the innings closed for 47. The School, with the help of a well-played innings of 27 of J. S Liddell, and 15 by K. A. Norcombe, soon topped the visitors score, and eventually the score reached 72. The second innings of the scratch team began disastrously, as seven wickets were down for only 14 runs, but T. F. Hammell raised the score somewhat before he had unfortunately to retire. Of the School bowlers J. K. Deakin did best with an analysis of seven wickets for 17. Score:—

ST. JOHN'S v. BROMSGROVE.—This match was played at St. John's on Saturday, a capital game resulting in favour of St. John's by 37 runs. Bromsgrove won the toss, but Mr. Beach Hicks and T. Straw both bowled so well, and were so ably supported by capital fielding, that the visitors were all disposed of for 33 runs, Straw taking five wickets for 7 runs. St. John's on taking the bat soon lost four wickets, through the excellent bowling of Rolleston, for 15 runs, but on Mr. Young joining Mr. Curtis an invaluable stand was made, and, thanks to their excellent batting, the score was taken to 41 before the next wicket fell. Mr. Curtis playing capital cricket for his 15, and Mr. Young an equally good innings for 17. Mr. Park also hit well for his score, and the innings closed for 70. Rolleston bowled speedily, and Messrs. J. K. and E. Perkins shared the bowling at the other end, and were well backed up by the excellent fielding. Score:—

WORCESTER GRAMMAR SCHOOL v. HARTLEBURY GRAMMAR SCHOOL.—Played at Flag Meadow on Saturday. Hartlebury batted first and were all sent back for 20. Of these Vale, who was top scorer with nine, played very steadily. Thorp took eight wickets for ten runs. Worcester's prospects did not look very bright when two good wickets went for one bye, but on Davis and Thorp being associated runs came more briskly, and their opponents' total was passed with four wickets down. Ultimately the whole side was dismissed for 34 Davis playing very well for his 15. Vale and Dorsett both bowled very well for Hartlebury. HANLEY CASTLE GRAMMAR SCHOOL v. WORCESTER CATHEDRAL VOLUNTARY CHOIR.—The return match between these teams was played at Hanley Castle on Saturday, and resulted, as in the first match, in a victory for the Grammar School. The Choir batted first, on a good pitch, but wickets fell rapidly before the excellent bowling of Chadd, who took seven wickets for 7 runs, and Hemus who took three wickets for 4 runs. The innings realised 14 runs. Chadd and Toys started well for the Grammar School, making 22 runs for the first wicket. Hemus and Harraves also batted in good form against the somewhat weak bowling of the Choir. After tea, which was served on the ground, Chadd continued batting in excellent style, and carried out his bat for 71 runs. Hemus was drawn at 5.30, when the School had made 123 runs for six wickets. BROMSGROVE SCHOOL v. KIDDERMINSTER.—At Edgbarston. Edgbaston, 202, Bromsgrove School, 92.

In the Birmingham and District League Kidderminster scored 210 for four wickets (H. Gotch not out 83, R. M. Hinton 51, H. Tomkinson not out 43) against Dartmouth's 57 for four wickets. The Kidderminster second eleven made 185 against Dartmouth's 116.

WATFORD TIMES: THURSDAY 30 MAY 1895-3.

BARNET v. SOUTHGATE.
Barnet met Southgate last Saturday on the pretty ground of the latter, and gave an excellent batting display on a perfect wicket. The feature of the game was the fine stand made by Hossack and Steer for the first wicket, the score being only five short of the century when the latter was taken at point. As is so often the case, Hossack did not long survive his partner, as two runs later he was lbw. to Ewart. Dresser 28, Jukes 24, Illing 16, and Cartwright 18, helped to bring the Barnet total to 201 for nine wickets, when the innings was declared closed. This was a good performance against the best eleven Southgate could put into the field. Southgate had only three-quarters-of-an hour's batting, and a wicket fell to Harrison at five. Hossack, who kept an excellent length and was always difficult to play, landed Harris with the score at 18. Then Sugden and Kemp kept their wickets intact to a quarter to seven, when the match was left drawn. Full score and analysis:

MOSSWELL v. ALL SAINTS' (HIGHGATE)
1st XI.
Played at East Finchley on Saturday and ended in a tie. Mosswell, with only nine men, including one substitute, batted first. Walter Dudley and A. HADLEY v. WROTHAM PARK.
Played at Wrotham Park on Saturday last, and resulted in a win for the home team by 19 runs. Wrotham winning the toss, decided to go to the wickets first. Edwards and Shears started the bowling for Hadley, the latter capturing the first wicket for 16 runs. Runs came very fast until the bowling was changed, Tailby and Samuels taking the place of Shears and Edwards. Tailby started well, taking two wickets in the first over, but fell right off afterwards. A good stand was made by J. W. Aris and J. Bell, and the bowling was again changed, Richardson and Edwards going on. The home team were all out for 97. Hadley then went in and made a very bad start, five wickets falling for 26 runs. Samuels and Shears made a good stand, bringing the total up to 61; then Shears was run out, Samuels soon followed with a bailer from Ringham. Hadley all out 78. Wrotham played well, both in the field and at the wicket; Bell and Aris making top score with 26 and 17 respectively. Hadley were very weak in the field, letting several chances go; Samuels and Shears were best at the wicket with 21 and 12 respectively. The following are the full scores:

STEREO v. OAKLEIGH, NEW SOUTHGATE
These clubs were down to play the first cup tie of the season, for the Burrows Challenge Cup, at the Recreation Ground, on Saturday. Oakleigh were unable to get a team together eligible to compete, so gave Stereo a point, but with the help of a friend or two played them a friendly game, which resulted in a very easy win for the cup holders, who batted first and scored 103 runs for 7 wickets, and declared their innings closed. Wilton played a good game for his 41 not out, and Lavington broke his spell of bad luck by scoring 21. The Oakleigh made a good start, as the first 4 wickets realized 52 runs, of which Dalton scored 33. After his departure Leeson and Wilton disposed of the remainder for 8 runs. STEREO B TEAM v. FRIERN BARNET.
This match was played last Saturday at Friern Barnet, and after a close game, Stereo ran out winners by six runs. The Friern batted first, and their innings realised 63. The Stereo set to work and ran up a score of 69. For the Stereo, Pizzey made 19, and Cary 14. For the Friern, Thomson was top scorer with 14, and Clarke made 11. One amusing little incident occurred during the match, when one of the Friern's batsmen picked up the ball and handed it to the Stereo's bowler, upon which, the bowler appealed to the umpire, who replied that the man was not out because he picked up the ball accidentally!

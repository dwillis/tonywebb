ABINGDON HERALD: SATURDAY 24 AUGUST 1895-3.

OXFORDSHIRE CUP.
O.C.C.C. v. VICTORIA C.C.—This tie in the semi-final round of the above competition was played before a large number of spectators on the Hertford ground on Saturday last. The City "skipper" was quite justified in putting his opponents in first. The wicket just suited W. S. Case, and in a little over an hour he had got the side out, and made the remarkable analysis of nine wickets for 22. As 50 was all the Victoria set the City to win, the match looked all over bar shouting; but greatly to the delight of the Victoria supporters, Freeborn and Nutt were in their best form, and four of the best of the City wickets were captured for only 10 runs. Here, however, their successes ended, for Moulder and A. W. Roberts made a brilliant stand, and for exactly 100 minutes defied all the bowling brought against them. Moulder's innings of 94 was by far the best he has ever played for the City Club, and with the exception of one chance in the long field was quite faultless. The fielding of each team left a lot to be desired, although up to the time their score had been passed the fielding of the Victoria was very clean. Play commenced at 1.30, when the home team, having won the toss, decided to put their opponents in to bat, on a drying wicket, Welbury and New were the first pair of batsmen, and the attack was opened by Case (pavilion end) and Goold, and in the former's second over he got Welbury caught at the wicket, the score being 13. With Freeborn in, New drove Case to the boundary, and cut Goold for 4 and 3, but at 26 fell to a catch at slip. Gray played a ball into his wicket at 30, and with only 4 added, both Ridgway and Roberts were clean bowled by Case. Freeborn was batting just an hour for 10 runs, but nobody else stayed long, and the venture closed for 49. Case secured 9 wickets for 22 runs, a splendid performance. The innings of the home team opened in a very sensational manner. Freeborn and Nutt were the bowlers, and when only 3 runs had been scored, Case was secured in the long field. The score was gradually taken towards double figures, but before that was effected Sheard succumbed to a "yorker" from Nutt, and at 10 Freeborn got rid of Day and Phillips. Here A. W. Roberts joined Moulder, the latter out each bowler twice for a trio, and Roberts got Nutt to leg for a couple. At 29 Hewer relieved Nutt, and in his second over Moulder was missed in the long field by New, a mistake which proved very expensive to the Victoria. At 32 Gray superseded Freeborn, and at 40 Hewer gave way to Roberts. Moulder hit Gray twice for 3, and Roberts for a couple, and then with a snick to the boundary passed the Victoria total with 6 wickets in hand. Nutt and Freeborn resumed bowling at 52, and Moulder drove the latter for 2 and 3 in his first over, and then hit Nutt to leg for 3, while Roberts, after a long period of inactivity, cut him for a like number. Moulder placed Freeborn twice through the slips for 3, and on-drove him to the boundary. This brought on New at 76, and each batsman got him away to leg for 4 in his first over. Moulder drove Nutt for 2 and 4, which caused him to give way to Gray. Roberts drove him each side the wicket for 4, and played him to leg for a couple, bringing up the century at 4.50. At 103 Weller was given a trial, but Moulder cut him twice to the boundary and drove him for 2 from his first three balls, and in his second over Roberts cut him for 4 and 3. He shortly afterwards had his revenge, for at 130 he got Roberts l-b-w for an invaluable innings of 34. The pair had added 120 for the fifth wicket in an hour and forty minutes. With G. A. Roberts in, Moulder drove Weller for 3 and three 2's, but when only 6 runs short of the century, he returned a ball to Weller. He had been batting just two hours, and his splendid innings of 94 included eight 4's, ten 3's, and thirteen 2's. Goold, who filled the vacancy, hit each bowler to the boundary, but then was smartly caught by Roberts, and Dickenson arrived. G. A. Roberts hit Weller to leg for 4 and 2, and Dickenson got him away in the same position for 3, and drove him for 2, which brought on Nutt at 160. Dickenson cut him for 3, while G. A. Roberts got 4 from the other end. At 177 Dickenson was easily caught and bowled, and Pike came in, but after Roberts had cut his namesake to the boundary, was clean bowled without scoring, and Wellpert being absent, the innings closed for 183, leaving the home team winners by 134 runs. COWLEY v. THAME.—This match came off on Saturday, in splendid weather, and before a large number of spectators. Cowley were fortunate enough to win the toss, and decided to bat first. P. Rogers and Arnold opened the match, and so well did they bat that 55 runs were scored before the first wicket fell, Arnold being caught at the wicket, after playing a very steady innings for 25. P. Rogers on being joined by F. King still played very correct cricket for his 31 runs. Among the others who batted well were A. Rogers, who hit up 40 in very smart fashion, W. King (21), P. King (14), and C. Walker (12) also played sound cricket. The Cowley batting was most consistent all through the game, and not until they had reached the very respectable total of 181 runs were they all out. The bowling of A. Rogers, P. Rogers, and W. King was first class, and the Thame men were all out for 81, leaving Cowley winners by 100 runs. The only Thame batsman to reach double figures were W. Wells (11), W. Rowell (23), and G. Holloway (15), Mr. Rowell especially batting well for his runs. Score and analysis:—

OXFORD CITY CUP.
CORPORATION (holders) v. CLARENDON PRESS.—This match, played on Saturday, proved most exciting. The Press batted first, and with the help of good play by Simons and Eddles put together 89. Nash bowled very finely, taking 9 wickets for 30. The Corporation could do little till Jennings appeared, but notwithstanding determined play by him and Kimber 9 wickets were down for 85. Tanner and Thomas proved equal to the occasion and won the match by 6 runs. Score:—

ST. PAUL'S v. E.O.C.C.—This tie took place on the Christ Church ground on Saturday last, and resulted in the easiest of wins for St. Paul's, who made 176 for the loss of seven wickets, and disposed of their opponents for 63. Huggins, Mason, and Hart were most conspicuous for their respective sides in the batting.

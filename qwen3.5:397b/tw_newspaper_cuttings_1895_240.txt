OUR SPORTING COLUMN
What with the rain and the easy wicket Walsall did not have much chance of showing what their full batting strength could do in their match on Saturday, although they had a by no means representative team of Dudley to deal with. For once in a way the bowling of Bird lost its terrors, and he was treated with very little ceremony. In fact the whole team batted with confidence, and at the conclusion of the play it was found that Dudley had put together the respectable score of 129 for nine wickets. S. Morgan—who, by the way, has not had the best of luck this season—batted a capital 29 not out, whilst F. Wheldon (21), Mr. Deeley (15), W. Brasnell (11), F. Brown (12), J. Cropper (14), and Cole (not out 13) played the bowling without any apparent difficulty. Smith, too, was evidently in scoring form, but he had the misfortune to return a ball to Barnaby. If the ball had dropped short about an inch it would have been out of the bowler's reach, and it was unquestionably a fine catch. The rain undoubtedly spoilt what would have been a very interesting match.
The Dudley second team on Saturday were in exactly the same comfortable position as the first team. They kept Walsall second in the field all day, and when play concluded only six wickets were down for a score of 115. Some very useful scores were made by A. Fereday (27), E. J. Thompson (27), G. S. Thompson (23 not out), and J. W. Collins (14), all playing well for their respective scores.
Dudley hardly expected to win the Charity Shield with the team they had against Walsall on Monday, but they made a good show, all things accounted for. They hit up 118, and it took the best Walsall wickets to exceed it. Mr. Deeley, the Dudley captain, was principal scorer for Dudley, with a lively hit 39. Wheldon was next best with 29 and Smith and Morgan made 16 and 11 respectively. Batting took another time.
The rain evidently did not damp the ardour of the Netherionians in their match with Darlaston, for the game was brought to a satisfactory conclusion. Netherton, with five wickets down, knocked up a total of 72, Jones (36) and Darby (not out 19) being to the fore. Darlaston were soon disposed of, only 33 runs coming from them, and out of that small total Thomas of Tainall 13 runs. Shaw Road beat Flood Street by 40 runs, the defeated ones being able to put together 19. Woodsetton was another club to take things easily on Saturday, their opponents (Slaters') only making 26, whilst they rattled together 78. W. Baker (35) and T. Walters (10) were chief scorers for Woodsetton, and for Slater's Singler with 10 was exceedingly useful.
Ladbrook Grounds, Wednesbury, were on Saturday and Monday occupied with an interesting pedestrian handicap, and the grounds were largely patronised. The distance was 133 yards, and there were 105 entries, classed into 21 heats. S. Dunn (of Bradley), G. Payne (of Brookwood), and E. Weale (of Can Lane) each won their heats, whilst T. Hulton (of Halesowen) ran second to Weale. After the first round the 20 successful men were re-drawn in four heats, and Payne and Weale managed to win their heats again, and in the last they came in first and second respectively, after Weale had been put back a yard. Payne, who was on the 30 yards' mark, started at 2 to 100, and 7 to 1 against Weale.
The Birmingham League matches suffered, in common with all other games, on Saturday. Stourbridge had a poor team against Aston Unity, and perhaps it was well for them that the rain prevented the game being played. They were without Foster, Foley, Rhodes, and Arnold. Five wickets for 64, however, does not seem quite Unity form; but this was due, probably, to the fine bowling of Bingley, who took four wickets for 22. The Smethwick and Moseley match was abandoned in consequence of the rain.

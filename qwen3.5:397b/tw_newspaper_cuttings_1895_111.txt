The Brewery were very lucky to escape defeat in this match which was played at Bushey on Saturday. The "men in blue" batted first, but declared at 121. ST. ALBANS v. FROGMORE.
Played on the Clarence Park on Saturday, when victory rested with the Albanians with the narrow majority of two runs. Frogmore batted first, sending in Giddins and W. T. Wright to face the bowling of Dickerson and Day. Runs came slowly, and when only 8 had been registered W. T. Wright was bowled by Day with the first ball of his third over. Gentle joined Giddins, and together these two carried the score to 39 before Gentle was dismissed by H. J. Hill. One run later, A. Boxall was sent back by Tom Sansom, and at 42 G. Boff was caught by Westell off Sansom. Giddins, who had been playing a careful game for his 21, was also caught by Westell off the same bowler at 47, and at 55 W. M. Robertson was sent back without scoring. With no alteration W. Wright was also bowled by Sansom, and at 86 the Rev. F. Sullivan fell to the same bowler. The ninth wicket fell for 87, but the remaining two batsmen, A. Wright and J. W. Sharpe, sub., by careful batting carried the score to 101 before the close of the innings. St. Albans opened their innings with H. C. Webdale and W. Spencer, but with the first ball of the match Spencer was caught by Westland off Boxall. A. J. Day came in, but Webdale was also caught off the third ball of Westland's second over. The first five overs were sent down without a run being scored. H. J. Hill joined Day, but when he had only made three, he was clean bowled by Boxall, the telegraph reading 5-3 3. E. R. Hill was caught by Sullivan, also off Boxall, at 15, and at twice that number Day was also caught by Sullivan, after scoring 12. Thirty-seven runs saw Sansom disposed of, and at 43 Westell was also sent back. Dr. Norman and T. Small were the principal scorers, the total being carried to 97 before Dr. Norman was bowled by Westland; 97-8-30. Simmons was caught and bowled by Westland at 102, and Dickerson and Small carried the score to 103, when the former was got out in a very peculiar manner. Boxall delivered a rather short pitched ball which bounced straight for Dickerson's head. He attempted get out of its way; but the ball struck his bat and knocked it on the wicket—hit wicket.

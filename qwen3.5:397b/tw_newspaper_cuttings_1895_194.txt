OXFORD TIMES: SATURDAY 31 AUGUST 1895-3.

O.C.C.C. v. LORD JERSEY'S XI.
The City Club were practically represented by a second eleven team at Middleton Park on the 21st inst., owing to a very large number of their regular players being out of town. Lord Jersey had got a very strong team together, and going in first on a good wicket at one time looked like hitting up a long score. C. T. Hoare was in great form, and gave a splendid display of batting for his 59. The score at lunch time was 127 for four wickets, but on resumption of play three wickets fell without any addition to the score, and the team was finally disposed of for 168. The visitors cut up very badly before the bowling of Gerson, Curtis and Polley, and but for a spirited stand by Buckell and R. T. Roberts, their total would have been meagre in the extreme. Thanks to these two players the total reached 71, but on following on they did even worse, and when stumps were drawn had lost five wickets for 13 runs. Score:-

O.C.C.C. v. BICESTER (MA. H. TUBB'S XI.).
The visitors were again very indifferently represented at Leicester on Saturday, and had to put up with a somewhat severe beating. H. Bassett made his first appearance for the City Club this season. The visitors batted first, and hit up 117, the only stand of any consequence being by Bassett and Holloway, who made 37 for the first wicket, and Freeborn and Holloway, who added 25 for the last. Besides these players Gould, Buckell, and Dickinson each got into double figures, but the home team began none too well, and at the fall of the sixth wicket at 83 it looked anybody's game. Here, however, Polley joined Dauglish, and in spite of various changes in the attack they took the score to 202 before the professional was caught in the slips. The Old Blue continued to bat in vigorous form, and was not disposed of till he had compiled 88 runs, in which were thirteen 4's and six 3's. Stratton quickly knocked up 20, and the side were disposed of for 245, leaving them winners by 128 runs. Score:-

After the match a smoking concert was held at the Old White House, which was well attended.

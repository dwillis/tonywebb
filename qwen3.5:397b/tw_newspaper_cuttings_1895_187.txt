OXFORD TIMES: SATURDAY 17 AUGUST 1895-4.

CITY CUP.
STAR RESERVES v. IPPLEY C.C.—The latter playing much below their true form allowed the Star to win very easily by 70 runs. Except A. R. Parker and Savins, none of the losers could play J. Simmonds with any confidence. For the Star, R. Neale played a most dashing game, scoring his 66 in an hour, his innings including nine 4's, one a magnificent drive on to the new pavilion. Galt, who had bowled with bad luck, also played a very safe game for his 23. Belgrove's dismissal was a bit of bad luck. Score:—

OXFORD CORPORATION C.C. v. CHELTENHAM COR-PORATION C.C.—Owing to the extremely one sided affair at Cheltenham on May 18th, when the Oxford Employes beat those of Cheltenham Corporation by an innings and 59 runs, Dr. Dighton brought over a strong team for the return match, only four Corporation employes being included, and a most exciting contest ended in favour of Dr. Dighton's team by 3 runs only. For the winners Dr. Dighton and Lamb batted with great patience, while Broughton hit vigorously. Tibbetts bowled well and Broughton was also effective, though more erratic. For the losers Rees, Tanner, Crass, and Nash, played with confidence, while Messrs. Gunton and Captain Kimber defended in their characteristic patient style. The fielding on both sides was excellent considering the wet state of the ball occasioned by showers. Lunch and tea were served in a marquee on the ground, by Mr. F. Lyster, in satisfactory style. Among those present were the Mayor (Mr. G. W. Cooper), the Mayoress, Mrs. Hughes, Alderman Deazaley and Buckell, Councillors G. H. Cooper, J. H. Salter, T. H. Kingersley, W. Richings, W. Turrill, and E. J. Brooks, Messrs. J. J. Collett, Supt. Head, J. S. Halliwell, W. S. Carver, T. J. Hull, H. Lindars, H. J. Crane, H. J. White, H. Poulter, and G. H. Webb (Hon. Sec.) From Cheltenham were Aldermen Lawrence and Norman, Councillors Dighton, Margrett and Davies, the Borough Engineer (Mr. Hall), the Borough Accountant, and Messrs. Laveridge, T. W. Halliwell, and F. H. Harris (Hon. Sec.) After dinner speeches were made by the Mayor, Alderman Deazaley and Mr. Carver (Oxford), Aldermen Norman and Lawrence and Mr. A. Lamb (Cheltenham). During the match some of the Cheltenham visitors were con-ducted round the colleges by Messrs. T. J. Hull, H. Lindars, and H. J. Crane. Score:—

STAR C.C. v. VICTORIA C.C.—After a lapse of three years, these two well known teams met on the ground of the latter on Saturday last. The Vics won the toss and ran up the capital score of 142 for six. George Gray played excellent cricket for his 42, as did Roberts 30, and T. New 29. J. Walters took three wickets for 39, no one else did much. For the Star J. Penny played in first class style for his 40. Score:—

ST. BARNABAS v. DEACONIANS.—Played on Exeter ground, on Saturday, and was won by St. Barnabas by 20 runs, chiefly through the bowling of Court, who took 6 wickets for 25 runs. Messrs. Craddock, Thomas, Brooke, Jones, and Axtell reached double figures. For the losers, B. V. Harcourt and Baines batted well. Scores:—St. Barnabas, 89. Deaconians, 69.

ST. THOMAS' v. HOLY TRINITY.—This match was played for a presentation cup, and took place in Mr. Hall's Close, resulting in a victory for St. Thomas' by 10 wickets. W. White and A. Whitaker bowled well for the winners, and A. Longford and R. Wilkins for the losers.

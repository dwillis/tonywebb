HERTS ADVERTISER: SATURDAY 3 AUGUST 1895-4.

ST. MICHAEL'S v. COLNEY HEATH.
Played at Colney Heath on Saturday, on a wicket that was not all that could be desired. Various circumstances were quite adverse to the strength of the two teams being fairly proved, but a pleasant game ended in a victory for the home team by eight runs. St. Michael's batted first, the wickets falling rapidly to the bowling of Austin and Aslett. The Rev. P. Robinson, Bangs and Elborne were the only batsmen who faced the bowling for any length of time, and these were only able to score a few runs. The innings realised a total of 22. When Colney Heath went to the sticks they had everything in their favour, and it seemed apparent that a huge defeat was in store for the Michaelsonians. Anticipations, however, were not realised, as Elliott, Spencer, Johnson, James and Wimbury were all out for five runs. The old veteran, Potter, was the most fortunate batsman, being let off not less than three times, but he was ultimately held by Weatherley at cover after scoring 15. Only 30 runs were scored by the home team, who thus gained a victory as stated above. Bangs and Weatherley did the principal part of the bowling, taking three wickets each for 15 and 13 runs respectively, whilst the Rev. P. Robinson also bowled well for his two wickets, which only cost him a couple of runs, but he delivered two wides. Long's wicket keeping was very smart, but the fielding of St. Michael's was not so close as that of the home team, otherwise victory would have undoubtedly rested with them. WEST HERTS v. EARL OF ESSEX'S XI.
This annual fixture, which is always looked forward to with much interest, took place on the West Herts enclosure on Saturday, in beautifully fine weather. Among those present during the afternoon were Lady Essex and her daughter, Iris de Vere Capell, Lord Hyde, Lady Edith Villiers, the Hon. Holland-Hibbert and party, Mrs. Humbert, Dr. A. T. Brett, Dr. Berry, Dr. Meyer, the Rev. H. Godwin, Capt. Wyner and several others. The visitors were the first to go to the wickets, the Hon P. Egerton and the Hon. R. Ward opening the account against the bowling of J. Woods and R. R. Barker. In a few minutes the Hon. R. Ward was bowled after having made two runs and Lord Roselyn who filled the vacancy shared the same fate. The rest of the team followed in, but were all dismissed before an adjournment was made for luncheon, the total reaching being 75. Mr. F. Egerton carrying his bat for out 35. After lunch H. H. Cobb and E. R. Hill opened the batting for the home team, and at the fall of the last wicket the telegraph showed the total to be 156, A. Butcher 41, C. H. Little 32 and H. Barnett 15 being the highest scorers. Lord Essex's eleven were put in a second time, but failed to reach their previous score by 24 runs. The following were the scores:—

HEATH BROW SCHOOL: SCHOOL HOUSE v. LODGE.
The School brought their season to a close last Tuesday by playing the above house match. The result was an easy victory for the School House, thus reversing last season's verdict. For the losers Richards was the only one to offer any resistance to the bowling of Johnson and Garrington. On the winners going to bat there wickets soon fell, but on Garrington and Horn becoming associated they defied the Lodge attack, both being not out at the drawing of stumps. THE BUSHEY CRICKET WEEK.
This week begins on Monday next, and with fine weather a large gathering is expected to visit the grounds of the Manor House, when the respective matches will be played. On Wednesday a grand promenade and concert will be given, and the grounds of the mansion will be illuminated. Programme for the week:—
Aug. 5th, Bushey v. Vampires.
" 6th, " v. Lister.
" 7th, " v. Mr. Black's XI.
" 8th, " v. Old Eton Johns.
" 9th, " v. Eration.
" 10th, " v. West Herts.
The match with West Herts is looked forward to with much interest as both sides, it is stated are endeavouring to put strong teams in the field. Bushey O.C. has beaten West Herts in every previous match played.

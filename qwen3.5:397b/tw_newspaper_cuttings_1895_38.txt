WYCOMBE RESERVES v. WEST WYCOMBE.
This match was played at West Wycombe on Saturday afternoon. The visitors went to the wickets first, but offered very feeble resistance to the bowling of Turner and Fletcher, who each took five wickets for 10 runs. The home side, owing to the good batting of Turner, A. Harman, and Fletcher reached a total of 78. For the Reserves Keen bowled in good form, taking six wickets for 33 runs. In their second attempt the Reserves did better, and reached their fourth wicket before the third wicket fell; but their total was not high enough to avert defeat, as it left the home side only 32 to get to win, which they accomplished with the loss of four wickets.

HIGH WYCOMBE v. WOOBURN INSTITUTE.
This match, which was played on the Wycombe ground last Saturday afternoon, resulted in a draw that, if time had permitted, would have ended in favour of the home team. The visitors were without Barbour, the Lenman, and Foster, who generally play for the Institute; but they had the assistance of H. Bacon, who proved a tower of strength to them in both departments of the game. Wycombe did not play their full strength, more than half their number being reserve men. The home side went in first, and a total of 156 was reached before they were disposed of. The chief contributor to this score was F. George, whose figures included a 4, six 3's, and seven 2's. Other good innings were played by Bird, G. George, and Page who drove one of Bacon's deliveries out of the field for 6. Bacon was a word of praise ought to 1; given to Raffety among the younger members of the team, who played well for 21; his principal hits being a 4, three 3's, and three 2's. Bacon was very successful in bowling, his eight wickets costing a trifle over six runs apiece, and the last three being obtained without a run being scored off him. For Wooburn Institute, Bacon, Bowler, and George played well, the second named hitting G. George for a 6. When stumps were drawn the visitors had made 129 for seven wickets, the last man of which had put on but eight runs between them. Page was the most successful with the ball, but his four wickets cost nearly 13 each. Score and analysis:-

MARLOW v. MR. J. MONRO WALKER'S XI.
On Saturday Marlow met an eleven got together by Mr. J. Monro Walker, of Bisham Abbey, on the Marlow ground. The weather was delightfully fine, and an enjoyable game resulted. The visitors won the toss, and took first innings. Harbottle hit hard for 33, and with smaller contributions from all the side except Langley (who was run out before he had scored), the total reached 123. Harbottle hit a 9 (four for an overthrow) and four 4's. Monro Walker was credited with 22 (including an overthrow) but Paine and Faulkner played cheaply, but with useful scores from Langley, Bower, Baillie, and Want, the visitors' total was passed with six wickets down. Eventually the innings closed for 183. Want—one of the Australian visitors to Marlow—hit a 9, six 4's, and three 2's in his dashing score of 48. Langley hit five 4's in his 30. W. Walkers team went in a second time, and had compiled 113 for six wickets, when stumps were drawn. Marlow therefore won by 30 runs on the first innings. Full score and analysis:-

WYCOMBE CHURCH ROOM v. WYCOMBE ST. JOHN'S.
The third encounter between the above-named teams took place on the Wycombe Rye on Saturday last in splendid weather, and, after a pleasant and interesting game, resulted in a win for the Church Room by 37 runs on the first innings. The St. John's won the toss and elected to bat first, and compiled the moderate total of 40, S. Free (12), Burnham (8), and Ing (9) being the top scorers. The Church Room then went in, and replied with 77, G. Vickers (not out, 26), Sexton (16), and G. Twitchen (12) being the double-figure contributors. The St. John's then went in again, and showed better form, knocking up 60, W. Rolls (11), Ing (12), and Ford (9) being the chief scorers. WYCOMBE BELLS V. WANDERERS v. HOLLOWAY'S BOOT OPERATIVES O.C.
The return match between the combined teams, which took place on the Wycombe Rye last Saturday, excited much interest. Mr. H. Ockenden, who "skippered" the Wanderers, was unable to obtain a formidable eleven, his team being but poorly represented, in fact at the last moment they had to make up with five substitutes. The men of "was and leather" batted first, and put up a creditable total of 56; White, Humphrey, Collier, Lillywhite, and Cohen, contributing their respective scores in fine style. Tibbles, with the ball, was in rare form, his four wickets only costing a fraction over 4 runs a-piece; Anger took 2 wickets for 32. The time eventually arrived for the Wanderers' turn at the wickets, Colman and Towers opening the attack against the destructive delivery of Humphrey and Mead. Whites 1 and 2 went down for 16, and at this juncture it looked as though the Wanderers' contingent would collapse, six wickets being captured for 22, the last three of which not adding a run. The score rose slowly, and when Thorne went in the Wanderers' hopes revived, the great slogger piling on his 15 in double quick time. The end eventually arrived with the total at 54, which left the operatives winners on the first innings by 12 runs. Both Mead and Humphrey, who bowled throughout the game, captured 4 wickets each for 32 runs apiece. The election went in a second time, and at the close of the venture 31 runs had been added for the loss of seven wickets. After the match the teams heartily enjoyed a social at the "Bells Vue," Gerrards-cross.

SPEEN v. ICKENHAM.
This match was played at Speen, a good number of spectators being present to witness the play. It was felt by both teams that it was now, or never, so each team entered the field ready to do its best to win, but, after a very pleasant game, it was found that Speen had been beaten by 9 runs on the first innings. The Speen Brass Band added to the pleasantness of the day by playing selections of music. A capital tea was provided by Mrs. Gomm for all who cared to partake, and altogether a thoroughly enjoyable day was the result of the club's efforts.

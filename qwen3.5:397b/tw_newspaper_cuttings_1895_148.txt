LINCOLN GAZETTE: SATURDAY 29 JUNE 1895-2.

LINCOLN ST. ANDREW'S v. SOUTH PARK.—This match was played on the ground of the former on Saturday afternoon, in beautiful weather. The home team batted first, and ran up a score of 74. H. E. Reade and W. Reade were the principal scorers, the former making 16, when he was given out lbw, the ball hitting his left foot—a very doubtful decision. W. Reade made 21 by capital cricket, which included several boundary hits. Gardner was the most successful bowler, taking 6 wickets for 28 runs. On South Park going in to bat, the game became very slow, Weighell and Nicholson being the only batsmen to make a stand, the former especially playing a very patient game. The innings realised 70, South Park thus losing by four runs. Pearce again bowled splendidly, taking 7 wickets for 21 runs. Score :—

HYKEHAM v. LINCOLN "IMPS."—Played at Hykeham on Saturday last. The visitors won the toss, and through the batting of Turner, A. Johnson, and F. W. Shaw, jun., together with extras, the total of 49 was raised. A. M. Swift and Smythe commenced to bat to the deliveries of Turner and A. Johnson, Swift being caught in the long field at six. E. Hides and Smythe then gave a good display of free cricket, the score being raised to 41 before Smythe was bowled. Hides leaving at the same total. Dodgson and Watkins added 19 before the latter was caught. J. Hides and Dodgson then hit out, and the total was raised to 85 before Hides was caught at the wicket, Dodgson leaving two runs later for a well-played 18. Shepherd hit out freely for his 18 not out. RICHARDSON CHALLENGE CUP.
SECOND ROUND.
FRODINGHAM v. BRIGG.—On Saturday over 200 spectators assembled on the Frodingham ground to witness the above match. Our readers will remember that on the previous Saturday Frodingham had totalled 161 in the first innings, and Brigg had made 83 for the loss of five wickets, when time was called. The Committee of Management ordered the game to be played out on the Frodingham ground, and also instructed the umpires that if possible two innings should be completed, the time to be extended to half-past seven if necessary in order to finish the game. The match was resumed on Saturday, Brigg continuing their first innings, and such a good fight did they make that it was anybody's game right up to the fall of the last wicket, the total being 155 at the close of the innings, Flowers, who played splendidly, being top scorer with 39. On Frodingham going in a second time a sensational start was made, wickets falling rapidly to Barley's slows. Seven men were out for 25, but Childe, Crooke, and Sinclair made a better stand, and 82 was the total when the last wicket fell. Barley did the "hat trick," and took eight wickets for 27 runs. With 91 runs to get to win Brigg commenced their second innings a little before six o'clock, and in an hour had knocked off 82 for the loss of six wickets, when the game was stopped, notwithstanding the instructions given to the umpires that it was to be continued till half-past seven should there be any prospect of finishing the two innings. With nearly half-an-hour to play and only nine runs and four wickets to go down it was hard lines for Brigg. However, the matter will be brought before the Committee for them to decide what is now to be done. It was a grand game all through, and the pity is that it was spoiled by any unpleasantness just at the close. Score :—

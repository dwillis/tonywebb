BISCUIT FACTORY B XI v WHITE CROSS (BASINGSTOKE).—
On the Factory club ground on Saturday. HEATH END v. ST. LAURENCE'S (READING).—At Heath End on Saturday, the result of a highly enjoyable game being an easy win for Heath End by 85 runs and two wickets. The batting of Maxwell, White and W Hunt was exceedingly commendable, Maxwell's innings of 72 runs being marked with two mistakes only. The bowling of Maxwell and Roberts was superb, Roberts' analysis reading seven overs, four maidens, nine runs and five wickets; and Maxwell's thirteen, four maidens, fourteen runs and three wickets. After the match, through the hospitality of Mr Hunt, the players partook of tea on the lawn. At the conclusion of the meal the captain of the home club (Mr E J Hunt), presented Mr H Roberts with a cap in recognition of his recent performance of the hat trick. GUILDHALL v NEWTOWN.—On the Newbury ground on Saturday, and resulted in a win for the Guildhall by 75 runs on the first innings. Guildhall, winning the toss, sent Pope and Humphries to the wickets, but when the score was at 21, Humphries was bowled, and bowled, by Watson. No lengthy stand was then made until Edwards joined Pope; Edwards soon made 14. Drower was the only other man to reach double figures; and by free play he scored 22, the innings closing for 102. Newtown, on handling the yellow, made a most disastrous start, six wickets falling for half-a-dozen runs. Allsop was the best batsman, playing well for his 18 not out. The innings closed for 27, Newtown being 62 runs behind their confreres. Allsop and Williamson at their next venture played admirably, scoring between them 101 before being parted. Pope bowled well for G.C., doing the hat trick.

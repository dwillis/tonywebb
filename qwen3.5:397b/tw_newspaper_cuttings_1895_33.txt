HIGH WYCOMBE v. MR. E. STEVENS' XI.
On Saturday Mr. Edmund Stevens, of The Yache, Chalfont St. Giles, brought over a strong team to oppose the local club, but rain prevented the game from being played out. It was, however, drawn in favour of Wycombe, who had made 63 for the loss of two wickets, when stumps were drawn. No play was possible before luncheon, and a start was not made till past 3 o'clock, when the visitors went to the wicket. Before their innings was concluded, a smart shower compelled them to make a precipitate rush to the friendly shelter of the pavilion. The rain holding off for a time allowed the innings to be finished, 57 runs being the result. In Wycombe going in the light was very bad, and the rain came down at intervals in torrents, so that after several attempts to play out time it was thought best to leave the game drawn. At this point the match was left in the position shown by the following score and analysis. The wet did not hinder Bates from making some good strokes, his 29 including a 6, two 4's, three 3's, and two 2's.

BAYNERS XI v. PERMANENT STAFF OF THE 2nd BATT. OXFORD LIGHT INFANTRY.
On Monday last, August 5th, a match was played on Bayners private ground, between the two teams, and, after being hindered a little by the unsettled weather, ended in a decisive victory for the former by an innings and 11 runs. Mr. C. M. Rose captained the home eleven, and, having won the toss, elected to bat. The game was very slow at the commencement, and 4 good wickets were lost for 21, but when the captain and Mr. Powell became associated, the best stand of the day was made, and some good cricket was seen, both playing with confidence. They put on 63 runs between them, when Mr. Rose was caught at the wicket for an invaluable 57. Powell left soon after with a well-played 39 to his credit. Wheeler made 14, and the remaining batsmen being quickly disposed of, the innings closed for 114. An adjournment was now made for lunch, which had been prepared (in a spacious marquee, erected for the occasion) through the generosity of Sir Philip and Lady Rose, after which a very good wicket for ground. Powell had the best analysis, taking 6 wickets for 23, while Mr. Rose's 4 cost 25 runs. Having to follow on, they failed to reach the number required to save an innings' defeat, Dean taking 6 wickets for 13 runs, and the game resulted as before stated. Full score and analysis:-

MARLOW v. ORIEL.
Played at Marlow on Monday in very showery weather. The local club found it difficult to raise a side, and but for some help from Wycombe, would probably have had to play short. The team which took the field included H. H. Maple, who is staying in Marlow for a few days. Wycombe beat Marlow won the toss and batted. Meade, Langley, and T. Thurlow all compiled useful scores, but the stand of the innings was made by Bowes and Coleman, who carried the score from 112 for eight wickets to 175 for nine. Bowes hit a six, three 4's, and two 2's in his 52. The innings closed for 175. The visitors made an excellent start with Arnold and H. G. O. Owens. Bates came steadily from the bowling of Meade and Coleman, and 70 went up before Battle offered a separation by catching Owens off his own bowling. Afterwards the Oriel men did badly. Arnold was not out at 69, bowled by Coleman. His dashing innings of 54 included a 6, six 4's, 2 3's, etc. Langley took up the bowling with the total at 107 for four wickets and met with great success, securing five of the last six wickets at a cost of 13 runs. The innings closed for 136, and Marlow therefore won by 39 runs. Score and analysis:-

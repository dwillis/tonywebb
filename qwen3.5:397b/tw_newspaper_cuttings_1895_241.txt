LORD COBHAM'S XI. v. MRS. ISAAC'S XI. (JUNIOR 17).—This match was played on the County Ground at Houghton on August 14th, in very pleasant weather. Lord Cobham's boys batted first, and made a good start, the first five all getting into double figures, of which Seymour's 25 was the highest score. Ninety-five was reached when the fifth wicket fell, but the last five only added 18 runs. R. Foster taking four for 20 runs. The home eleven made an unfortunate start, losing two wickets for 6 runs. Thanks, however, to a good innings by F. Mackintosh, and some free hitting by Harkness and F. Isaac, the total amounted to 85. Lord Cobham's XI in their second innings made 77 for the loss of four wickets, of which C. Mackintosh scored an excellent 44 not out, including five 4's and five 3's. The innings being closed at this point, Mr. Isaac's boys had to bat for 75 minutes, with a balance of 85 runs to wipe off. Again they had a very poor start, as three wickets fell for a single run. J. Isaac and Harkness put, however, a difficult few runs together, and raised the score materially. Harkness, who left at 36, hit one 4 in his freely made 22. Isaac was still not out for 23 when time was called. The match was decided by the first innings, which gave victory to Lord Cobham's team by 18 runs. Scores:-

LORD COBHAM'S XI. v. MR. GIBBS' XI.—The annual boys' match at Hagley was played in Hagley Park, on Aug. 15. Although two of the players were 17 years and three more were 16, the great majority were of 15 and 14, while three were only 13. As by far the greater number of those playing had taken part in matches during the four preceding years, the game produced much interest. The day was fine, though dull; the wicket, in the earlier part of the day was somewhat difficult. J. Gibbs won the toss and put his men in first. They made a disastrous start, losing three wickets for six runs. P. Lyon and Jones-Williams improved matters by some steady play, but the whole side were out for 42, of which eight were extras. J. Lyttelton going on to bowl in the last part of the innings, took three wickets for one run. C. Mackintosh also did well, capturing four for 13. The opening of the innings of Lord Cobham's XI. was no better than that of their opponents, four men were out for nine runs. Harkness and F. Mackintosh improved matters considerably, but they were treated kindly by some of the fielders, since five catches (not all easy ones) were dropped in a very short space of time. When Harkness left at 23, the end was not long in coming, and the total fell short by ten runs of that of their opponents. P. Lyon, whilst bowling and fielding were alike admirable, took five wickets for 10 runs, and made four catches. In their second innings the visitors made a much better show. Jones Williams and W. H. Gibbs put on 28 runs for the second wicket, and C. A. Lyon, aided by his brother, added 31 for the match. The former made the highest score of the match, a freely hit 33. The other batsmen all did something, and, with the aid of 14 extras, 96 was reached when the 10th wicket fell. Of the bowlers in this innings, N. Talbot came out best, taking in 18-3 overs four wickets for 16 runs. Lord Cobham's team were left with 99 to make in about an hour and a half. An excellent catch by P. Lyon off his own bowling took the first wicket for 0. Matters improved somewhat, and Harkness again did much service to his side by vigorous hitting. Seymour and F. Mackintosh also got double figures, and their score reached 65 for seven wickets when barely 10 minutes remained for play. Two men, however, failed to score, and, as the last man was absent, the innings was brought to a close just before time for 70, leaving the other side victorious, after a very interesting match, by 28 runs. Apart from the errors mentioned above, the all-round fielding was good on both sides. Score:-

ST. JOHN'S 2ND XI. v. MALVERN 2ND XI.—The return match between these clubs was played at Malvern on Thursday, and resulted in a win for St. John's by 11 runs. St. John's lost seven wickets for 21 runs, and then the last three put on 22. Mr. L. James, who was the only one to reach double figures, hit well for his total. Messrs. Park, Isaac, and Curtis also batted well. For Malvern, Messrs. W. H. Hartland and Romer were top scorers; but, owing to some smart catches being taken, the total only reached 32. The bowling of Messrs. Bannister and Robinson for St. John's and Messrs. Clegg and Jenkins for Malvern was very good. Score:-

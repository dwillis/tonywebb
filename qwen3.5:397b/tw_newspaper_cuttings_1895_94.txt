SOUTH WALES DAILY NEWS: MONDAY 5 AUGUST 1895-2.

GENTLEMEN OF MONMOUTHSHIRE v. R. M. L.
MILITIA.
This two-day match, which was played on Lord
Tredegar's Park, was concluded on Saturday, and
owing chiefly to the bowling of Porter, and the fine
batting displays by G. F. Harding and H. Breham,
ended right on time in a victory for the Gentlemen by
five wickets and 75 runs. Scores :—

CARDIFF v. BRISTOL SCHOOLMASTERS
On Monday next the Bristol Schoolmasters will meet
a fairly strong Cardiff eleven on the Cardiff Arms
Park. Wickets pitched at 11.30 sharp. The Cardiff
team is as follows, Lowe accompanying the 2nd on
their tour to Warminster, Frome, and Wells, while
J. H. Brain will play for Fairwater :—J. G. Clarke
(capt.), T. M. Barlow, Selwyn Biggs, N. Sweet-Escott,
V. A. Hickley, W. W. F. Pullen, A. H. Mann, E. W.
Hodge, C. C. McCall, J. E. McKay, and E. H. Schiele.

WINDSOR v. GRANGE CONSERVATIVES.—Played at
Grangetown, resulting in a win for the Windsors.
Score :—Windsors, 50 (for seven wickets); Grange
Conservatives, 23.

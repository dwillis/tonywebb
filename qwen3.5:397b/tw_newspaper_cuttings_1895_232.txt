BERROW'S WORCESTER JOURNAL: SATURDAY 27 JULY 1895-3.

WORCESTERSHIRE CLUB AND GROUND v.
HALESOWEN.
At Worcester. Although Club and Ground had much the best of matters, time nearly saved the visitors, their last wicket going down to the last ball of the match. E. G. Hobson and E. G. M. Carmichael played good cricket for their respective totals, and Burrows and J. Lightwood bowled well.

WICKENFORD v. ST. JOHN'S 2ND XI.—This return match was played at Wichenford on July 18, and resulted in a win for Wichenford by 10 runs. St. John's batted first, the bowling of Messrs. Hobson and Williams proving a great deal too effective for the opponents. Wichenford found a good point in the water bailiff. Messrs. Buckland, Hobson, and Jones Williams batted well for Wichenford; and Messrs. H. Appleyard, Parke, and Williams batted well for St. John's. Score:-

KIDDERMINSTER GRAMMAR SCHOOL v. WORCESTER GRAMMAR SCHOOL.—Played at Worcester, on July 22. The bowling of the visitors was very deadly, and four of the home team's wickets fell for no runs. Shortly afterwards Woodward took three wickets with three successive balls. Score:-
